## Role Summary

Jordan owns the company's entire AI literacy vertical — from
foundational "what is AI and why does it matter" units to advanced
applied-AI coursework for students pursuing tech careers. They are the
person who ensures every AI-related product decision is grounded in
how students actually learn, what teachers actually need to feel
confident teaching this material, and what industry actually means
when it says it wants "AI-ready graduates."

## Why They're the Right Hire

1. **They've taught AI to students who had never touched a
   terminal.** Jordan built and taught one of the first high school AI
   literacy electives in Georgia — before AI literacy was a buzzword.
   They know what works in a real classroom with real students.
2. **They understand the teacher-confidence problem.** Most CTE
   teachers feel underprepared to teach AI. Jordan has spent years
   coaching non-CS teachers on how to facilitate AI concepts without
   a computer science degree. Their curriculum is designed around
   teacher confidence, not just student outcomes.
3. **They think in pathways, not lessons.** Jordan doesn't write
   standalone AI activities — they architect sequences: from AI
   awareness → AI application → AI creation. They know where a student
   needs to be in week one to be credential-ready in semester two.
4. **They've watched the hype cycle from inside education.** Jordan
   has seen too many "AI in the classroom" initiatives collapse because
   the curriculum was vendor-sponsored fluff. They bring deep
   skepticism toward edtech marketing and a sharp eye for substance.
5. **They're a practitioner, not just an instructor.** Jordan builds
   with AI tools, runs experiments, and stays current. They won't teach
   a tool that was obsolete six months ago.

## Responsibilities (First 90 Days)

1. **Audit the AI landscape in CTE.** Map every meaningful AI
   literacy standard, framework, and credential (CSTA, AI4K12, Digital
   Promise, CompTIA, AWS, Google, Microsoft) against what CTE teachers
   are actually being asked to teach. Identify the gaps and
   opportunities.
2. **Define the AI literacy pathway architecture.** Determine the
   progression: units, competencies, and credential-prep hooks from
   introductory to advanced. Get Maya's sign-off and Sergio's gut
   check.
3. **Run teacher discovery on the AI vertical.** Conduct 20+
   interviews with CTE teachers currently tasked with teaching AI or
   trying to avoid it. Understand the confidence gap, the resource
   gap, and the "what would actually help me" answer.
4. **Ship the first AI literacy unit.** One complete, classroom-ready
   unit — not a sample, not a demo. Something a real teacher can run
   next semester.
5. **Identify the cornerstone industry credential.** Recommend the
   primary certification students should be prepping for (Google AI
   Essentials, CompTIA AI+, AWS AI Practitioner, or equivalent) and
   build the alignment framework.
