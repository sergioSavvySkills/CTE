## Role Summary

Daniel builds the software that makes Maya 10x and the product
teachers and students actually use. He owns the platform end-to-end:
the AI-assisted authoring pipeline, the learner experience, the
teacher tools, and the unglamorous district-readiness plumbing.

He is explicitly **not** writing courses. Maya writes courses. Daniel
builds the system that lets Maya ship curriculum at 10–50x the speed
of a legacy publisher committee — and the product that delivers it.

## Why He's the Right Hire

1. **Full-stack generalist who actually ships.** Comfortable going
   from Postgres schema to React component to deploy pipeline in a
   single afternoon. No specialist hand-offs at a team of 3.
2. **AI-native, not AI-curious.** Has built real LLM pipelines with
   evaluations, guardrails, and cost controls — not just called an
   API in a side project.
3. **Education wasn't an accident.** Shipped teacher-facing features
   at an edtech company early in his career and has stayed close to
   the space.
4. **Startup-shaped.** Already been employee #4 at one startup. Knows
   what the first 18 months of a pre-revenue company actually feels
   like and chose to do it again.
5. **Respects the domain.** Takes Maya's pedagogical calls seriously.
   Will not try to "just use the LLM" in places where it would
   produce plausible-sounding wrong answers in front of students.

## Responsibilities (First 90 Days)

1. **Stand up the platform spine.** Auth, data model, deploy pipeline,
   basic multi-tenancy. Nothing fancy — the minimum that lets the
   team stop rebuilding scaffolding every week.
2. **Ship v0 of the authoring tool with Maya.** The thing Maya uses to
   produce the first unit in the wedge pathway. AI-assisted drafting,
   standards alignment, assessment generation — scoped to what the
   first unit actually needs, no more.
3. **Ship v0 of the learner experience.** What a student opens in the
   first pilot classroom. Interactive, not a PDF.
4. **Ship v0 of the teacher view.** Assign, review, customize. The
   3–5 things a teacher actually does every week.
5. **Put v0 in front of real teachers.** Target: at least one real CTE
   teacher running a real unit with real students by day 90.
