## Learning objective

Students will be able to describe the economic scale of the health services sector and analyze how local, state, and federal governments shape the health science industry.

## Content stub

- **Scale of the sector:** healthcare is roughly 17-18% of U.S. GDP and employs 20+ million U.S. workers per the current BLS *Occupational Outlook Handbook* — one of the largest sectors in most states and counties; generates local jobs and tax revenue
- **Cost drivers and levers:** technology, drug prices, administration, chronic disease, aging; cost-containment via Medicare payment reform, bundled payments, value-based care
- **Federal role:** CMS (Medicare, Medicaid), FDA (drugs, devices), CDC (public health), NIH (research funding), OSHA (workplace safety), HRSA (safety-net providers)
- **State role:** license health professionals and facilities, administer Medicaid jointly with CMS, set public-health laws (e.g., vaccination, reporting), operate state hospitals
- **Local role:** county health departments, public hospitals, EMS systems, school health, inspection and food safety
- **How government shapes practice:** coverage rules set what care gets paid for; licensure sets who can deliver it; regulation sets how it's delivered safely; funding shapes what research happens

## Assessment stub

- Level-match: given 10 policies/actions (Medicare rule, state licensure law, county restaurant inspection, FDA drug approval), identify federal, state, or local
- Analysis: pick one public-policy change (e.g., Medicaid expansion, HPV vaccine requirement, Part D) and explain its effect on patients, providers, and the workforce
- Local inquiry: research your county or city health department's current top 3 priorities and explain how each affects residents
