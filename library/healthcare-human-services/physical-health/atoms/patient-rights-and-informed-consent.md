## Learning objective

Students will be able to describe the core rights patients hold in clinical settings and explain the elements that make consent legally and ethically valid.

## Content stub

- **Patient Bill of Rights:** dignity, privacy, information about care, participation in decisions, refusal of treatment, continuity, grievance process
- **Elements of informed consent:** capacity, disclosure (risks/benefits/alternatives), understanding, voluntariness, documentation
- **Implied vs. expressed vs. written consent:** when each applies; emergency treatment as implied consent
- **Special situations:** minors, incapacitated adults, surrogate decision-makers, advance directives, POLST/MOLST, emergency exceptions
- **Right to refuse:** including against medical advice (AMA); documentation of refusal; exceptions for public-health threats or incapacity
- **Common violations:** forms signed without explanation, consent obtained under sedation, language barriers without interpreter, coerced decisions

## Assessment stub

- Consent audit: given 5 scenarios, identify which elements of informed consent are missing and what must happen before the procedure
- Classification: sort 10 situations into implied, expressed, or written consent
- Role-play: explain a simple procedure to a "patient" peer and obtain proper consent; observer checks for all five elements
