## Learning objective

Students will be able to evaluate how relationships with peers, family, and community affect physical and emotional health and apply communication skills that support healthy relationships across the lifespan.

## Content stub

- **Relationships as a health factor:** strong social ties correlate with longer life, lower cardiovascular risk, better mental health; isolation correlates with mortality comparable to smoking
- **Positive patterns:** respect, trust, honest communication, reciprocity, appropriate boundaries, conflict handled constructively
- **Negative patterns:** control, isolation, contempt, stonewalling, substance-enabling, abuse (physical, emotional, financial) — screen for and report where mandated
- **Communication skills for maintenance:** active listening, I-statements, clear boundary-setting, repair after conflict, seeking help early
- **Lifespan variation:** peer relationships dominate adolescence; romantic/partner in young adulthood; family caregiving in middle age; loss and grief in older adulthood
- **Building community:** social groups, civic participation, spiritual/religious community, peer support — modifiable factors that improve health outcomes

## Assessment stub

- Classify: given 10 relationship behaviors, sort as supporting, neutral, or harming wellbeing
- Scenario evaluation: read three relationship vignettes and identify specific supportive and harmful patterns, plus at least one intervention
- Reflection: describe one relationship in your life that has supported your wellbeing and one that has not; name the specific patterns that made the difference
