## Learning objective

Students will be able to apply structured teamwork tools — SBAR, closed-loop communication, and TeamSTEPPS concepts — to clinical handoffs and shared decisions.

## Content stub

- **SBAR:** Situation, Background, Assessment, Recommendation — the default handoff and phone-call-a-provider format; example scripts
- **Closed-loop communication:** sender gives message, receiver reads back, sender confirms — especially for critical values, medication orders, and code events
- **TeamSTEPPS essentials:** team leadership, situation monitoring (shared mental model), mutual support, communication — why systems beat individual heroism
- **CUS and two-challenge rule:** "I'm Concerned, I'm Uncomfortable, this is a Safety issue"; if first concern is ignored, raise it again — authority gradient problem
- **Leadership at every level:** brief before the shift, huddle when something shifts, debrief after events; lead yourself first, then the team
- **Psychological safety:** the team's ability to raise concerns without fear — linked directly to patient outcomes; what leaders do to build it

## Assessment stub

- SBAR drill: write and deliver a phone-call-the-physician SBAR for a specified change in patient status; peer scores clarity and completeness
- Role-play: simulate a closed-loop exchange for a medication order; observer identifies where the loop closes or breaks
- Reflection: describe a team you've been on (sports, work, school) — identify what leadership behaviors built or eroded psychological safety and how that affected outcomes
