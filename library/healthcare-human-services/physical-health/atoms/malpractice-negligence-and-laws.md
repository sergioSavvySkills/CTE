## Learning objective

Students will be able to distinguish negligence and malpractice, describe the elements of each, and research the main bodies of law that govern the health science industry.

## Content stub

- **Negligence:** failure to act with the care a reasonable person would use — any person can be negligent
- **Malpractice:** professional negligence — a licensed practitioner fails to meet the standard of care; requires a professional duty
- **Four elements required:** duty of care, breach of that duty, causation (breach caused harm), damages (measurable harm) — missing any element, no malpractice claim
- **Liability types:** personal, vicarious (employer for employee), institutional (facility); malpractice insurance addresses personal and vicarious
- **Major bodies of healthcare law:** federal — HIPAA, EMTALA, Stark/Anti-Kickback, ACA, FDA Act, CLIA; state — practice acts, reporting laws, tort reform, informed-consent statutes
- **Common malpractice scenarios:** diagnostic errors, medication errors, surgical errors, failure to monitor, birth injuries; documentation is frequently the deciding factor in court

## Assessment stub

- Classify: given 10 workplace incidents, identify which are negligence, malpractice, neither, or insufficient info
- Four-elements analysis: given 5 scenarios, walk through each of duty / breach / causation / damages and conclude whether a malpractice claim would stand
- Law mapping: pick one federal and one state healthcare law; identify the agency enforcing it and one concrete rule it imposes on daily practice
