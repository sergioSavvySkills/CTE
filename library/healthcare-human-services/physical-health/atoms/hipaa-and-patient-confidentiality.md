## Learning objective

Students will be able to explain what HIPAA protects, identify protected health information (PHI), and recognize actions that violate patient confidentiality.

## Content stub

- **What HIPAA is:** federal law (1996) with three main rules: Privacy, Security, Breach Notification — who must comply and why
- **Covered entities and business associates:** hospitals, providers, health plans, clearinghouses — plus any vendor handling PHI on their behalf
- **What counts as PHI:** the 18 identifiers — name, DOB, MRN, photos, address, phone, email, biometrics, any health info tied to an individual
- **Minimum necessary rule:** share only what's needed for the task; looking up a celebrity's chart "just to see" is a violation even if you tell no one
- **Common violations in practice:** discussing patients in elevators or on social media, leaving charts visible, sharing passwords, texting PHI on personal phones
- **Consequences:** fines (from $100 to $1.5M), termination, criminal charges; patient harm (identity theft, stigma, loss of trust)

## Assessment stub

- Classify: for 15 items (name, shoe size, MRN, license plate, diagnosis), mark PHI or not PHI
- Scenario judgment: 8 workplace situations — identify which are violations and which are allowed
- Short answer: a coworker asks you to pull up their neighbor's chart "because she's worried." Explain exactly what you say and why
