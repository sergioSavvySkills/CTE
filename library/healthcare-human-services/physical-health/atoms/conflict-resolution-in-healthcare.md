## Learning objective

Students will be able to evaluate common conflict-resolution techniques and apply them to realistic healthcare scenarios involving patients, families, or coworkers.

## Content stub

- **Conflict styles:** avoid, accommodate, compete, compromise, collaborate (Thomas-Kilmann) — each has a right moment; collaboration is the default in clinical teams
- **Core skills:** calm tone, active listening, separating person from problem, validating feelings before problem-solving, using "I" statements, reframing
- **De-escalation for upset patients/families:** acknowledge, apologize where appropriate (AHRQ CANDOR model), explain, offer next step; avoid defensiveness
- **Team conflict:** speak up using CUS or the two-challenge rule; escalate through the chain of command; document objectively
- **When to get help:** threats of violence, impairment, scope violations, patterns of disrespectful behavior — supervisor, risk management, ethics committee, security
- **Pitfalls:** winning at all costs, gossiping with uninvolved coworkers, texting about the conflict, apologizing for things you shouldn't admit legally

## Assessment stub

- Match: given 6 scenarios, select the most appropriate conflict style and explain why
- Role-play: de-escalate an upset family member in a simulated scenario; observer scores on acknowledge/apologize/explain/next-step
- Short case: describe a team conflict you witnessed (or can imagine) and walk through exactly how CUS or two-challenge would apply
