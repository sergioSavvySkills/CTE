## Learning objective

Students will be able to apply the four core ethical principles — autonomy, beneficence, non-maleficence, and justice — to common dilemmas in healthcare practice.

## Content stub

- **Autonomy:** the patient's right to make their own decisions, even unwise ones; capacity vs. competence; informed refusal
- **Beneficence:** the duty to act in the patient's best interest; balancing benefit against cost, effort, and preference
- **Non-maleficence:** "first, do no harm" — risks of action vs. inaction; iatrogenic harm as a real category
- **Justice:** fair allocation of limited resources; access, bias, and social determinants as justice issues
- **Common dilemmas:** refusing treatment, end-of-life choices, mandatory reporting, gifts from patients, resource scarcity, honesty about errors
- **Resources for reasoning:** professional codes (ANA, AMA), institutional ethics committees, state nurse/medical practice acts

## Assessment stub

- Dilemma analysis: for 4 short cases, identify which principles conflict and propose a defensible decision
- Code comparison: compare one principle across the ANA Code of Ethics and the AMA Principles — spot differences
- Reflection essay: a patient refuses life-saving treatment. Explain how each of the four principles applies and which should win, and why
