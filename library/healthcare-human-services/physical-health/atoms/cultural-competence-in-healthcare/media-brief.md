## Accessibility requirements

- Alt text for each asset must name the concept depicted, not the
  people in the image (e.g., "two-column contrast of cultural
  competence and cultural humility" rather than "woman and man
  talking")
- Role-play video (m04) requires open captions, an audio-description
  track, and a transcript — the interpreter modality is part of the
  content, not background
- Color choices for m01, m02, and m05 must not rely on color alone
  to convey meaning; use line weight, label position, or hatching so
  the distinction survives grayscale printing and common color-vision
  differences
- All diagrams use text labels at minimum 14 pt with high-contrast
  backgrounds
- Avoid imagery that codes "culture" with stereotypical dress,
  symbols, or settings; use everyday clinical scenes and let the
  concept do the work

## Reuse notes

- **m01** (competence vs humility contrast) reuses in any atom
  covering professional reflection, ethics, or patient-centered care
- **m02** (social determinants schematic) reuses in any public-health
  or population-health atom; keep it generic so the ordering claim
  remains true without specific numbers
- **m03** (interpreter decision flow) reuses in any atom that
  includes an intake or consent step with language access
- **m04** (interpreter role-play) reuses across clinical-skills atoms
  that include a patient-interview component
- **m06** (vocab icon set) reuses wherever this atom's anchor terms
  are referenced, preventing duplicate iconography
