## Learning objective

Students will be able to decode and build medical terms by combining word roots, prefixes, and suffixes, and correctly interpret unfamiliar terms from their parts.

## Content stub

- **Word structure:** root carries the meaning (cardi-, nephr-, hepat-); combining vowel (usually -o-) joins parts; suffix modifies or adds action/condition; prefix modifies the front
- **Common roots by body system:** cardio (heart), hemato (blood), pulmo/pneumo (lung), nephro/reno (kidney), gastro (stomach), osteo (bone), derm (skin), neuro (nerve), myo (muscle)
- **Common suffixes:** -itis (inflammation), -ectomy (removal), -ostomy (opening), -oscopy (visual exam), -algia (pain), -emia (blood condition), -logy (study of)
- **Common prefixes:** hypo/hyper (low/high), brady/tachy (slow/fast), peri (around), sub (below), inter (between), poly (many), dys (abnormal)
- **Decoding strategy:** suffix first → prefix next → root last — example: pericarditis = peri (around) + cardi (heart) + itis (inflammation) = inflammation around the heart
- **Pitfalls:** two roots that look alike (ileum vs. ilium), silent letters, and abbreviations that look like word parts but aren't

## Assessment stub

- Decode exercise: given 15 unfamiliar terms, break each into prefix/root/suffix and state the meaning
- Build exercise: given a definition, construct the term (e.g., "inflammation of the kidney" → nephritis)
- Clinical scenario: read a short discharge note with 8 terms and explain what happened to the patient in plain language
