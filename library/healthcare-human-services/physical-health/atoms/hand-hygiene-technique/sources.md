# Sources — hand-hygiene-technique

## Standards and guidelines

### WHO-HandHygiene

- **Citation:** World Health Organization. *WHO Guidelines on Hand Hygiene in Health Care* (2009).
- **URL:** https://www.who.int/publications/i/item/9789241597906
- **Used for:** canonical technique steps for soap-and-water and alcohol-based hand rub, 20-second timing, hand-surface coverage, evidence that hand hygiene is the single most effective prevention measure against healthcare-associated infections
- **Confidence:** high — international health authority, peer-reviewed guideline
- **Access date:** 2026-04-21

### WHO-FiveMoments

- **Citation:** World Health Organization. *My 5 Moments for Hand Hygiene* poster and user guide (part of SAVE LIVES: Clean Your Hands campaign).
- **URL:** https://www.who.int/campaigns/world-hand-hygiene-day
- **Used for:** the five indications (before patient contact, before aseptic task, after body-fluid exposure risk, after patient contact, after contact with patient surroundings) and their placement in a care sequence
- **Confidence:** high — WHO patient-safety program reference
- **Access date:** 2026-04-21

### TJC-NPSG-07

- **Citation:** The Joint Commission. *National Patient Safety Goal 07.01.01 — Comply with current WHO or CDC hand hygiene guidelines*.
- **URL:** https://www.jointcommission.org/standards/national-patient-safety-goals/
- **Used for:** U.S. accreditation requirement that hospitals follow WHO or CDC hand-hygiene guidelines and measure staff compliance
- **Confidence:** high — U.S. hospital accrediting body

## Government / regulatory pages

### CDC-HandHygiene

- **Citation:** Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. *Hand Hygiene in Healthcare Settings*.
- **URL:** https://www.cdc.gov/handhygiene/
- **Used for:** U.S. technique recommendations, volume and drying time for alcohol-based hand rub, jewelry and artificial-nail guidance, summary of when soap-and-water is required over ABHR
- **Confidence:** high — U.S. federal public-health agency

### CDC-CDiff

- **Citation:** Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. *Clostridioides difficile Infection (CDI) — Prevention and Control*.
- **URL:** https://www.cdc.gov/cdiff/
- **Used for:** explanation that *C. difficile* spores are not killed by alcohol-based hand rub, requirement for soap-and-water hand wash after caring for CDI patients, analogous guidance for norovirus
- **Confidence:** high — U.S. federal public-health agency
