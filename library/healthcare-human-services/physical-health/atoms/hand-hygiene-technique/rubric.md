# Performance rubric — hand-hygiene-technique

Use this rubric to score the skill check. The student demonstrates
both techniques (ABHR and soap-and-water) on a peer-observer, then
performs a 10-minute simulated care sequence in which an observer
counts Five-Moments indications and hand-hygiene events.

A **Competent** rating across every row is the minimum passing
standard. Any **Novice** score on Technique correctness, Technique
selection, or Five Moments requires re-demonstration.

## Criteria

### Technique correctness

- **Novice** — Skips one or more required steps (e.g., does not wet
  before soap, does not use paper towel to turn off tap, wipes ABHR
  off before dry). Obvious procedural errors.
- **Competent** — Performs every step of both techniques in order:
  wet-lather-scrub-rinse-dry-towel-off-tap for soap-and-water;
  sufficient volume-rub-until-dry for ABHR. No step skipped.
- **Proficient** — Performs every step smoothly and coaches a peer
  through errors without prompting. Names the "why" behind each step.

### Timing (20-second minimum)

- **Novice** — Finishes in under 15 seconds on either technique, or
  stops ABHR before hands are fully dry.
- **Competent** — Meets or exceeds 20 seconds on both techniques.
  Lets ABHR air-dry fully without wiping.
- **Proficient** — Consistently lands at 20–25 seconds without
  watching a timer, using an internalized cue (song, count, or
  habit).

### Surface coverage (all hand surfaces)

- **Novice** — Visibly misses two or more of: palms, backs of hands,
  interlaced fingers, thumbs, fingertips/nails, wrists.
- **Competent** — Covers all six surface groups on both hands for
  both techniques. No visibly dry spots on an ABHR dye test.
- **Proficient** — Covers all surfaces in a consistent, repeatable
  sequence that an observer can follow, including the commonly
  missed thumbs and fingertips.

### Technique selection (ABHR vs. soap-and-water)

- **Novice** — Chooses ABHR when soap-and-water is required (visible
  soil, *C. diff* or norovirus exposure, post-restroom), or vice
  versa. Cannot state the rule.
- **Competent** — Chooses the correct technique in every prompted
  scenario. States the rule: ABHR by default; soap-and-water when
  hands are visibly soiled, after *C. diff* or norovirus contact, or
  after restroom use.
- **Proficient** — Selects correctly and explains the mechanism
  (alcohol does not kill spores; visible soil needs mechanical
  removal) without prompting.

### Five Moments identification (observed sequence)

- **Novice** — Misses more than two indicated Moments during the
  10-minute simulation, or cannot name the Five Moments when asked.
  Treats hand hygiene as a room-entry/exit rule.
- **Competent** — Performs hand hygiene at every indicated Moment
  with no more than one miss, and correctly names all five Moments
  (before patient contact, before aseptic task, after body-fluid
  exposure risk, after patient contact, after contact with patient
  surroundings).
- **Proficient** — Hits every indicated Moment including the
  often-missed Moment 2 (before aseptic task) and Moment 5 (after
  contact with patient surroundings), and verbalizes each Moment
  aloud as they perform it.
