## Learning objective

Students will be able to identify common technological equipment across the five health science systems, recognize signs of malfunction, and describe the process for reporting equipment problems.

## Content stub

- **Diagnostic equipment:** imaging (X-ray, CT, MRI, ultrasound), lab analyzers, EKG, spirometers, point-of-care glucose and pregnancy tests
- **Therapeutic equipment:** IV pumps, ventilators, defibrillators (AED and manual), beds, dialysis machines, radiation-therapy units
- **Health informatics equipment:** EHR workstations, barcode scanners, telemetry monitors, patient portals, HIE infrastructure
- **Support-services equipment:** sterilizers, transport carts, dietary carts, laundry, HVAC with HEPA filtration, fire and call-light systems
- **Biotech research and development equipment:** centrifuges, PCR thermocyclers, flow cytometers, microscopes, biosafety cabinets
- **Signs of malfunction and reporting:** alarms, error codes, abnormal readings, physical damage, lockout tags; when in doubt take out of service, label "Do Not Use," and file a work order. Device-related patient harm is reported to biomed, risk management, and — for FDA-regulated devices — through the MDR process

## Assessment stub

- Match: given 15 pieces of equipment and 5 healthcare systems, assign each to its primary system
- Malfunction spot: given 5 scenarios (alarm, wrong reading, damaged cord, unresponsive display, fluid leak), identify the safety action and who to notify
- Process diagram: sketch the reporting flow for a suspected device-related patient injury, from bedside discovery to FDA MDR filing
