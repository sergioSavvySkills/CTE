# Media brief — body-systems-overview

A 45-minute lesson on eleven systems needs visual anchors — one per system
at minimum, plus diagrams for the cross-cutting concepts (levels of
organization, feedback loops, system interactions). Below is the full asset
list the lesson generator should source, create, or select.

## Visuals needed

| id | type | purpose | description | priority | status | source |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| m01 | diagram | hook | Labeled silhouette of the human body with all 11 organ systems shown as color-coded overlays. Systems labeled at the edge, not on the body. Legible at 10-foot viewing distance. | high | needed | create (or adapt OpenStax Fig 1.3) |

## Accessibility requirements

- Every diagram carries alt text naming the system(s) shown and the key
  structures labeled
- Color-coding must pair with shape or label differences (color-blind safe);
  do not rely on color alone to distinguish systems in m01
- Videos (m09) need closed captions even for the no-audio version — describe
  the visible action
- Labels minimum 14 pt in the rendered slide
- Do NOT use red-green as the only distinguishing color pair anywhere

## Reuse notes

- **m01** (labeled body silhouette) will reuse in almost every other HHS-PH
  atom — it is the anchor image for the whole sub-cluster. Worth investing
  in a clean original rather than licensing a stock photo.
- **m02** (levels of organization) will reuse in *body-organization-and-
  directional-terms* and any early anatomy course.
- **m03** (generic feedback-loop schematic) is a template that every
  homeostasis-related atom (thermoregulation, glucose, blood pressure, pH
  balance, fluid balance) will reuse with different labels.
- **m06** (skin cross-section) will reuse in integumentary-focused atoms
  once those exist.
- **m07** (nephron) will reuse in any urinary-system atom.
- **m08** (alveolus) will reuse in any respiratory-system atom.

Budget roughly half the original-art effort into m01-m05; the remaining
six assets can be licensed or lightly adapted from OpenStax, which is
CC BY 4.0 and requires only attribution.
