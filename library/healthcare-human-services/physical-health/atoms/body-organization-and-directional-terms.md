## Learning objective

Students will be able to describe levels of body organization, identify anatomical planes and cavities, and use directional terms to locate structures on a body.

## Content stub

- **Levels of organization:** chemical → cellular → tissue → organ → organ system → organism; example trace through one system
- **Anatomical position:** standing, facing forward, palms out — the reference for all directional language
- **Planes of the body:** sagittal, midsagittal, frontal/coronal, transverse — what each plane reveals on imaging
- **Body cavities:** dorsal (cranial, spinal), ventral (thoracic, abdominopelvic); membranes (pleural, pericardial, peritoneal)
- **Directional pairs:** superior/inferior, anterior/posterior, medial/lateral, proximal/distal, superficial/deep, ipsilateral/contralateral
- **Abdominal regions and quadrants:** RUQ/LUQ/RLQ/LLQ plus the nine-region map; which organs live where

## Assessment stub

- Label: on a blank anatomy diagram, mark the four quadrants, the planes, and the cavities with correct terms
- Describe: given imaging studies (CT vs. MRI cuts), identify which plane each shows and what structures are visible
- Clinical scenario: a patient has pain in the RLQ — list two organs commonly found there and describe the location using proper directional terms
