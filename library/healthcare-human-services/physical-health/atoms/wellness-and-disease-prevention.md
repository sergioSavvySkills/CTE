## Learning objective

Students will be able to distinguish primary, secondary, and tertiary prevention, and identify evidence-based interventions at each level.

## Content stub

- **Primary prevention:** stop disease before it starts — vaccines, seatbelts, clean water, smoking cessation, safe-sex education, sunscreen, exercise
- **Secondary prevention:** catch disease early when treatment works best — screenings (mammogram, colonoscopy, HIV test, BP check), contact tracing, newborn screens
- **Tertiary prevention:** limit damage once disease is established — cardiac rehab, insulin management, stroke rehab, chronic pain management
- **Dimensions of wellness:** physical, mental/emotional, social, intellectual, occupational, spiritual, environmental, financial — how they interact
- **Evidence hierarchies:** USPSTF grades (A–D, I), CDC guidelines, WHO recommendations; why "everyone should get X" isn't always true
- **Population vs. individual:** a screen that saves lives at the population level still has false positives and harms for individuals; counseling handles both

## Assessment stub

- Classify: sort 20 interventions into primary, secondary, or tertiary prevention
- Research a screen: pick one USPSTF-graded screen, explain the grade, who gets it, and what harms/benefits it produces
- Plan: design a one-week personal wellness plan touching at least five dimensions, with specific measurable actions
