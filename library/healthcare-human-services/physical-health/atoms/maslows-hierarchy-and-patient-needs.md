## Learning objective

Students will be able to identify the five levels of Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs and apply the framework to prioritize patient care situations.

## Content stub

- **Physiological needs:** air, water, food, elimination, sleep, shelter, temperature — the base; if unmet, nothing else matters
- **Safety and security:** physical safety (fall prevention, infection control, violence-free environment), emotional safety, financial security
- **Love and belonging:** relationships, family presence, therapeutic rapport, community; isolation harms outcomes
- **Self-esteem:** autonomy, respect, dignity, recognition — easily eroded in hospital gowns and shared rooms
- **Self-actualization:** realizing potential, meaning, purpose; addressed in palliative care, rehab, mental health
- **Prioritizing in practice:** ABCs first (airway/breathing/circulation = physiological), then safety, then the higher levels; don't discuss lifestyle goals with a hypoxic patient

## Assessment stub

- Label: given a diagram of Maslow's pyramid with 10 needs listed around it, place each at the correct level
- Prioritize: given 5 simultaneous patient concerns, rank-order them using Maslow and defend the sequence
- Reflection: identify a time a hospitalization (yours or a family member's) felt undignified or isolating; describe which Maslow level was unmet and one concrete change that would help
