# Sources — conflict-resolution-in-healthcare

## Standards and guidelines

### AHRQ-CANDOR

- **Citation:** Agency for Healthcare Research and Quality. *Communication and Optimal Resolution (CANDOR) Toolkit*.
- **URL:** https://www.ahrq.gov/patient-safety/settings/hospital/candor/modules.html
- **Used for:** the acknowledge-apologize-explain-next-step pattern for de-escalating upset patients and families, disclosure framing
- **Confidence:** high — U.S. federal health-services research agency

### TeamSTEPPS

- **Citation:** Agency for Healthcare Research and Quality. *TeamSTEPPS: Team Strategies and Tools to Enhance Performance and Patient Safety*.
- **URL:** https://www.ahrq.gov/teamstepps-program/
- **Used for:** CUS (Concerned, Uncomfortable, Safety issue) script, two-challenge rule, structured team-conflict escalation
- **Confidence:** high — AHRQ national teamwork curriculum

### TJC-Workplace

- **Citation:** The Joint Commission. *Sentinel Event Alert 59: Physical and Verbal Violence Against Health Care Workers*.
- **URL:** https://www.jointcommission.org/resources/sentinel-event/sentinel-event-alert-newsletters/sentinel-event-alert-59-physical-and-verbal-violence-against-health-care-workers/
- **Used for:** when to escalate beyond direct resolution, disruptive-behavior response expectations
- **Confidence:** high — national hospital accreditation body

### ANA-Bullying

- **Citation:** American Nurses Association. *Position Statement: Incivility, Bullying, and Workplace Violence*.
- **URL:** https://www.nursingworld.org/practice-policy/nursing-excellence/official-position-statements/id/incivility-bullying-and-workplace-violence/
- **Used for:** distinguishing ordinary conflict from a pattern of disrespectful behavior, reporting expectations
- **Confidence:** high — national professional nursing body
