## Learning objective

Students will be able to interpret technical healthcare material, write clear reports and summaries, and plan effective oral presentations using precise language.

## Content stub

- **Interpret technical material:** journal abstracts, drug package inserts, clinical guidelines, discharge summaries — spot purpose, findings, limitations
- **Reports and summaries:** audience-first writing; headline → background → findings → recommendations; one idea per paragraph; cite sources
- **Oral presentations:** plan (audience, purpose, time), storyboard, practice aloud, manage nerves; slides support but don't replace the speaker
- **Precise language:** specific verbs over vague ones (administered vs. gave), measurable values over approximations (2 cm vs. "small"), no jargon without definition
- **Active vs. passive voice:** active for clarity in reports; passive has narrow uses (emphasizing the action over the actor)
- **Proofreading for clinical contexts:** decimal errors, contradicted info, ambiguous pronouns, missing units — any of these can harm a patient

## Assessment stub

- Interpret: given a 1-page drug package insert, answer 8 questions about indication, dosing, contraindications, and warnings
- Write: produce a 1-page summary of a real public-health report for a non-expert audience; peer-scored on clarity and precision
- Present: deliver a 5-minute oral presentation on a health topic; observer scores planning, delivery, and precise language using a rubric
