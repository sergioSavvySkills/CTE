## Learning objective

Students will be able to describe the six links in the chain of infection and apply standard precautions to break the chain in common clinical scenarios.

## Content stub

- **Chain of infection:** infectious agent → reservoir → portal of exit → mode of transmission → portal of entry → susceptible host — breaking any one link stops transmission
- **Modes of transmission:** contact (direct/indirect), droplet (large, <6 ft), airborne (small, room-scale), vehicle (food, water, instruments), vector (insects)
- **Standard precautions:** applied to every patient every time — hand hygiene, PPE as indicated, safe injection practices, respiratory etiquette, cleaning/disinfecting surfaces and equipment
- **Transmission-based precautions:** contact (MRSA, C. diff), droplet (flu, pertussis), airborne (TB, measles, COVID) — added on top of standard precautions
- **Reservoirs in healthcare:** hands, surfaces, water, invasive devices (catheters, central lines), contaminated linen or equipment
- **Healthcare-associated infections (HAIs):** CLABSI, CAUTI, SSI, VAP, C. diff — why they're a patient-safety priority and what prevention looks like

## Assessment stub

- Break-the-chain exercise: given 5 scenarios, identify each link and propose one intervention per link
- Precautions match: for 10 conditions (TB, MRSA, C. diff, chickenpox, norovirus, etc.), select the correct precaution category
- Reflection: after observing a simulated patient interaction, list three missed standard-precaution moments and how to correct each
