## Learning objective

Students will be able to describe what an EHR is, navigate common chart sections, and apply safe-practice rules for documentation and access.

## Content stub

- **EHR vs. EMR:** EMR = single-facility chart; EHR = shareable across providers; meaningful-use incentives drove widespread adoption
- **Typical chart sections:** demographics, problem list, allergies, medications, orders, progress notes, results, vitals, imaging, flowsheets
- **Charting basics:** objective language, time-stamped entries, no blank lines, correct-not-erase, late entries marked as late, every entry signed
- **Access controls:** unique login, no shared passwords, auto-logoff, role-based permissions, audit trails — every chart view is logged
- **Common EHR errors:** copy-paste carrying wrong info forward, wrong-patient documentation, alert fatigue causing missed warnings, upcoding
- **Interoperability and the patient's role:** patient portals, HIE exchanges, FHIR APIs, patients as partners in reviewing their own record

## Assessment stub

- Navigation task: in a simulated EHR, locate 8 pieces of information (latest A1c, current meds, allergies, next appointment, etc.)
- Documentation drill: write three progress-note entries for a given scenario using proper format; peer-review for objectivity and completeness
- Audit scenario: given a log showing a clinician viewed a neighbor's chart outside their care team — identify the violation and the consequences
