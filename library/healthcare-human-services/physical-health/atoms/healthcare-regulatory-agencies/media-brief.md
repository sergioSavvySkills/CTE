## Accessibility requirements

- Alt text for each asset must name the concept and the agencies
  involved, not just the imagery (e.g., "grid of six healthcare
  regulatory agency cards with missions and signature products")
- Color choices must not rely on color alone to distinguish
  agencies; use icon shape, label placement, and hatching so the
  distinction survives grayscale printing and common color-vision
  differences
- All diagrams use text labels at minimum 14 pt with high-contrast
  backgrounds
- For m03, the regulation-vs-guideline graphic must include a
  caption line stating that accreditation requirements (Joint
  Commission) are a third category enforced through voluntary
  contract rather than federal law, so students do not assume the
  binary is exhaustive
- Avoid imagery that codes "government" with party symbols or
  politician likenesses; use neutral institutional iconography
  (seals, buildings shown in silhouette, or abstract badges)

## Reuse notes

- **m01** (agency-to-bedside graphic) reuses in any introduction to
  patient safety or infection-control atom that wants to open by
  showing multiple agencies layered on one practice
- **m02** (agency card grid) reuses in any atom that touches one of
  the six agencies — CDC-centric atoms, FDA drug-approval atoms,
  OSHA worker-safety atoms — as the orientation visual
- **m03** (regulation vs. guideline) reuses in any policy or
  compliance atom where the distinction matters, including scope-
  of-practice and research-ethics atoms
- **m05** (sorting CFU) reuses as a formative-assessment template
  whenever the atom needs a quick check on "which agency"
- **m06** (vocab icon set) reuses wherever these anchor terms
  appear downstream, preventing duplicate iconography
