# Sources — common-medical-abbreviations

Every bracketed citation in this atom's `book.md`, `examples.md`, and
`misconceptions.md` resolves to one of the keys below.

## Standards and guidelines

### TJC-DoNotUse

- **Citation:** The Joint Commission. *Official "Do Not Use" List of Abbreviations* (Fact Sheet, updated periodically).
- **URL:** https://www.jointcommission.org/-/media/tjc/documents/standards/national-patient-safety-goals/do_not_use_list.pdf
- **Used for:** the minimum "do not use" list (U/u, IU, QD/QOD, trailing zero, missing leading zero, MS, MSO4, MgSO4), accreditation rationale
- **Confidence:** high — accrediting body for U.S. hospitals

### TJC-NPSG

- **Citation:** The Joint Commission. *National Patient Safety Goals (Hospital Program)*.
- **URL:** https://www.jointcommission.org/standards/national-patient-safety-goals/
- **Used for:** context on why abbreviation safety is a named, audited patient-safety goal
- **Confidence:** high

### ISMP-ErrorProne

- **Citation:** Institute for Safe Medication Practices. *ISMP List of Error-Prone Abbreviations, Symbols, and Dose Designations*.
- **URL:** https://www.ismp.org/recommendations/error-prone-abbreviations-list
- **Used for:** the broader error-prone list beyond the TJC minimum, real-world error case framing
- **Confidence:** high — independent non-profit, widely cited by accreditors and professional bodies

## Government / regulatory pages

### AHRQ-Errors

- **Citation:** Agency for Healthcare Research and Quality. *Patient Safety* topic hub (includes WebM&M case collections on documentation and dose errors).
- **URL:** https://www.ahrq.gov/topics/patient-safety.html
- **Used for:** framing medication-error burden, system-level fixes, culture-of-safety language
- **Confidence:** high — U.S. government health-research agency

### FDA-SafeDose

- **Citation:** U.S. Food and Drug Administration. *Strategies to Reduce Medication Errors* and related safe-dose-designation guidance.
- **URL:** https://www.fda.gov/drugs/information-consumers-and-patients-drugs/working-reduce-medication-errors
- **Used for:** leading-zero / trailing-zero policy rationale, safe dose-expression conventions
- **Confidence:** high

## Other authoritative pages

### ISMP-HighAlert

- **Citation:** Institute for Safe Medication Practices. *ISMP List of High-Alert Medications in Acute Care Settings*.
- **URL:** https://www.ismp.org/recommendations/high-alert-medications-acute-list
- **Used for:** context on why abbreviation errors involving insulin, heparin, and opioids are especially dangerous
- **Confidence:** high
