# Sources — fire-and-emergency-safety

## Standards and guidelines

### NFPA-101

- **Citation:** National Fire Protection Association. *NFPA 101: Life Safety Code*, Chapters 18–19 ("New" and "Existing Health Care Occupancies").
- **URL:** https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/all-codes-and-standards/list-of-codes-and-standards/detail?code=101
- **Used for:** framing of smoke compartments, horizontal-before-vertical evacuation principle, defend-in-place strategy in healthcare occupancies
- **Confidence:** high — consensus national life-safety code

### NFPA-HealthcareFire

- **Citation:** National Fire Protection Association. *Fires in Health Care Facilities* (NFPA Research report).
- **URL:** https://www.nfpa.org/education-and-research/research/nfpa-research/fire-statistical-reports/fires-in-health-care-facilities
- **Used for:** cooking, electrical, and oxygen-related ignition statistics; why defend-in-place works; rarity of full-building healthcare evacuation
- **Confidence:** high — primary fire-data research body

### TJC-EM

- **Citation:** The Joint Commission. *Emergency Management (EM) Chapter* (accreditation standards for hospitals and ambulatory care).
- **URL:** https://www.jointcommission.org/standards/standards-field-reviews/
- **Used for:** annual fire-drill expectation, emergency-code announcement practice, staff competency on RACE and PASS
- **Confidence:** high — dominant U.S. healthcare accreditor

## Government / regulatory pages

### OSHA-EAP

- **Citation:** Occupational Safety and Health Administration. *Emergency Preparedness and Response — Emergency Action Plans* (29 CFR 1910.38) and fire-extinguisher training (29 CFR 1910.157).
- **URL:** https://www.osha.gov/emergency-preparedness
- **Used for:** employer duty to train on alarms, evacuation routes, and portable extinguisher use; fight-vs.-flee criteria
- **Confidence:** high — U.S. federal workplace-safety regulator

### CMS-EmergencyPrep

- **Citation:** Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services. *Emergency Preparedness Rule* (42 CFR Parts 482 and 485, Conditions of Participation).
- **URL:** https://www.cms.gov/medicare/health-safety-standards/quality-safety-oversight-emergency-preparedness
- **Used for:** requirement that participating facilities maintain and train staff on emergency plans; incident-reporting expectations
- **Confidence:** high — U.S. federal healthcare regulator

## Other authoritative pages

### Ready-RunHideFight

- **Citation:** Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) / DHS Ready.gov. *Active Shooter Preparedness — Run, Hide, Fight*.
- **URL:** https://www.cisa.gov/topics/physical-security/active-shooter-preparedness
- **Used for:** framing the initial civilian response to an active-shooter (Code Silver) announcement at the awareness level
- **Confidence:** high — federal preparedness authority
