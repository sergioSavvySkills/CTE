# Media brief — ethics-of-technology-in-healthcare

A 45-minute ethics atom needs visuals that anchor abstract principles to
concrete cases. Per-technology deep-dive diagrams (neural-net
architecture, CRISPR mechanism, ECMO circuit schematic) belong in the
deep-dive atoms for those fields, not this one. This brief keeps the
assets at the level the learning objective targets: a principle, a
technology, and a trade-off visible at a glance.

## Visuals needed

| id | type | purpose | description | priority | status | source |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| m01 | diagram | hook | Four-quadrant diagram of the four principles (autonomy, beneficence, non-maleficence, justice) as labeled quadrants around a patient-centered core. Each quadrant names the question it asks. Anchor image reused in every case analysis. | high | needed | create |
| m02 | chart | concept | 2x5 grid crossing the five technology categories (AI, genomics, telehealth, wearables, end-of-life) with the four principles, with 1-2 words in each cell naming the primary tension. | high | needed | create |
| m03 | diagram | example | Algorithmic-bias schematic: a training-set silhouette, a deployed-population silhouette that differs, and an accuracy gap shown as a bar chart at the output end. Abstract, not technology-specific. | medium | needed | create |
| m04 | diagram | concept | Digital-divide infographic: a map-style graphic showing three patient profiles (rural senior, urban parent, disabled young adult) and which telehealth requirements (broadband, device, privacy, skills) each meets. | medium | needed | create |
| m05 | chart | cfu | GINA coverage chart: two columns showing what GINA covers (health insurance, employment) and what it does not (life, disability, long-term-care insurance, military). Used to probe Misconception 1. | high | needed | create |

## Accessibility requirements

- Alt text on every diagram names the ethical principle or the
  technology category shown and the relationship between them
- Color-coding in the four-quadrant diagram (m01) pairs each principle
  with a distinct label, shape, and pattern — do not rely on color alone
- Avoid red-green as the only distinguishing pair in any asset
- Labels minimum 14 pt in rendered slides; 2x5 grid in m02 must be
  legible at 10-foot distance
- The digital-divide infographic (m04) should not code access as a
  green/red traffic light — use filled/unfilled icons plus labels

## Reuse notes

- **m01** (four-principles quadrant) is the anchor image for this atom
  and for any other case-based ethics atom in the HHS cluster.
  One clean vector original saves recreating it five times.
- **m02** (technology-by-principle grid) is specific to this atom but
  the template pattern can be reused in other cross-cutting ethics
  topics.
- **m05** (GINA coverage chart) may be reused by future genomics
  atoms that touch patient counseling; flag for shared ownership.
- **m06** (advance-directive flow) will reuse in any end-of-life or
  palliative-care atom the curriculum adds later.
