## Learning objective

Students will be able to convert units between measurement systems used in healthcare and interpret tables, charts, and graphs to support clinical decisions.

## Content stub

- **Systems of measurement:** metric (g, kg, mL, L, cm, m, °C) vs. US customary (oz, lb, tsp, tbsp, in, ft, °F); healthcare standardizes on metric
- **Common conversions:** lb↔kg (lb÷2.2), mL↔tsp (5 mL/tsp), °C↔°F ((F-32)÷1.8), kg↔mg for dosing — practice until automatic
- **Reading clinical tables:** lab result reference ranges, drug dosing by weight, BMI tables; spot the outlier quickly
- **Charts and graphs in healthcare:** trend lines (glucose over 24h), bar charts (outcomes by site), scatterplots (BP vs. age), infographics on public-health data
- **Solving health-related problems:** a patient's dose given weight in lb; converting fluid orders; interpreting a growth-chart percentile; fluid-balance totals
- **Common error sources:** unit mixing, decimal misplacement (1.0 vs. 10), misreading chart axes, trusting a graph's scale without checking

## Assessment stub

- Conversion drill: 15 mixed unit-conversion problems (weight, volume, length, temperature) — no calculator; show work
- Chart interpretation: given 3 clinical charts (growth percentile, lab trend, medication curve), answer specific questions about values and trends
- Scenario: calculate a weight-based med dose for a pediatric patient weighing 44 lb using mg/kg dosing; double-check the decimal
