## Standards and guidelines

### OSHA-SafePatient

- **Citation:** Occupational Safety and Health Administration. *Safe Patient Handling: Guidelines for Nursing Homes and Hospital eTool*.
- **URL:** https://www.osha.gov/healthcare/safe-patient-handling
- **Used for:** zero-lift policy framing, manual-lifting risk reduction, employer responsibility as the named federal authority
- **Confidence:** high — U.S. federal workplace-safety regulator

### ANA-SafePatient

- **Citation:** American Nurses Association. *Safe Patient Handling and Mobility (SPHM): Interprofessional National Standards*.
- **URL:** https://www.nursingworld.org/practice-policy/work-environment/health-safety/handle-with-care/
- **Used for:** professional standards for assessing patients before a lift, staffing and equipment expectations
- **Confidence:** high — national professional nursing body

- **Used for:** 35-pound manual-lift limit rule of thumb, cumulative-load risk, ergonomic assessment
- **Confidence:** high — federal research institute for occupational safety

- **Confidence:** high — federal labor statistics agency
