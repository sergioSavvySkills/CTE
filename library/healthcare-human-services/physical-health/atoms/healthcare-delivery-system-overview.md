## Learning objective

Students will be able to describe the major components of the healthcare delivery system — providers, settings, payers, and regulators — and explain how they interact.

## Content stub

- **Care settings:** hospitals, outpatient clinics, long-term care, home health, hospice, urgent care, telehealth
- **Provider types:** physicians, nurses, allied health, behavioral health, public health — and how licensure ties to setting
- **Payers:** private insurance, employer-sponsored plans, Medicare, Medicaid, TRICARE, CHIP, self-pay; difference between payer and provider
- **Regulation:** federal (HHS, CMS, FDA, OSHA), state licensing boards, accreditors (The Joint Commission); what each body actually enforces
- **Continuum of care:** preventive → primary → acute → specialty → post-acute → end-of-life; why transitions are where errors happen
- **Access barriers:** cost, geography, language, insurance status, transportation — and how they drive outcomes

## Assessment stub

- Map it: given a patient scenario, diagram which settings/providers/payers they touch across one episode of illness
- Short answer: distinguish payer, provider, and regulator — give one example of each and what they do
- Research brief: pick one federal agency (CMS, FDA, OSHA) and describe two rules it enforces that affect daily clinical work
