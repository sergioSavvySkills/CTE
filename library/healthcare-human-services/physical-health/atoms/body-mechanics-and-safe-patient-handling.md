## Learning objective

Students will be able to apply correct body mechanics to lifting, transferring, and positioning patients, and identify when lift equipment is required.

## Content stub

- **Center of gravity and base of support:** feet shoulder-width apart, weight centered, knees bent, back straight — the foundation for every lift
- **Lift with legs, not back:** bend at the hips and knees, keep the load close, avoid twisting; pivot with feet instead
- **Patient handling risks:** musculoskeletal injuries are the top occupational hazard in healthcare; cumulative strain is the real killer, not single lifts
- **Transfers:** bed-to-chair, chair-to-bed, stand-pivot — when each works, when a sit-to-stand aid or sling is needed
- **Safe-patient-handling programs:** ceiling lifts, sit-to-stand devices, slide sheets, friction-reducing boards — zero-lift policies
- **Before any lift:** assess patient weight, cognition, ability to assist, fall risk, lines/tubes; ask for help rather than lifting alone

## Assessment stub

- Skill check: demonstrate a stand-pivot transfer and a proper bed boost with a peer, scored against a checklist
- Ergonomic audit: identify 5 body-mechanics errors in a video of patient care and propose corrections
- Decision scenario: given 5 patient descriptions (weight, cognition, mobility), decide which require a lift device and which can be done with one or two staff
