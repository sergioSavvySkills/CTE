## Learning objective

Students will be able to select the correct personal protective equipment for a given precaution and demonstrate the correct donning and doffing sequence without self-contamination.

## Content stub

- **PPE types:** gloves, gown, mask (surgical vs. N95), face shield/goggles, shoe covers, head cover — what each protects and from what
- **Matching PPE to precautions:** contact (gown + gloves), droplet (mask + eye protection), airborne (N95 + eye protection, AIIR); high-consequence pathogens layer more
- **Donning sequence:** gown → mask/respirator → goggles/face shield → gloves (gloves last, covering gown cuffs)
- **Doffing sequence:** gloves → goggles/face shield → gown (roll inside out) → mask/respirator (by ties/ear loops, not the front) → hand hygiene
- **Fit testing and seal checks:** N95 respirators require annual fit testing and a user seal check every time; surgical masks don't
- **Common mistakes:** reusing single-use items, touching the mask front, doffing in the wrong order, stepping into the hallway with gown still on

## Assessment stub

- Skill check: don and doff a full contact-precautions ensemble on a peer-observer; no self-contamination, correct sequence, hand hygiene at the right moments
- Selection quiz: for 10 precaution scenarios, pick the correct PPE set (gloves? mask? N95? eye protection?)
- Error analysis: watch a video of PPE use with 5 deliberate errors and identify each plus the infection risk it creates
