## Learning objective

Students will be able to identify the major agencies that regulate and oversee healthcare safety and relate their rules to daily safety practices in the health science industry.

## Content stub

- **WHO (World Health Organization):** global public health standards, pandemic coordination, IHR (International Health Regulations), hand-hygiene guidelines
- **CDC (Centers for Disease Control and Prevention):** U.S. disease surveillance, standard and transmission-based precautions, immunization schedule, outbreak response
- **OSHA (Occupational Safety and Health Administration):** workplace safety for healthcare workers — bloodborne pathogens (29 CFR 1910.1030), PPE, workplace violence
- **FDA (U.S. Food and Drug Administration):** drug and device approval, safety alerts, medical-device reporting (MDR), post-market surveillance
- **The Joint Commission:** voluntary accreditor for hospitals and health systems; National Patient Safety Goals, sentinel event reporting, tracer surveys
- **NIH (National Institutes of Health):** research funding and oversight, protection of human subjects; and, more broadly, how agencies translate into daily practices (cleaning protocols, PPE, incident reporting, OSHA logs)

## Assessment stub

- Agency match: given 12 rules or practices (bloodborne pathogen training, PPE requirement, hand-hygiene policy, vaccine schedule, device recall, sentinel event review), identify which agency drives each
- Research: pick one agency and describe two concrete requirements it places on a hospital's day-to-day work; cite the rule or document
- Scenario walk: a needlestick injury on the floor. Identify which agency's rule governs the response and walk through the reporting steps
