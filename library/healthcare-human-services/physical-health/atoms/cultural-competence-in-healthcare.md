## Learning objective

Students will be able to distinguish cultural competence from cultural humility, recognize health disparities tied to social identity, and adapt their approach to culturally diverse patients.

## Content stub

- **Competence vs. humility:** competence = learned facts about cultures; humility = lifelong posture of curiosity and self-reflection — you need both, humility matters more
- **Social determinants of health:** income, housing, education, food access, transportation, environment — often outweigh clinical care in outcomes
- **Disparities by identity:** race, ethnicity, language, gender, sexual orientation, disability, immigration status, rural/urban — and the specific outcome gaps each correlates with
- **Bias in care:** implicit bias studies in pain treatment, cardiac care, maternal mortality; how bias shapes assessment, listening, and follow-through
- **Working with interpreters:** qualified medical interpreters (not family), speak directly to the patient, short clear sentences, written materials in the right language
- **Adaptations:** dietary restrictions, modesty norms, family decision-making, gender of the clinician, spiritual practices, distrust of healthcare systems

## Assessment stub

- Case analysis: read 3 cases of documented disparity (Black maternal mortality, Indigenous diabetes care, LGBTQ+ primary care) and identify system-level and clinician-level contributors
- Role-play: conduct a clinical intake with an interpreter (peer acts as interpreter); observers score direct address, sentence length, and patient focus
- Reflection: identify one bias you might hold and plan two concrete behaviors to counter it in clinical situations
