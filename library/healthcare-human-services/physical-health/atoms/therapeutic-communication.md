## Learning objective

Students will be able to apply therapeutic communication techniques — active listening, open questions, empathy, and silence — in patient interactions, and recognize common communication barriers.

## Content stub

- **Active listening:** eye contact, open body posture, paraphrasing, summarizing, not interrupting — the difference between hearing and listening
- **Open vs. closed questions:** "How have you been feeling?" invites story; "Did you take your meds?" gets yes/no. Use each deliberately
- **Empathy statements:** "That sounds really hard" — naming the emotion without judging it; different from sympathy, which is "I feel bad for you"
- **Productive silence:** after a hard question, letting the patient think before filling the space; 3-5 seconds feels long but often surfaces the real concern
- **Barriers:** language, hearing, vision, cognitive impairment, stress, cultural differences, jargon, rushing, assumptions — and concrete adaptations for each
- **Nonverbal signals:** facial expression, tone, pacing, proximity, eye contact — often communicate more than words, and cultures interpret them differently

## Assessment stub

- Role-play: conduct a 5-minute mock patient intake; a peer scores you on open questions, paraphrasing, empathy, and silence
- Critique a transcript: annotate a scripted patient-provider conversation, marking each therapeutic technique and each barrier
- Adaptation plan: for a patient who is hard of hearing, speaks limited English, or is anxious, list three specific communication adjustments
