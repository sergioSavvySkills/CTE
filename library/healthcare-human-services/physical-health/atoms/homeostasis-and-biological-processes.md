## Learning objective

Students will be able to describe homeostasis as the body's regulated steady state and explain the biological and chemical feedback loops that maintain it.

## Content stub

- **Homeostasis defined:** the dynamic equilibrium of internal conditions (temperature, pH, blood glucose, fluid, electrolytes) despite external change
- **Negative feedback loops:** the dominant mechanism — sensors detect change, control center compares to setpoint, effectors restore. Thermoregulation and glucose regulation as canonical examples
- **Positive feedback loops:** rarer, amplifying — childbirth (oxytocin), blood clotting
- **Key chemical processes:** pH buffering by bicarbonate/carbonic-acid, oxygen transport by hemoglobin, glucose metabolism by insulin/glucagon, water balance by ADH
- **When homeostasis fails:** fever (pyrogens reset the setpoint), diabetes (insulin failure), dehydration, acidosis/alkalosis — the beginning of every disease state
- **Every body system contributes:** nervous system senses/signals, endocrine adjusts chemistry, circulatory transports, respiratory/renal excrete — no system acts alone

## Assessment stub

- Diagram: sketch a negative feedback loop for body temperature regulation; label sensor, control center, effectors, and setpoint
- Classify: given 10 biological events, identify each as negative feedback, positive feedback, or neither
- Scenario: a patient has uncontrolled type 1 diabetes. Explain which homeostatic loop has failed, what is breaking down chemically, and what the visible signs would be
