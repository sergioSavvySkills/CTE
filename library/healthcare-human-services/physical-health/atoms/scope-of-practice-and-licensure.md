## Learning objective

Students will be able to explain how licensure, certification, and scope of practice define what each healthcare worker can legally do, and the risks of acting outside scope.

## Content stub

- **Licensure vs. certification vs. registration:** legal authority to practice (license) vs. verified competency (cert) vs. listing on a roster (reg); who issues each
- **State practice acts:** nurse practice acts, medical practice acts, pharmacy acts — state boards define and enforce scope
- **Delegation:** what tasks a nurse can delegate to a CNA; what a physician can delegate to an MA; the "five rights" of delegation
- **Working within scope:** knowing your role's permitted tasks; recognizing the line between "help" and "practicing without a license"
- **Consequences of scope violations:** license suspension, civil liability, criminal charges, patient harm, institutional sanctions
- **Mobility and interstate practice:** Nurse Licensure Compact, state-by-state variation, telehealth across state lines

## Assessment stub

- Scope grid: given 15 clinical tasks, check which roles (CNA, MA, LVN, RN, NP, MD) can legally perform each in your state
- Delegation drill: 5 scenarios — identify whether the task can be delegated, to whom, and why
- Research: look up your state's nurse (or medical assistant) practice act and identify two tasks students commonly assume are in scope but are not
