## Learning objective

Students will be able to describe the major stages of human development and identify the health needs, risks, and communication adaptations at each stage.

## Content stub

- **Infancy (0-1):** rapid physical growth, sensorimotor learning; health focus — feeding, attachment, immunizations, safe sleep, SIDS prevention
- **Early childhood (1-6):** language explosion, preoperational thought, autonomy; health focus — nutrition, injury prevention, developmental screening, vaccines
- **School age (6-12):** concrete operations, peer relationships, industry vs. inferiority; health focus — vision/hearing, mental health, obesity prevention
- **Adolescence (12-18):** formal operations, identity formation, risk-taking; health focus — mental health, substance use, sexual health, injury, eating disorders
- **Young and middle adulthood (18-65):** career, relationships, generativity; health focus — chronic disease prevention, reproductive health, screening, mental health
- **Older adulthood (65+):** cognitive and physical decline, integrity vs. despair, grief; health focus — polypharmacy, falls, cognitive screening, chronic disease management, advance care planning

## Assessment stub

- Match: given 10 developmental milestones, match each to the correct stage
- Health-focus chart: complete a grid of 6 stages × 3 top health priorities with evidence-based sources
- Communication adaptation: for 4 patient ages (2-year-old, 8-year-old, 16-year-old, 82-year-old with mild dementia), describe three specific communication adaptations you would use
