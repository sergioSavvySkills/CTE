# Sources — healthcare-equipment-and-malfunction-reporting

Every bracketed citation in this atom's `book.md`, `examples.md`, and
`misconceptions.md` resolves to one of the keys below.

## Standards and guidelines

### AAMI-DeviceSafety

- **Citation:** Association for the Advancement of Medical Instrumentation.
  *Medical device safety and equipment management resources.*
- **URL:** https://www.aami.org/standards
- **Used for:** framing of medical-device safety practices, preventive
  maintenance expectations, and biomed/healthcare-technology-management
  role descriptions used by U.S. hospitals
- **Confidence:** high — primary professional body for medical
  instrumentation standards

### TJC-EC

- **Citation:** The Joint Commission. *Environment of Care (EC) standards —
  medical equipment management.*
- **URL:** https://www.jointcommission.org/standards/
- **Used for:** accreditation expectations that hospitals inventory
  equipment, run preventive maintenance, track work orders, and remove
  unsafe equipment from service
- **Confidence:** high — accrediting body for U.S. hospitals

### OSHA-LOTO

- **Citation:** Occupational Safety and Health Administration. *Control of
  Hazardous Energy (Lockout/Tagout) — 29 CFR 1910.147.*
- **URL:** https://www.osha.gov/laws-regs/regulations/standardnumber/1910/1910.147
- **Used for:** the lockout/tagout principle applied to out-of-service
  healthcare equipment, "Do Not Use" tagging, and preventing accidental
  energization of damaged devices
- **Confidence:** high — federal workplace-safety regulator

### CDC-SterilizationGuidelines

- **Citation:** Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, Healthcare
  Infection Control Practices Advisory Committee. *Guideline for
  Disinfection and Sterilization in Healthcare Facilities (2008, reaffirmed).*
- **URL:** https://www.cdc.gov/infection-control/hcp/disinfection-sterilization/
- **Used for:** sterilizer-failure scenarios, biological-indicator-failure
  response, and support-services equipment malfunctions that affect
  patient safety
- **Confidence:** high — federal public-health regulator

## Government / regulatory pages

### FDA-MDR

- **Citation:** U.S. Food and Drug Administration. *Medical Device
  Reporting (MDR) — mandatory and voluntary adverse event reporting for
  medical devices.*
- **URL:** https://www.fda.gov/medical-devices/medical-device-safety/medical-device-reporting-mdr
- **Used for:** the MDR reporting path for device-related patient harm,
  who is required to report, and the distinction between internal
  incident reports and the federal MDR submission
- **Confidence:** high — federal medical-device regulator

### FDA-DeviceRecalls

- **Citation:** U.S. Food and Drug Administration. *Medical Device
  Recalls — how FDA and manufacturers notify the field when a device
  poses a risk.*
- **URL:** https://www.fda.gov/medical-devices/medical-device-safety/medical-device-recalls
- **Used for:** the recall framing for widespread device issues and the
  staff response when a recall notice arrives at a facility
- **Confidence:** high — federal medical-device regulator

## Other authoritative pages

### ECRI-AlertsList

- **Citation:** ECRI Institute. *Top 10 Health Technology Hazards — annual
  list of the most common and consequential medical-device safety issues
  observed across U.S. healthcare facilities.*
- **URL:** https://www.ecri.org/top-10-health-technology-hazards
- **Used for:** illustrative examples of common real-world equipment
  problems (alarm hazards, IV-pump errors, single-use-device reuse),
  used to ground the "signs of malfunction" discussion in patterns
  reviewers actually see
- **Confidence:** high — independent patient-safety research organization
  widely cited by accreditors and federal regulators
