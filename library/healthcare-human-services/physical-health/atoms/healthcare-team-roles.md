## Learning objective

Students will be able to identify the major roles on a healthcare team, describe what each can and cannot do within their scope, and explain why team-based care improves outcomes.

## Content stub

- **Physicians and advanced practice providers:** MD/DO, PA, NP — who diagnoses, prescribes, performs procedures; differences in training and autonomy
- **Nursing ladder:** CNA, LPN/LVN, RN, BSN, MSN, NP — what each can assess, administer, delegate
- **Allied health:** respiratory therapists, radiology techs, medical lab scientists, pharmacists, PT/OT/SLP, medical assistants — role-specific tasks
- **Support and admin:** unit clerks, patient access, coders, billers, housekeeping, food service, transport — "invisible" roles that keep care flowing
- **Behavioral and social support:** social workers, case managers, chaplains, counselors — addressing non-clinical determinants of health
- **Why teams beat individuals:** complementary expertise, cross-checks, handoffs, SBAR communication, reduced errors

## Assessment stub

- Role grid: given 12 patient care tasks, identify which role performs each (and which cannot)
- Case study: a diabetic patient with new stroke — list every team member who touches their care in 24 hours and what they contribute
- Short answer: a CNA notices a change in a patient's breathing. Walk through who they tell, what they cannot do themselves, and why
