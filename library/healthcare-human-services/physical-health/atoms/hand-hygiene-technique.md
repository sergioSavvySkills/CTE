## Learning objective

Students will be able to perform correct hand-hygiene technique (soap-and-water and alcohol-based hand rub) and identify when each of the WHO "Five Moments" applies.

## Content stub

- **Why hands:** hands transmit most healthcare-associated infections; hand hygiene is the single most effective prevention measure
- **Five moments (WHO):** before patient contact, before aseptic task, after body-fluid exposure risk, after patient contact, after contact with patient surroundings
- **Soap-and-water technique:** wet, lather, scrub all surfaces for 20 seconds including under nails, rinse, dry with paper towel, turn off tap with towel
- **Alcohol-based hand rub (ABHR):** enough volume to cover all surfaces, rub for ~20 seconds until fully dry, no wiping; don't use if hands visibly soiled
- **When soap is required:** visibly soiled hands, after using the restroom, after caring for C. diff or norovirus patients — ABHR doesn't kill spores
- **Practical barriers:** jewelry, artificial nails, chapped skin, time pressure, empty dispensers; how institutions remove them

## Assessment stub

- Skill check: demonstrate both techniques on a peer-observer using a checklist; meet the 20-second timing
- Moments quiz: given 10 clinical micro-scenarios, identify which of the Five Moments applies and which technique is correct
- Observation: in a simulation or video, count missed hand-hygiene moments during a simulated 10-minute care sequence and rank barriers
