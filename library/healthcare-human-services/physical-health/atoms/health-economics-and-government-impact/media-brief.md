## Accessibility requirements

- Alt text for each asset must name the concept, not the imagery
  (e.g., "three-column table of federal, state, and local
  healthcare roles" rather than "a government building")
- All numeric-looking graphics (m01, m04) must carry a caption
  stating the figures are order-of-magnitude and change annually,
  and must name the source (CMS NHE, BLS OOH, CBO)
- Color choices for m02, m03, and m05 must not rely on color alone
  to distinguish layer or lever; use label placement, line weight,
  or hatching so the distinction survives grayscale printing and
  common color-vision differences
- All diagrams use text labels at minimum 14 pt with high-contrast
  backgrounds
- Avoid imagery that codes "government" with party symbols,
  politician likenesses, or politically charged colors; use neutral
  institutional iconography

## Reuse notes

- **m01** (scale graphic) reuses in any atom that opens with the
  "how big is healthcare" framing — insurance economics, public
  health, workforce overview atoms
- **m02** (three-column federal/state/local) reuses in any civics
  or public-policy atom that touches health
- **m03** (four levers) reuses in any policy-mechanics atom as a
  framing diagram before diving into specifics
- **m04** (cost drivers) reuses in insurance and payment-reform
  atoms; keep it conceptual so it does not age out quickly
- **m06** (vocab icon set) reuses wherever this atom's anchor
  terms appear, preventing duplicate iconography across the
  curriculum
