## Learning objective

Students will be able to describe how professional organizations prepare practitioners and govern credentialing and certification across health careers.

## Content stub

- **Why professional organizations exist:** set scope and standards, credential, publish guidelines, advocate, continuing education; they shape the profession itself
- **Major physician and nurse bodies:** AMA, AANP, specialty boards (ABIM, ABFM); ANA, NCSBN, AACN, AAMN, specialty certifications (CCRN, CEN)
- **Allied health bodies:** ASRT (radiology), AARC (respiratory), ASCLS (lab science), APTA (PT), AOTA (OT), ASHA (SLP), AAMA/AMT (medical assisting)
- **Credentialing bodies:** NBME/USMLE (physician licensing exams), NCSBN (NCLEX), ARRT, CCI, NREMT; often different from the professional society
- **What a credential actually governs:** initial eligibility (education, clinical hours), examination, maintenance (CE hours, practice hours, ethics), renewal cycles
- **Student organizations and pathways:** HOSA (Future Health Professionals), health-pro student chapters, college pre-health clubs — early exposure and mentoring

## Assessment stub

- Match: given 10 professional organizations and 10 roles, match each organization to the role it governs or supports
- Credential research: pick one credential you might pursue; describe its sponsoring body, eligibility, exam, and renewal requirements with specific sources
- Short essay: explain why the professional organization for nursing (ANA) is different from the credentialing body (NCSBN) and why that matters
