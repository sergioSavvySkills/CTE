## Learning objective

Students will be able to identify the employer expectations the TEKS names — punctuality, attendance, time management, communication, organizational skills, and productive work habits — and describe observable behaviors that demonstrate each one in a healthcare workplace.

## Content stub

- **Punctuality vs. attendance, distinguished:** punctuality = arriving ready to work at the scheduled time, not just present; attendance = the pattern of showing up, including call-out protocols when you genuinely cannot
- **Patient-safety cost of no-shows and late arrivals:** shifts run on minimum staffing ratios; a missing team member means unsafe patient loads for everyone else
- **Time management in clinical work:** prioritize by acuity, not convenience; finish tasks you start; document in real time rather than end-of-shift
- **Communication as an employer expectation:** the short, clear, accurate, confirmed message — this standard directly reinforces §127.403(c)(1)(A)
- **Organizational skills:** knowing where supplies and records live, keeping your own documentation current, tracking open tasks
- **Productive work habits:** focused work, minimal personal-phone use, care with supplies and equipment, staying off social media about work
- **Appearance and grooming as employer expectations:** uniform, name badge visible, short nails, hair secured, minimal jewelry — these are infection-control and patient-identification requirements, not vanity rules
- **Receiving feedback:** treat it as information for improvement, not an attack; ask clarifying questions; change behavior

## Assessment stub

- Self-assessment: rate yourself 1–5 on each of the six TEKS-named expectations; pick three to improve and write a concrete observable action for each (e.g., "set phone on Do Not Disturb from 0700–1500")
- Scenario analysis: given 5 workplace situations (late arrival, phone use during a patient interaction, sharing patient gossip, refusing to cover a shift, dress-code violation), describe the professional response and explain the underlying expectation it honors
- Portfolio artifact: draft a one-page "professional standards commitment" for clinical rotations, listing observable behaviors for each of the six TEKS expectations

## References

- **Texas Workforce Commission / TEA CTE employability-skills framework** — the state-level taxonomy that seeds the expectations this standard names; aligns with TEKS §127.403 subsection (c)(1).
- **O*NET and BLS occupational "work activities" and "work styles" descriptors** — standardized, evidence-based descriptions of the behaviors employers expect across healthcare occupations, useful for building rubrics.
- **OSHA / CDC workplace-attire standards** — uniform, nail, jewelry, and hair guidelines as infection-control requirements rather than discretionary dress codes (CDC Guideline for Isolation Precautions; OSHA Bloodborne Pathogens Standard, 29 CFR 1910.1030).
- **Joint Commission workplace-behavior standards** — unprofessional conduct is addressed as a patient-safety issue, reinforcing why these expectations are enforced, not optional.
