## Learning objective

Students will be able to conduct and participate in clinical meetings — shift briefings, team huddles, post-event debriefs, and standing staff meetings — producing written agendas and action items and using structured facilitation to keep the meeting on time and on topic.

## Content stub

- **Two roles to demonstrate:** *conducting* a meeting (facilitating) and *participating* in a meeting (contributing) — both are testable
- **Clinical meeting formats students will encounter (TeamSTEPPS):**
  - *Brief* — start-of-shift or start-of-case: goals, roles, anticipated issues (3–5 min)
  - *Huddle* — mid-shift or mid-case regroup when conditions change (2–3 min)
  - *Debrief* — post-event review: what went well, what didn't, what changes (5–10 min)
  - *Standing staff meeting* — recurring, agenda-driven, decisions and follow-ups (30–60 min)
  - *Care-team/interdisciplinary rounds* — patient-focused, each role contributes
- **Conducting a meeting — facilitator behaviors:**
  - Written agenda shared before the meeting (topics, time allocation, decisions needed)
  - Start on time and end on time — respect attendees' time
  - State the purpose and desired outcome at the top
  - Move through the agenda; park off-topic items rather than lose control
  - Invite quieter voices; manage dominant voices without shaming
  - Summarize decisions and action items out loud before closing: who does what by when
  - Written follow-up within 24 hours
- **Participating effectively — attendee behaviors:**
  - Arrive prepared (agenda read, materials at hand)
  - Contribute without monopolizing; use I-statements when offering a position
  - Speak up on safety concerns (CUS, two-challenge rule) rather than saving them for the hallway
  - Avoid side conversations and phones
  - Take ownership of action items assigned to you; say "no" honestly rather than silently not doing it
- **Virtual and hybrid meeting etiquette:** video on when bandwidth allows (builds trust), mute when not speaking, use chat to signal questions, visibly include remote attendees in decisions
- **Respecting confidentiality:** patient identifiers are not discussed in public spaces, elevators, or on personal phones; HIPAA applies to every meeting room and hallway conversation
- **Common meeting failures and fixes:**
  - *No agenda* → decisions float; fix: require an agenda to call the meeting
  - *Dominant voices* → silent disagreement later; fix: facilitator invites quieter voices by name
  - *No action items named* → nothing happens; fix: end every meeting with "who does what by when"

## Assessment stub

- Agenda-build exercise: given a realistic clinical scenario (e.g., planning a quality-improvement project for hand hygiene on your floor), produce a written agenda with topics, time allocations, and named decisions needed
- Conduct practical: lead a 10-minute simulated team meeting using the agenda you produced; observer rubric scores start-on-time, agenda adherence, voice inclusion, decisions-and-action-items summary, and on-time close
- Participate practical: in a separate simulated meeting led by a peer, demonstrate preparation, a non-monopolizing contribution, one speak-up moment using CUS, and ownership of an action item
- Brief-huddle-debrief sequence: with a group of 4, run all three in sequence around a simulated clinical scenario; observer evaluates each separately against TeamSTEPPS criteria

## References

- **AHRQ TeamSTEPPS 3.0 — Brief, Huddle, Debrief tools** — the authoritative format for short clinical meetings in US healthcare.
- **Joint Commission standards on team communication and handoff protocols** — accreditation-backed requirements that shape real clinical meeting practice.
- **Robert's Rules of Order** — for standing staff meetings and governance bodies; still the default parliamentary procedure in US healthcare committees.
- **Institute for Healthcare Improvement (IHI) Huddle templates** — widely adopted one-page huddle formats for clinical teams.
- **Project Oxygen (Google) research on productive meetings** — behavioral evidence for the agenda/decision/action-item pattern.
