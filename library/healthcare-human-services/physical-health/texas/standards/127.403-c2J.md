## Learning objective

Students will be able to describe the major stages of human development across the lifespan — infancy through older adulthood — and identify the dominant health needs, risks, and communication adaptations at each stage.

## Content stub

- **Infancy (0–1 yr):** rapid physical growth, sensorimotor learning, attachment formation; health focus — feeding, immunizations, safe sleep (SIDS prevention), developmental screening
- **Early childhood (1–6 yr):** language explosion, preoperational thought, autonomy struggles; health focus — nutrition, injury prevention, vaccines, early-intervention screening
- **School age (6–12 yr):** concrete operational thinking, peer relationships, industry vs. inferiority (Erikson); health focus — vision/hearing screening, mental health, obesity prevention
- **Adolescence (12–18 yr):** formal operational thinking, identity formation, risk-taking (developing prefrontal cortex); health focus — mental health, substance use, sexual health, injury, eating disorders
- **Young and middle adulthood (18–65 yr):** career and relationship formation, generativity; health focus — chronic-disease prevention, reproductive health, cancer screening, mental health
- **Older adulthood (65+ yr):** cognitive and physical changes, integrity vs. despair, grief; health focus — polypharmacy, fall prevention, cognitive screening, chronic-disease management, advance care planning
- **Using stages in clinical work:** developmental stage drives what assessments are done, how consent is obtained, what communication style lands, and what risks to screen for

## Assessment stub

- Match: given 10 developmental milestones or behaviors, match each to the correct stage
- Health-focus grid: complete a 6 stages × 3 top-health-priorities table, citing the source (AAP Bright Futures, CDC, USPSTF) for each priority
- Communication adaptation: for 4 patient ages (2-year-old, 8-year-old, 16-year-old, 82-year-old with mild dementia), describe three specific communication adaptations and justify each

## References

- **Erikson, *Childhood and Society* (1950) — Eight Stages of Psychosocial Development** — the canonical stage model used across nursing, social work, and allied health education.
- **Piaget's Stages of Cognitive Development** — complementary cognitive-stage framework (sensorimotor, preoperational, concrete operational, formal operational).
- **AAP *Bright Futures: Guidelines for Health Supervision of Infants, Children, and Adolescents*** — evidence-based age-specific health priorities from birth through 21.
- **USPSTF Recommendations by Age Group** — age-specific screening guidance for adults.
- **CDC Developmental Milestones ("Learn the Signs. Act Early.")** — public-facing milestone checklists by age.
