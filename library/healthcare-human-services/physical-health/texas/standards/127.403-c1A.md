## Learning objective

Students will be able to express ideas in a clear, concise, and effective manner using speaking, writing, and visual formats appropriate to a health science workplace audience.

## Content stub

- **Attributes of clear communication:** audience awareness, purpose, structure, plain language, tone
- **Conciseness:** cut filler, lead with the main point, respect the reader's or listener's time
- **Effectiveness criteria:** the message is received, understood, and acted on — confirm with read-back or paraphrase
- **Precise language:** specific verbs over vague ones (administered vs. gave), measurable values over approximations (2 cm vs. "small"), no jargon without definition
- **Active voice for clarity:** active in most workplace communication; passive only when emphasizing the action over the actor
- **Modes covered by this standard:** spoken (handoff reports, huddles), written (chart notes, emails, incident documentation), visual (signage, patient education aids)
- **Workplace stakes:** miscommunication in healthcare affects patient safety, continuity of care, and the legal record

## Vocabulary

- **Audience awareness** — shaping word choice, detail level, and tone for the specific reader or listener (peer clinician vs. patient vs. family vs. supervisor).
- **Plain language** — everyday words for everyday meanings; the health-literacy writing standard that makes a message understandable on first reading.
- **Precise language** — specific, measurable terms over vague ones ("administered 2 mg" not "gave a little"); eliminates clinical ambiguity. *(also in c2F)*
- **Active voice** — sentence structure where the subject performs the action ("the nurse gave the medication"); default for workplace communication because it names the actor and is shorter.
- **Passive voice** — sentence structure where the subject receives the action ("the medication was given"); appropriate only when the action matters more than the actor.
- **Read-back / check-back** — the listener repeats a spoken message back to the sender to confirm accurate receipt; TeamSTEPPS closed-loop communication.
- **SBAR** — Situation, Background, Assessment, Recommendation; structured handoff format used in clinical transitions.
- **Handoff** — structured transfer of patient information and responsibility from one clinician to another, typically at shift change or care-setting transition. *(also in c1B)*
- **"Do Not Use" list** — ISMP / Joint Commission list of abbreviations (e.g., "U" for units, "QD") prohibited in clinical documentation because they cause medication errors. *(also in c2F, c3D)*
- **Teach-back** — patient-education technique where the patient explains instructions back in their own words to confirm understanding. *(also in c3B)*

## Assessment stub

- Rewrite a rambling patient-care note into a clear, concise entry; score against a rubric (audience, brevity, completeness, precision)
- Deliver a 60-second verbal handoff to a peer; peer paraphrases back to demonstrate the message was received accurately
- Given four sample workplace communications for the same scenario, identify which best fits a stated audience and purpose and justify the choice

## Activities

- **SBAR Handoff Builder** — drag 40 unsorted patient facts into Situation / Background / Assessment / Recommendation slots under a 90-second timer. End-of-round reveals the gold placement and the rubric score.
  → *application, procedural fluency*

- **Rewrite This Chart Note** — a rambling 6-sentence progress note appears; student compresses it to ≤3 clean sentences against a rubric (audience, brevity, precision). The model answer unlocks only after submit, so students have to commit before comparing.
  → *communication under pressure, metacognition*

- **Active-vs-Passive Spotter** — an incident report paragraph appears; every passive-voice sentence is a click target. Student clicks all passives in 30 seconds, then rewrites the top three in active voice. Bonus for correctly *not* clicking the two sentences where passive is justified ("the medication was administered at 14:00").
  → *pattern recognition, metacognition*

## References

- **SBAR (Situation, Background, Assessment, Recommendation)** — structured handoff format; Institute for Healthcare Improvement and widely adopted by Joint Commission–accredited organizations. Frames the "60-second verbal handoff" assessment.
- **TeamSTEPPS 3.0 (AHRQ)** — teamwork and communication curriculum; defines "check-back" (closed-loop communication) as a standard tool for confirming a message was received and understood. Frames the read-back / paraphrase practice.
- **Joint Commission National Patient Safety Goals** — NPSG.02.03.01 (critical-test-results read-back) and sentinel-event root-cause data that consistently identify communication failures as a leading contributor to patient harm.
- **AHRQ Health Literacy Universal Precautions Toolkit** and **CDC Plain Language guidelines** — plain-language writing standards for patient-facing and workplace health communication.
- **ISMP / Joint Commission "Do Not Use" list** — precision-language guidance for clinical documentation; supports the "measurable values over approximations, no jargon without definition" bullet.

### SBAR Handoff Builder

```yaml
name: SBAR Handoff Builder
format: drag_and_drop
supports:
  primary: 127.403-c1A
  secondary: [127.403-c3A]
duration_min: 15
mode: single_player
equipment: one_device_per_student
learning_goal: >
  Given 40 unsorted patient facts, sort each fact into the correct SBAR slot
  (Situation, Background, Assessment, Recommendation) with at least 80%
  accuracy under a 90-second timer.
vocab_exercised:
  - SBAR
  - Handoff
  - Precise language
  - Read-back / check-back
content_model: list_of_cases
content:
  case_pool_size: 12
  facts_per_case: 40
  each_case_provides:
    - patient_vignette  # 2-3 sentence clinical context
    - facts             # 40 mixed across the four SBAR categories
    - gold_placement    # canonical slot for each fact
initial_state:
  timer_seconds: 90
  case_rotation: random_without_replacement
  difficulty: standard
controls: [drag, drop, submit, skip_case]
feedback:
  moment: end_of_round
  shows: gold placement and one-sentence explanation per missed fact
result:
  type: pass_fail
  pass_rule: >
    Student places at least 20 of 40 facts in their gold slot before the 90-second timer expires.
  instructions_for_scorer: >
    Count correct placements at end of round. 20 or more = pass; below 20 = fail, offer a new case.
    Ambiguous facts (e.g., a lab value that fits Background or Assessment) count as correct when the
    student's slot matches gold_placement OR the student can justify an alternate placement in debrief.
student_view: >
  Four SBAR slots down the left; 40 fact-cards on the right;
  countdown timer top-right; submit button bottom.
teacher_view: >
  Class pass rate per case; flag any student who failed two cases in a row.
accessibility:
  - Keyboard-only dragging supported (space to pick up, arrow keys to move, space to drop)
  - Color-blind safe slot tints (not red/green)
  - Screen reader announces slot name on focus and fact-card text on pick-up
success_criteria: Student passes 2 of 3 cases within one class period.
tech_stack:
  base: html+css+js
  libraries:
    - name: Tailwind CSS (Play CDN)
      cdn_url: https://cdn.tailwindcss.com
      purpose: Type scale, spacing, color palette for slots and fact cards
    - name: Alpine.js
      cdn_url: https://unpkg.com/alpinejs@3.x.x/dist/cdn.min.js
      purpose: State for drag source, drop target, timer, per-fact placement
ai_required: []
visual_notes: >
  Card-on-rail layout; muted clinical palette (slate + cyan accent);
  fact cards lift subtly on hover; correct/incorrect feedback appears
  as a soft color ring on the card — never a modal dialog.
```

**Instructions to student:** You will see a patient case with 40 facts drawn from the chart. Drag each fact into the correct SBAR slot — Situation, Background, Assessment, Recommendation — before the 90-second timer expires. Get 20 out of 40 right to pass. You can skip any case; the next one is different.

**Teacher notes:** Run 3 cases per student per class period. After the activity, spend 5 minutes on a whole-class debrief looking at the teacher-view heatmap — focus on the 2–3 facts most commonly misplaced. Ambiguous cases (a lab value often belongs in Background *or* Assessment depending on whether it triggered a decision) are the most productive to discuss; they're where the SBAR framework stops being mechanical and starts requiring clinical judgment.

### Rewrite This Chart Note

```yaml
name: Rewrite This Chart Note
format: build_and_critique
supports:
  primary: 127.403-c1A
  secondary: [127.403-c2D, 127.403-c2F]
duration_min: 12
mode: single_player
equipment: one_device_per_student
learning_goal: >
  Given a rambling 6-sentence progress note, produce a rewrite of at most
  3 sentences that scores at least 20/25 on a 5-dimension rubric (audience
  fit, brevity, precision, completeness, clarity).
vocab_exercised:
  - SBAR
  - Precise language
  - Plain language
  - "Do Not Use" list
content_model: pass_fail_writing_task
content:
  note_pool_size: 12
  each_note_provides:
    - source_text       # the rambling 6-sentence note
    - gold_rewrite      # one canonical 3-sentence compression (of many valid rewrites)
    - required_facts    # keywords that must survive in the rewrite
    - banned_phrases    # vague language the source contains on purpose
    - audience          # stated reader: peer_clinician | patient | supervisor (scenario flavor)
  notes: []             # populate with 12 entries matching the schema above. PLACEHOLDER.
initial_state:
  gold_visible: false       # unlocks only after submit so students commit first
controls: [type, backspace, undo, redo, submit]
feedback:
  moment: end_of_activity
  shows:
    - pass_or_fail
    - which_rule_failed_if_any
    - gold_rewrite_side_by_side
result:
  type: pass_fail
  pass_rule: >
    Rewrite is 3 sentences or fewer AND every required_facts keyword appears
    AND no banned_phrase appears.
  instructions_for_scorer: >
    Three deterministic checks:
    1. Count sentences (split on . ! ?). 3 or fewer passes this check; else fail.
    2. For each required_facts keyword, search the rewrite case-insensitively. Any missing = fail.
    3. For each banned_phrase, search the rewrite case-insensitively. Any present = fail.
    All three checks pass = student passes. Audience fit and clarity are debrief topics, not scored.
student_view: >
  Source note in a read-only panel on top with a subtle background;
  compose box below as the visual focus; a small checklist on the right
  showing the three pass rules (sentence count, required facts, banned phrases)
  with live deterministic hints as the student types.
teacher_view: >
  Class pass rate per note; flag any student who failed two notes in a row
  so the teacher can pull them aside during the debrief.
accessibility:
  - Standard textarea — screen-reader friendly out of the box
  - Font size 16px minimum; high-contrast mode honored
  - No time cutoff — soft timer only, no anxiety pressure
success_criteria: Student passes 2 of 3 notes within one class period.
tech_stack:
  base: html+css+js
  libraries:
    - name: Tailwind CSS (Play CDN)
      cdn_url: https://cdn.tailwindcss.com
      purpose: Three-panel layout (source, compose, live checklist)
    - name: Alpine.js
      cdn_url: https://unpkg.com/alpinejs@3.x.x/dist/cdn.min.js
      purpose: Submit state, gold-rewrite unlock, live checklist reactivity
ai_required: []
visual_notes: >
  Writing-first visual weight; source note de-emphasized (muted background,
  smaller body); compose box is the largest element; the three-rule checklist
  on the right flips from grey to green as each rule is satisfied;
  gold-rewrite reveal appears below the compose box in a two-column diff
  with additions in cyan and subtractions in muted strike-through.
```

**Instructions to student:** You will see a 6-sentence chart note that is wordy, vague, or both. Rewrite it in 3 sentences or fewer. Every patient fact in the original must survive the rewrite; do not use any of the vague phrases the checklist flags as banned. You will not see the model answer until you submit — commit before comparing.

**Teacher notes:** The gold rewrite is one of many valid answers — say so in the debrief so students stop hunting for a single "right" phrasing. Watch the class pass rate: if most students fail the sentence-count rule they are over-documenting (show 2 examples next session); if most fail the banned-phrase rule they are still using vague verbs (pair with the Do-Not-Use Trap sim next). Audience fit and clarity are deliberately *not* scored — use them as discussion prompts in the debrief.

### Active-vs-Passive Spotter

```yaml
name: Active-vs-Passive Spotter
format: timed_drill
supports:
  primary: 127.403-c1A
  secondary: [127.403-c2D, 127.403-c2F]
duration_min: 8
mode: single_player
equipment: one_device_per_student
learning_goal: >
  In a mixed incident-report paragraph, correctly identify every
  unjustified passive-voice sentence within 30 seconds, then rewrite
  three of them into grammatically correct active voice.
vocab_exercised:
  - Active voice
  - Passive voice
  - Precise language
content_model: passage_set_with_tagged_sentences
content:
  passage_pool_size: 8
  sentences_per_passage: 10
  sentence_tags:
    - active
    - passive_unjustified       # passive for no good reason — should be clicked
    - passive_justified         # action-focused or unknown-actor — should NOT be clicked (the trap)
  distribution_per_passage:
    active: 4
    passive_unjustified: 4
    passive_justified: 2
  passages: []             # populate with 8 entries; each: {id, body_text, sentences: [{index, text, tag, justification_if_justified, required_facts, gold_active_rewrite}]}. PLACEHOLDER.
initial_state:
  phase_1_timer_seconds: 30   # spotting phase
  phase_2_timer_seconds: 120  # rewrite phase
controls:
  phase_1: [click_sentence, submit_selections]
  phase_2: [type, backspace, submit_rewrites]
feedback:
  phase_1_end:
    shows:
      - correct_selections_green
      - missed_passives_red
      - false_positives_amber   # passive_justified incorrectly clicked
  phase_2_end:
    shows:
      - pass_or_fail_per_rewrite
      - gold_active_rewrite_per_sentence
result:
  type: pass_fail
  pass_rule: >
    Phase 1: student clicked at least 2 of the 4 passive_unjustified sentences
      AND wrongly clicked at most one passive_justified sentence.
    Phase 2: at least one of the three rewrites is in active voice
      AND keeps every clinical fact from the original.
    Both phases must pass.
  instructions_for_scorer: >
    Phase 1 (deterministic): count clicked passive_unjustified (need >= 2)
      and count clicked passive_justified (need <= 1).
    Phase 2 (deterministic): regex-scan each rewrite for "(was|were|been|is|are) + past-participle".
      Any hit fails that rewrite. Keyword-match each rewrite against the source sentence's
      required_facts list. One fully passing rewrite out of three clears Phase 2.
    Teacher reviews all three rewrites during debrief regardless of pass/fail.
student_view: >
  Phase 1: incident-report paragraph with each sentence as a clickable
  segment; countdown timer top-right; submit button bottom.
  Phase 2: modal with the 3 selected sentences stacked, one rewrite
  box per sentence, countdown timer top.
teacher_view: >
  Class miss-rate per sentence to surface which "trap" sentences catch
  the most (pedagogically the most valuable ones to debrief);
  class pass rate per passage.
accessibility:
  - Keyboard navigation with Tab across sentences; Space to select
  - High-contrast focus outline on current sentence
  - Screen-reader announces sentence index and selection state
success_criteria: Student passes 2 of 3 passages within one class period.
tech_stack:
  base: html+css+js
  libraries:
    - name: Tailwind CSS (Play CDN)
      cdn_url: https://cdn.tailwindcss.com
      purpose: Layout, typography, the monospace-nod chart-note aesthetic
    - name: Alpine.js
      cdn_url: https://unpkg.com/alpinejs@3.x.x/dist/cdn.min.js
      purpose: Two-phase state machine (spotting -> rewrite -> review), selection state per sentence
ai_required: []
visual_notes: >
  Incident-report visual style — slight monospace nod for the source
  paragraph to cue "this is a clinical document." Selected sentences
  ring in amber. Phase-1 review highlights correct/missed/false-positive
  with check / x / warning icons; no modal dialog boxes. Phase-2 rewrite
  modal is a clean form; the gold rewrite appears beside each student
  rewrite on submit.
```

**Instructions to student:** You will see an incident report for 30 seconds. Click every sentence that is in passive voice *and* could reasonably be active. Skip the sentences where passive voice is actually the right choice — focusing on the action, not the actor (*"the medication was administered at 14:00"*). After you submit your picks, you will have 2 minutes to rewrite your three selections in clear active voice. You only need one rewrite to land (active voice, all the facts preserved) to pass Phase 2.

**Teacher notes:** The "trap" sentences — passive-justified — are the high-value debrief material. Put the class miss-rate on the board after the drill and ask: *why is this sentence okay to leave passive?* The answer is almost always about emphasis (the action matters more than the actor) or an unknown actor. That's the judgment skill the standard actually requires; spotting unjustified passives is just the warm-up.
