## Learning objective

Students will be able to demonstrate core leadership skills — setting SMART goals, building and supporting a team, delegating effectively, giving and receiving feedback, and holding self and others accountable — in structured classroom and simulated-clinical exercises.

## Content stub

- **Performance standard, not knowledge standard:** c4A asked students to identify traits; c4B asks them to demonstrate the skills — expect observed practice, role-plays, and rubrics
- **Goal-setting — SMART goals:** Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound; for both personal development and team objectives; example: "by end of rotation, I will correctly use SBAR on three supervised handoffs" beats "I'll communicate better"
- **Team building — the behaviors that build a team:**
  - *Brief* at start of shift or project — shared mental model of goals and roles
  - *Huddle* mid-shift when something shifts — quick regroup
  - *Debrief* after events — what went well, what didn't, what to change
  - Trust-building through reliability and follow-through
  - Celebrating contributions, not just outcomes
- **Delegation — the "five rights" (nursing) adapted for healthcare teams:** right *task*, right *person* (scope and competency), right *circumstance*, right *direction/communication*, right *supervision/evaluation*
- **Giving feedback — SBI and similar frameworks:** *Situation* (where/when), *Behavior* (what specifically), *Impact* (what effect it had); feedback about behavior, not character; timely, private when corrective
- **Receiving feedback:** listen without defending, ask clarifying questions, thank the giver, change behavior — assume feedback is for patient or team benefit
- **Accountability — self and team:** owning outcomes, including team failures; following up on commitments; addressing performance issues directly, not through gossip
- **Speak-up behavior under authority gradient (from TeamSTEPPS):** CUS ("I'm Concerned, I'm Uncomfortable, this is a Safety issue"); two-challenge rule if first concern is dismissed
- **Leading yourself first:** the demonstrated skills of leadership start with leading yourself — preparation, punctuality, emotional regulation, asking for help — before they scale to leading others

## Vocabulary

- **SMART goal** — Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound; the framework used for personal and team goal-setting.
- **Brief** — TeamSTEPPS start-of-shift team-building tool to establish a shared mental model of goals and roles.
- **Huddle** — TeamSTEPPS mid-shift regroup when something shifts; a 2–3 minute team-building behavior.
- **Debrief** — TeamSTEPPS post-event review identifying what went well, what didn't, and what to change.
- **Five Rights of Delegation** — NCSBN framework: right task, right person, right circumstance, right direction/communication, right supervision/evaluation.
- **SBI feedback** — Situation, Behavior, Impact; a structured format for giving feedback about behavior (not character), timely and private when corrective.
- **CUS language** — TeamSTEPPS safety-escalation script used to speak up under authority gradient: "I'm Concerned, I'm Uncomfortable, this is a Safety issue."
- **Two-challenge rule** — TeamSTEPPS norm obligating a team member to raise a safety concern twice if the first is dismissed.
- **Accountability** — owning outcomes and following up on commitments; the demonstrated leadership skill of addressing performance issues directly, not through gossip.

## Assessment stub

- SMART goal practical: write three SMART goals — one for personal learning this semester, one for a group project, one for a simulated patient-care improvement; peer and instructor score each on whether it meets all five SMART criteria
- Brief/huddle/debrief simulation: in a group of 4, lead a 3-minute shift brief, a 2-minute mid-shift huddle, and a 5-minute debrief for a simulated clinical scenario; observer rubric scores structure, inclusion of all team members, and action items named
- Delegation drill: given 8 clinical tasks and a simulated team (RN, CNA, MA, housekeeping), delegate each task with the five rights named aloud; peer evaluates which delegations were correct and why
- Feedback role-plays (pair exercise): one student gives corrective feedback to a peer using SBI about a simulated performance issue; the other practices receiving it without defensiveness; observer scores both sides
- Speak-up practical: in a simulated scenario where a more senior team member gives an unsafe instruction, use CUS and the two-challenge rule to raise the concern; observer scores clarity, escalation, and documentation

## References

- **AHRQ TeamSTEPPS 3.0 — Leadership, Communication, and Mutual Support modules** — source of brief/huddle/debrief, CUS, and two-challenge rule protocols.
- **Doran, G. T. (1981) *There's a SMART way to write management's goals and objectives* — Management Review** — original source for the SMART framework.
- **National Council of State Boards of Nursing (NCSBN) *Five Rights of Delegation*** — the authoritative US framework for delegation in nursing and by extension healthcare teams.
- **Stone, Patton, and Heen, *Difficult Conversations* / *Thanks for the Feedback*** — widely adopted frameworks for giving and receiving feedback.
- **Kouzes & Posner, *The Leadership Challenge*** — behavioral framework for the skills listed above.
- **AONL Nurse Leader Competencies** — consensus leadership-skill set for US healthcare.
- **Project Oxygen (Google) leadership research** — evidence base for which specific leader behaviors correlate with team performance (applicable beyond tech).
