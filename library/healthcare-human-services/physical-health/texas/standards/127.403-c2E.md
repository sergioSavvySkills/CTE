## Learning objective

Students will be able to plan and prepare an oral presentation on a health-science topic — selecting content for a named audience, storyboarding before slide-building, and rehearsing aloud — so the message is delivered clearly within a time limit.

## Content stub

- **Plan before you build:** name the audience, the single message you want them to leave with, the time limit, and what decision or action you want from them
- **Storyboard before slides:** on paper or index cards, sketch the message flow end-to-end; only then choose which cards become slides
- **Slide discipline:** slides support the speaker — one idea per slide, minimal text, honest visuals; the audience should not be reading and listening at the same time
- **Rehearse aloud, not silently:** timing, stumble points, and pacing only surface when you speak the words; rehearse once without slides to confirm the message survives on its own
- **Clinical presentation formats students will encounter:** grand rounds, shift brief / huddle, in-service education, patient-education talk, journal-club summary — each has its own time budget and conventions
- **Manage nerves:** scripting the first 30 seconds, controlled breathing, knowing the opening sentence cold

## Vocabulary

- **Storyboard** — a paper or index-card sketch of the presentation's message flow end-to-end, built before any slide is created.
- **Slide discipline** — one idea per slide, minimal text, honest visuals so the audience is not reading and listening simultaneously.
- **Brief (TeamSTEPPS)** — a short start-of-shift or start-of-case presentation naming goals, roles, and anticipated issues (3–5 min). *(also in c4B, c4C)*
- **Huddle (TeamSTEPPS)** — a 2–3 minute mid-shift regroup to realign the team when conditions change. *(also in c4B, c4C)*
- **Debrief (TeamSTEPPS)** — a post-event structured review of what went well, what didn't, and what to change (5–10 min). *(also in c4B, c4C)*
- **Grand rounds** — a formal hospital teaching presentation format; one of the clinical oral-presentation conventions students will encounter.
- **In-service** — a brief staff-education presentation delivered to colleagues on a unit or department.
- **Rehearse aloud** — speaking the presentation out loud (not silently) to surface timing, stumble points, and pacing before delivery.

## Assessment stub

- Plan-first exercise: submit a written presentation plan (audience, message, time limit, outline, slide list) before any slides are allowed to be built; instructor feedback on the plan before rehearsal
- Presentation practical: deliver a 5-minute oral presentation on a health topic; observer rubric scores planning, structure, slide discipline, precise language, pacing, and eye contact
- Shift-brief simulation: deliver a 60-second clinical "brief" that would open a shift; peer paraphrases back to verify the message landed

## Activities

- **Slide-Timer Showdown** — student records a 90-second pitch to webcam. App measures only: words per minute, filler-word count ("um", "like", "so"), pauses longer than 2 s, hedging phrases. No human judgment — pure instrumentation. Replay with the waveform so the student sees their own vocal tics.
  → *metacognition, procedural fluency*

- **Audience Reshape** — same 3-minute case presentation delivered three ways: to a peer clinician, to the patient, to the hospital board. Rubric flags jargon to the patient, hedging to the board, and oversimplification to the peer. Forces explicit audience-switching.
  → *transfer, empathy*

- **Visual Density Audit** — student uploads or picks a slide deck. App scores each slide on word count, font size, and information density, and produces a heatmap showing which slides the audience will stop reading. Rewrite the worst two against a density rubric.
  → *metacognition, analysis*

## References

- **TeamSTEPPS 3.0 (AHRQ) — Brief, Huddle, Debrief tools** — defines the short structured-presentation formats used in real clinical work (shift brief, mid-shift huddle, post-event debrief).
- **SBAR** — the most common clinical oral-communication structure; reinforces planning ahead of speaking.
- **CDC Clear Communication Index** — evidence-based criteria for health communication that apply equally to oral and written products.
- **Hospital grand rounds / in-service presentation conventions** — standard time budgets and structures students will encounter in clinical rotations.

## Activity specs

### Slide-Timer Showdown

```yaml
name: Slide-Timer Showdown
format: build_and_critique
supports:
  primary: 127.403-c2E
  secondary: [127.403-c1A]
duration_min: 12
mode: single_player
equipment: one_device_per_student
learning_goal: >
  Deliver a 90-second pitch to webcam and hit four target metrics
  (130-160 wpm, <= 3 filler words, <= 2 pauses longer than 2 s,
  <= 2 hedging phrases) on 2 of 3 attempts.
vocab_exercised:
  - Storyboard
  - Slide discipline
  - Rehearse aloud
  - Brief (TeamSTEPPS)
content_model: speech_capture_with_metrics
content:
  prompt_pool_size: 6
  each_prompt_provides:
    - prompt_text                # e.g., "explain SBAR to a peer in 90s"
    - optional_3_slide_deck      # rendered as static images
    - target_metrics:
        wpm_min: 130
        wpm_max: 160
        max_filler_words: 3
        max_long_pauses: 2
        max_hedging_phrases: 2
initial_state:
  prompt_visible: true
  webcam_consent_required: true
  timer_seconds: 90
  attempt_count: 0
controls:
  - start_recording
  - stop_recording
  - replay
  - view_metrics
  - retry
feedback:
  moment: end_of_attempt
  shows:
    - words_per_minute
    - filler_word_count           # transcript-based: "um", "uh", "like", "so", "you know"
    - long_pause_count            # silences > 2 s
    - hedging_phrase_count        # "kind of", "sort of", "I think", "maybe"
    - waveform_with_filler_markers
scoring:
  metrics_in_target:
    wpm_in_range: 0_or_1
    fillers_under_cap: 0_or_1
    pauses_under_cap: 0_or_1
    hedges_under_cap: 0_or_1
  per_attempt_max: 4
  mastery_threshold: 4
  notes: 2 of 3 attempts must score 4/4
win_condition:
  type: 3_attempts_recorded
  mastery: 2 attempts at 4/4
reset:
  clears: [recording, metrics, attempt_count]
  preserves: [prompt, target_metrics]
student_view: >
  Prompt card at the top, optional slide deck rendered as 3 static
  thumbnails, big record button center-stage, webcam preview bottom-right,
  large countdown timer top. Post-attempt: waveform with filler markers
  is the dominant feedback artifact, metrics summary below.
teacher_view: >
  Class-aggregate distributions of wpm, filler count, pause count;
  opt-in sharing of any individual recording for whole-class review
  (with student consent). Most students cluster on filler count first;
  that's where to focus the debrief.
accessibility:
  - Audio-only mode (no webcam required); captioning on playback
  - All metrics shown numerically alongside any visual indicator
  - Record button activatable by Space; Stop by Esc
success_criteria: 2 of 3 attempts at 4/4 within one class period.
tech_stack:
  base: html+css+js
  libraries:
    - name: Tailwind CSS (Play CDN)
      cdn_url: https://cdn.tailwindcss.com
      purpose: Stage-light layout, big timer, button sizing
    - name: Alpine.js
      cdn_url: https://unpkg.com/alpinejs@3.x.x/dist/cdn.min.js
      purpose: Recording state machine, attempt tracking, metrics reveal
  notes: Web Speech API (browser-native) handles transcription; canvas renders the waveform. No external library needed for either.
ai_required:
  - category: stt
    reason: "Speech-to-text on the 90-second webcam recording is the basis for every downstream metric (words per minute, filler words, long pauses, hedging phrases)."
    fallback: "Browser Web Speech API (backed by cloud STT) is the default and acceptable; if unavailable, the sim degrades to self-measurement via audio playback with the student counting their own tics."
visual_notes: >
  Stage-light aesthetic — high-contrast prompt card centered like a
  cue card, big timer top, webcam preview small (the speaker is the
  performer, not the focus). Waveform on review uses a single accent
  color; filler markers are small dots above the wave, not labels
  cluttering it.
```

**Instructions to student:** You have 90 seconds to deliver the pitch. Record yourself; the system measures four things only — your speaking pace, your filler-word count, your long pauses, your hedging phrases. No human is grading. Watch the waveform on replay to see exactly where each filler hit. Three attempts; you need two clean ones to earn mastery.

**Teacher notes:** Filler count is almost always the metric students miss first. Project the class waveform aggregate and ask: *what filler shows up most? when?* It's almost always at sentence boundaries — students fill silence rather than letting it land. The fix is rehearse-aloud, which the standard explicitly names. Pair this drill with a 2-minute silent-pause exercise.

### Audience Reshape

```yaml
name: Audience Reshape
format: build_and_critique
supports:
  primary: 127.403-c2E
  secondary: [127.403-c1A]
duration_min: 15
mode: single_player
equipment: one_device_per_student
learning_goal: >
  Deliver the same 3-minute case presentation three times — to a peer
  clinician, to the patient, and to the hospital board — with each
  version meeting its audience-specific rubric on jargon density,
  hedging tolerance, and complexity floor at >= 4/5 per audience.
vocab_exercised:
  - Storyboard
  - Slide discipline
  - In-service
  - Grand rounds
content_model: audience_switch_task
content:
  case_pool_size: 5
  audiences:
    - name: peer_clinician
      jargon_ok: true
      hedge_max: 4
      complexity_floor: high
    - name: patient
      jargon_ok: false
      hedge_max: 2
      complexity_floor: low
    - name: hospital_board
      jargon_ok: light
      hedge_max: 1
      complexity_floor: medium
  each_case_provides:
    - shared_facts_pool
    - per_audience_target_metrics
    - example_phrases_to_avoid_per_audience
initial_state:
  case_visible: true
  audience_tabs: [peer_clinician, patient, hospital_board]
  three_unrecorded_slots: true
controls:
  - select_audience_tab
  - start_recording
  - stop_recording
  - retry_per_audience
  - view_metrics
feedback:
  moment: per_attempt
  shows:
    - jargon_density                  # auto: medical-term dictionary match per minute
    - hedge_density                   # auto: phrase match
    - sentence_complexity             # auto-heuristic: average sentence length, clause count
    - audience_specific_violations    # e.g., "jargon to patient" or "hedging to board"
scoring:
  per_audience: 0-5 (rubric)
  total_max: 15
  mastery_threshold: 12
win_condition:
  type: all_three_recorded_at_least_once
  mastery: total >= 12/15
reset:
  clears: [recordings, metrics]
  preserves: [case, audience_tabs]
student_view: >
  Case panel on the left with the shared facts; three audience tabs
  across the top; recorder at the bottom with target-metric chips
  visible per selected audience. Post-attempt metrics replace the
  recorder until the student starts the next attempt.
teacher_view: >
  Per-student matrix of which audience each handled best/worst.
  Most cohorts handle the peer audience easily, struggle with the
  patient audience (jargon leaks), and find the board audience
  hardest (hedging is the consistent failure mode). That diagnostic
  drives the debrief.
accessibility:
  - Audio-only mode supported
  - Transcripts shown for review and downloadable
  - All target-metric chips include numeric values, not just colors
success_criteria: 12/15 across the three audiences within one class period.
tech_stack:
  base: html+css+js
  libraries:
    - name: Tailwind CSS (Play CDN)
      cdn_url: https://cdn.tailwindcss.com
      purpose: Tab layout, target-metric chips, recorder UI
    - name: Alpine.js
      cdn_url: https://unpkg.com/alpinejs@3.x.x/dist/cdn.min.js
      purpose: Audience-tab state, per-audience recording slots, metric reveal
  notes: Web Speech API for transcription; metric checks run client-side against built-in jargon and hedge dictionaries.
ai_required:
  - category: stt
    reason: "All three audience-specific metric passes (jargon density, hedge density, sentence complexity) depend on a transcript of each recording."
    fallback: "Same as Slide-Timer Showdown — Web Speech API is the default, graceful degrade to self-measurement if unavailable."
visual_notes: >
  Tab-based audience switcher, with each tab subtly tinted (peer = slate,
  patient = warm neutral, board = deep slate) to reinforce audience
  identity. Target-metric chips show the threshold and the student's
  current value side-by-side, not as a binary pass/fail. No overlay
  during recording — the screen stays calm.
```

**Instructions to student:** Same case, three times, three audiences. Pick the audience tab, then deliver the 3-minute version for that audience. The system measures jargon density, hedging, and sentence complexity against the target for each audience. The peer cut is easiest — the patient cut is where jargon leaks; the board cut is where hedging shows up. You only have to record each audience once to finish; aim for 12/15 across all three.

**Teacher notes:** The diagnostic value is the per-student matrix. Students who score low on the patient audience are usually jargon-leakers — explicitly assign the "Plain Language" sub-skill before the next attempt. Students low on the board audience are hedgers — pair with the Slide-Timer Showdown drill (which catches hedging). The peer audience is rarely the problem; if it is, the student doesn't know the case material yet.

### Visual Density Audit

```yaml
name: Visual Density Audit
format: build_and_critique
supports:
  primary: 127.403-c2E
  secondary: [127.403-c2B]
duration_min: 10
mode: single_player
equipment: one_device_per_student
learning_goal: >
  Audit a 6-slide deck against three rules (word count <= 25 per slide,
  font size >= 24 pt, single-idea density), then rewrite the two worst
  slides to score in the green zone on all three dimensions, total
  >= 24/30.
vocab_exercised:
  - Slide discipline
  - Storyboard
content_model: deck_review_task
content:
  deck_pool_size: 8
  slides_per_deck: 6
  each_slide_provides:
    - rendered_text
    - word_count
    - font_size_px
    - density_score                   # 1-5; auto-computed (word count + idea count)
    - gold_rewrite                    # for the 2 worst slides only
initial_state:
  deck_loaded: true
  slide_thumbnails_visible: true
  density_heatmap_rendered: true
controls:
  - click_slide_to_inspect
  - edit_slide_text
  - edit_font_size
  - submit_two_rewrites
feedback:
  moment: real_time
  shows:
    - density heatmap updates as student edits
    - rubric chips per slide (word count, font size, density) flip red -> amber -> green
    - on submit, gold rewrite shown side-by-side for each rewritten slide
scoring:
  per_rewrite_max: 15            # 5 per dimension x 3 dimensions
  total_max: 30
  mastery_threshold: 24
win_condition:
  type: two_rewrites_submitted
  mastery: total >= 24/30
reset:
  clears: [edits, heatmap_state]
  preserves: [deck_pool]
student_view: >
  Deck thumbnails across the top with red / amber / green tint plus a
  text label; large slide editor in the center with a font-size slider;
  rubric chips on the right side. The two worst slides are flagged
  with a "rewrite this" badge.
teacher_view: >
  Per-deck pre/post density score; common student mistakes (most often:
  cutting words but leaving font tiny, or splitting one bad slide into
  two equally bad ones).
accessibility:
  - Keyboard slide nav (left / right arrows)
  - Density indicator includes numeric value, not color alone
  - Font slider has tick marks at standard sizes (18, 24, 32, 40)
success_criteria: Total >= 24/30 within one class period.
tech_stack:
  base: html+css+js
  libraries:
    - name: Tailwind CSS (Play CDN)
      cdn_url: https://cdn.tailwindcss.com
      purpose: Thumbnail strip, editor pane, rubric chips
    - name: Alpine.js
      cdn_url: https://unpkg.com/alpinejs@3.x.x/dist/cdn.min.js
      purpose: Slide selection, edit state, real-time rubric updates
ai_required: []
visual_notes: >
  Thumbnails render as actual mini-slides with the live text and font
  size — students see their edits update the thumbnail in real time.
  Heatmap tint is subtle (red = soft pink, green = muted sage), with
  text labels for accessibility. Gold rewrite reveal appears below the
  editor, not in a modal.
```

**Instructions to student:** Open the deck. The two worst slides are flagged. Edit them — cut words, raise the font size, split or merge ideas — until all three rubric chips turn green. The thumbnail updates as you type. Submit when both flagged slides are in the green zone. Goal: 24/30.

**Teacher notes:** The two most common failure modes are (1) cutting words but leaving the font at 12 pt (because the text "fits") and (2) splitting one overstuffed slide into two equally overstuffed slides. Both surface in the teacher view. The fix for both is the same — *one idea per slide* — and it pairs well with a quick whiteboard exercise: write a slide title, then state the one idea aloud in a single sentence.
