## Learning objective

Students will be able to formulate spoken and written responses in clinical contexts using precise language — specific verbs, measurable values, correct medical terminology, and answers that match the question asked.

## Content stub

- **Precision is a distinct requirement from clarity and conciseness** (c1A) — this standard is specifically about the accuracy and specificity of the words chosen
- **Specific verbs over vague ones:** *administered* vs. *gave*, *palpated* vs. *felt*, *ambulated 50 feet* vs. *walked a bit*
- **Measurable values over approximations:** *2 cm laceration* vs. *small cut*, *BP 160/95* vs. *blood pressure is up*, *voided 300 mL* vs. *peed*
- **Correct medical terminology** (crosswalks with §127.403(c)(3)(D)) — using the right word, used correctly; avoid "sounds like" errors
- **Answering the question asked:** formulating a response means hearing the question first; a precise answer to a different question is still wrong
- **Patient-appropriate precision:** with patients and families, pair the precise term with a plain-language explanation — precision is not a license for jargon
- **Why precision is patient-safety-critical:** verbal orders, handoffs, and telephone communication depend on unambiguous language; this is why the Joint Commission requires read-back and ISMP publishes the "Do Not Use" list

## Vocabulary

- **Precise language** — specific verbs and measurable values ("administered 2 mg," "palpated a 2 cm mass") used instead of vague approximations. *(also in c1A)*
- **Specific verb** — an exact action word ("administered," "palpated," "ambulated") used in place of a general one ("gave," "felt," "walked").
- **Measurable value** — a quantified observation (BP 160/95, voided 300 mL, 2 cm laceration) used in place of qualitative approximation.
- **Medical terminology** — the controlled clinical vocabulary used correctly and in the right context; reinforces §127.403(c)(3)(D).
- **Verbal order** — a spoken medication or treatment order; requires read-back under Joint Commission NPSG 02.03.01 because of precision risk.
- **"Do Not Use" list** — ISMP/Joint Commission prohibited abbreviations (e.g., "U" for units, trailing zeros) that cause medication errors. *(also in c1A, c3D)*
- **Closed-loop / check-back** — TeamSTEPPS pairing where the receiver repeats the message precisely to confirm accurate receipt. *(also in c3A)*
- **SNOMED CT** — a standardized clinical terminology that defines "precise" for controlled-vocabulary clinical documentation.

## Assessment stub

- Rewrite drill: 10 vague healthcare statements converted into precise ones; student must identify what was vague (verb, value, or term) and fix it
- Question-response practical: paired exercise — partner asks 5 clinical questions, student formulates a precise response; observer scores precision and whether the response answered the question asked
- Shadow-coach observation: during a 2-minute peer interview about a patient scenario, an observer flags every vague or approximate phrase; student revises live

## Activities

- **Precision Rewriter** — a vague workplace message appears: *"The patient didn't feel great this morning."* Rewrite with precise language in under 20 seconds. Auto-checker flags words like "great," "a little," "kind of"; rewards specific verbs and measurable values.
  → *application, pattern recognition*

- **Do-Not-Use Trap** — student writes 10 medication orders in 5 minutes with ISMP rules enforced live. Type `U` for units, get a red underline. Type `.5 mg` instead of `0.5 mg`, dock the score. No lookup allowed. Auto-feedback only.
  → *procedural fluency, metacognition*

- **Verb Bank** — vague verbs on one side ("did," "gave," "managed"), precise verbs on the other ("administered," "titrated," "discontinued," "escalated"). Context sentences fly by at 5 seconds each. Pick the precise replacement before the sentence locks. Wrong picks count against; unused precise verbs carry a penalty.
  → *application, procedural fluency*

## References

- **ISMP (Institute for Safe Medication Practices) "List of Error-Prone Abbreviations, Symbols, and Dose Designations"** — precision standards for clinical communication, directly tied to patient-safety events.
- **Joint Commission "Do Not Use" list and NPSG 02.03.01 (read-back for verbal orders)** — accreditation-backed precision requirements.
- **SNOMED CT and standardized clinical terminologies** — controlled medical vocabularies that define "precise" in clinical documentation.
- **AMA Manual of Style (11th ed.)** — conventions for medical terminology, units, and numerical precision.
- **AHRQ TeamSTEPPS "check-back" (closed-loop communication)** — pairs with precision: the sender says it precisely, the receiver confirms it was heard precisely.

### Precision Rewriter

```yaml
name: Precision Rewriter
format: timed_drill
supports:
  primary: 127.403-c2F
  secondary: [127.403-c1A, 127.403-c2D]
duration_min: 10
mode: single_player
equipment: one_device_per_student
learning_goal: >
  Across 10 vague clinical statements at 20 seconds each, produce
  rewrites that remove every banned vague phrase AND introduce at
  least one specific verb or measurable value, scoring >= 8/10.
vocab_exercised:
  - Precise language
  - Specific verb
  - Measurable value
  - Medical terminology
content_model: rewrite_drill_pool
content:
  statement_pool_size: 30
  statements_per_session: 10
  each_statement_provides:
    - source_text
    - banned_phrases             # vague qualifiers ("a little," "kind of," "great")
    - required_replacements      # at least one specific verb OR measurable value
    - gold_rewrites              # multiple acceptable answers
initial_state:
  statement_index: 0
  timer_seconds_per_statement: 20
  score: 0
controls:
  - type
  - submit
  - skip
feedback:
  moment: immediate
  on_correct:
    - green border flash; gold rewrite shown for 2 s
  on_wrong:
    - red border flash; specific feedback ("still vague: 'a little'") shown for 2 s
  on_timeout:
    - amber flash; gold rewrite shown for 2 s; no penalty
scoring:
  correct: +1
  wrong: 0
  timeout: 0
  mastery_threshold: 8
win_condition:
  type: 10_statements_completed
  mastery: score >= 8/10
reset:
  clears: [score, current_statement, history]
  preserves: [statement_pool]
student_view: >
  Source statement large at the top; compose box below with
  placeholder text reminding the student to use specific verbs and
  measurable values; timer bar across the top; score bottom-left;
  prompt counter bottom-right.
teacher_view: >
  Class heatmap of which banned phrases survive most often (the
  productive debrief targets); per-student replacement habits
  (verbs vs. values — most students lean one direction).
accessibility:
  - Keyboard-first; submit on Enter, skip on Esc
  - Border-flash feedback only — never a full-screen color wash
  - Font size 24 px minimum for source and compose
success_criteria: Student reaches 8/10 within one class period.
tech_stack:
  base: html+css+js
  libraries:
    - name: Tailwind CSS (Play CDN)
      cdn_url: https://cdn.tailwindcss.com
      purpose: Drill layout, large type, timer bar
    - name: Alpine.js
      cdn_url: https://unpkg.com/alpinejs@3.x.x/dist/cdn.min.js
      purpose: Drill state machine, timer, score, banned-phrase auto-detection
ai_required:
  - category: text_rubric_scoring
    reason: "Borderline — banned-phrase regex reliably catches 'great / a little / kind of', but confirming that the student's replacement is actually a precise verb or measurable value needs semantic check on creative rewrites."
    fallback: "Dictionary match against a precise-verb list + regex for numeric + unit patterns covers roughly 85%; edge-case rewrites surface for instructor review."
    dimensions: [replacement_quality]
    deterministic_dimensions: [banned_phrase_removed, replacement_present]
    prompt_template: text-rubric-v1
    output_schema:
      scores: map<dimension, int 0-5>
      diagnostics: map<dimension, string one-line>
    model: claude-haiku-4-5
    max_tokens: 200
    content_file: activities/content/c2F-precision-rewriter.yaml
visual_notes: >
  Drill aesthetic — source statement is the visual anchor, compose box
  is large and uncluttered. Border flash is a 2 px ring (soft, fast).
  Gold rewrites on feedback appear small and italic below the compose
  box, not in a modal.
```

**Instructions to student:** You'll see 10 vague clinical statements. You have 20 seconds each. Rewrite to remove the vague words AND add at least one specific verb or measurable value. Skip if you're stuck — no penalty.

**Teacher notes:** The "banned phrases that survive" report is the debrief gold. Almost every cohort lets the same handful through (*"a little"*, *"kind of"*, *"sort of"*, *"a bit"*) — those words feel professional but mean nothing. Project the heatmap and ask: *what objective observation could replace each of these?* The substitutions are usually obvious once a student is forced to name them aloud.

### Do-Not-Use Trap

```yaml
name: Do-Not-Use Trap
format: timed_drill
supports:
  primary: 127.403-c2F
  secondary: [127.403-c2A, 127.403-c1A]
duration_min: 8
mode: single_player
equipment: one_device_per_student
learning_goal: >
  Write 10 medication orders in 5 minutes with all ISMP "Do Not Use"
  rules enforced live; achieve >= 9/10 with no rule violations
  carrying through to submission.
vocab_exercised:
  - "Do Not Use" list
  - ISMP (Institute for Safe Medication Practices)
  - Verbal order
content_model: order_writing_drill
content:
  prompt_pool_size: 20
  prompts_per_session: 10
  each_prompt_provides:
    - prompt_text                # e.g., "Write the order: 5 mg of metoprolol PO every 6 hours"
    - expected_format            # canonical safe format
    - common_violation_traps     # what students typically mistype
violation_categories:
  - mg_mcg_confusion
  - missing_leading_zero
  - unsafe_trailing_zero
  - do_not_use_abbreviation     # U, QD, IU, q.d.
initial_state:
  prompt_index: 0
  timer_seconds_per_prompt: 30
  score: 0
controls:
  - type
  - submit
  - scratch_revise
feedback:
  moment: live_during_typing
  shows:
    - violating_tokens get a red underline within 200 ms of being typed
    - hover over an underlined token shows the rule name
    - submit blocked until all underlines are cleared
scoring:
  correct_submission_no_live_violations: +1
  correct_submission_after_visible_correction: +1   # the rule is "no violations at submit"
  wrong_or_unsubmitted: 0
  mastery_threshold: 9
win_condition:
  type: 10_orders_submitted
  mastery: score >= 9/10
reset:
  clears: [score, current_prompt, history]
  preserves: [prompt_pool]
student_view: >
  Prompt large at top; single-line order input below with character-by-
  character live-underline rendering as the student types; submit
  button bottom; timer bar top; score bottom-left.
teacher_view: >
  Class heatmap of violation patterns (mg/mcg confusion vs. leading
  zero vs. abbreviation) — surfaces which rule the class internalizes
  last, which is consistently the leading-zero rule.
accessibility:
  - Visible focus ring; screen reader announces violations as they appear
  - Font size 24 px minimum
  - No strobe; underlines fade in over 100 ms
success_criteria: Student reaches 9/10 within one class period.
tech_stack:
  base: html+css+js
  libraries:
    - name: Tailwind CSS (Play CDN)
      cdn_url: https://cdn.tailwindcss.com
      purpose: Drill layout, large type, timer bar
    - name: Alpine.js
      cdn_url: https://unpkg.com/alpinejs@3.x.x/dist/cdn.min.js
      purpose: Live regex-based violation detection, submit gating, drill state
ai_required: []
visual_notes: >
  Writing-first layout — order input is the focal element. Violation
  underlines are red but soft (not bright). Hover-tooltip for the rule
  name uses a small, calm popover, not a modal. The submit button
  changes from grey to a clear accent only when no violations remain.
```

**Instructions to student:** You'll see 10 medication orders to write in 5 minutes. As you type, the system underlines anything that violates an ISMP "Do Not Use" rule — `U` for units, `.5 mg` instead of `0.5 mg`, `5.0 mg`, `QD`. Hover any underline to see the rule name. The submit button stays disabled until all underlines clear. You can revise as much as you want before submit.

**Teacher notes:** The violation heatmap is unmistakable. Most cohorts internalize mg/mcg first, abbreviations second, leading-zero last. If leading-zero is still tripping the class after this drill, build a short verbal call-and-response into the next session: *"point five milligrams"* always becomes *"zero point five milligrams"* on the page. The verbalization habit transfers.

### Verb Bank

```yaml
name: Verb Bank
format: timed_drill
supports:
  primary: 127.403-c2F
  secondary: [127.403-c1A]
duration_min: 8
mode: single_player
equipment: one_device_per_student
learning_goal: >
  Across 20 context sentences at 5 seconds each, replace the vague
  verb with the precise verb from a side panel; score >= 18/20
  with no precise verb left unused at the end.
vocab_exercised:
  - Specific verb
  - Precise language
  - Medical terminology
content_model: verb_swap_drill
content:
  sentence_pool_size: 50
  sentences_per_session: 20
  precise_verbs_per_session: 8        # side-panel pool
  each_sentence_provides:
    - source_sentence_with_vague_verb_highlighted
    - vague_verb
    - precise_verb_options              # 2-3 acceptable
    - gold_choice                       # the most precise of the options
initial_state:
  sentence_index: 0
  side_panel_verbs_visible: true
  timer_seconds_per_sentence: 5
  score: 0
controls:
  - click_precise_verb_chip
  - keyboard_shortcuts: [1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8]
  - skip
feedback:
  moment: immediate
  on_correct:
    - green flash; verb chip slides off the panel (used)
  on_wrong:
    - red flash; gold choice shown for 1 s; verb chip stays available
  on_timeout:
    - amber flash; auto-advance; no chip used
scoring:
  correct: +1
  wrong: 0
  timeout: 0
  unused_precise_verb_at_end: -0.5 each
  mastery_threshold: 18
win_condition:
  type: 20_sentences_completed
  mastery: net score >= 18
reset:
  clears: [score, sentence_index, used_verbs]
  preserves: [sentence_pool, verb_pool]
student_view: >
  Source sentence large in the center with the vague verb highlighted;
  side panel of precise-verb chips on the right (chips fade after use);
  timer bar top; score bottom-left; sentence counter bottom-right.
teacher_view: >
  Per-vague-verb miss rate (which vague verbs students fail to swap
  most often); per-precise-verb usage distribution (which precise
  verbs are over- or under-used).
accessibility:
  - Keyboard shortcuts 1-8 mapped to side-panel chips
  - High-contrast highlight on the vague verb in the source sentence
  - Font size 24 px minimum
success_criteria: Student reaches net 18/20 within one class period.
tech_stack:
  base: html+css+js
  libraries:
    - name: Tailwind CSS (Play CDN)
      cdn_url: https://cdn.tailwindcss.com
      purpose: Drill layout, large type, chip styling, timer bar
    - name: Alpine.js
      cdn_url: https://unpkg.com/alpinejs@3.x.x/dist/cdn.min.js
      purpose: Drill state machine, chip used-state, score, end-of-drill unused-verb penalty
ai_required: []
visual_notes: >
  Drill aesthetic — sentence dominates the screen, verb chips are
  small but readable on the right. Used chips slide left and fade
  to grey rather than disappearing entirely (so students see their
  remaining inventory). No celebratory modal at the end; a clean
  score summary with the unused-chip penalty itemized.
```

**Instructions to student:** Each sentence has one vague verb highlighted. Pick the precise verb from the side panel that fits — you have 5 seconds. Use the keyboard shortcuts (1-8) to move faster. The unused-verb penalty matters: if you finish with three precise verbs you never used, that's -1.5 from your score. Aim to use every chip.

**Teacher notes:** The unused-precise-verb penalty is the metacognitive prompt — it forces students to *consider* every chip on the panel rather than gravitating to the same 2-3 they're comfortable with. Look at the under-used verbs in the teacher view; those are the verbs students don't yet trust. Demo each unused verb in a sentence in the next session — a single example fixes the under-use most of the time.
