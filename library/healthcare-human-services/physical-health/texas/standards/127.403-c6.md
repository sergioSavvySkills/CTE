## Learning objective

Students will be able to identify and distinguish the five academic-advancement mechanisms named in the TEKS — certification, licensure, registration, continuing education, and advanced degrees — and describe when and how each is used in healthcare career progression.

## Content stub

- **Five distinct mechanisms, distinguished:**
  - *Certification* — voluntary recognition by a professional body that a practitioner has met defined competency standards (e.g., CPR/BLS, ACLS, PALS, CMA through AAMA, CPT through NPA, CNA through state registry); typically earned by exam, renewed periodically; can be required by employers even when not required by law
  - *Licensure* — legal authority granted by a state government to practice a regulated profession; the state can suspend or revoke it; protected scope of practice (e.g., RN, LVN, MD, DO, PT, PharmD, respiratory therapist); working without a required license is illegal
  - *Registration* — listing on an official roster maintained by a governmental or recognized body (e.g., state CNA registry, Registered Radiologic Technologist via ARRT); usually verifies baseline training/exam completion; often the entry-level credential
  - *Continuing education (CE / CEU)* — required hours of ongoing learning to maintain a license or certification; subject matter and hour counts vary by profession and state; failure to complete = license lapse
  - *Advanced degrees* — formal academic credentials beyond entry-level (ADN→BSN→MSN→DNP; MD/DO; PhD; PharmD; MPH; MHA); unlock advanced scope, faculty roles, leadership roles, and higher pay
- **Relationships among them — a typical healthcare advancement ladder:**
  - High school + state-registered CNA program → *registered* CNA
  - Complete LVN program + pass NCLEX-PN → *licensed* LVN
  - Complete ADN + pass NCLEX-RN → *licensed* RN (BSN optional but increasingly employer-required)
  - *Advanced degree* (MSN) + specialty *certification* (e.g., CCRN, CEN) + ongoing *CE* → advanced practice role
- **Issuing bodies:**
  - *Certifications* — professional associations (AAMA, ARRT, ASCP, AACN, AANP), specialty boards
  - *Licensure* — state licensing boards (Texas BON, Texas Medical Board, Texas Optometry Board, etc.)
  - *Registration* — state registries or national registry bodies (ARRT registers radiologic technologists; state CNA registries list CNAs)
- **Portability and state variation:**
  - *Nurse Licensure Compact* — Texas is a compact state; compact-state licensure lets RNs and LVNs practice in other compact states without re-licensing
  - *Medical-licensure portability* — Interstate Medical Licensure Compact for physicians; Texas participates
  - *Certifications* are generally nationally portable; *licenses* and *registrations* typically are not (unless through a compact)
- **Costs and time for each mechanism:** certification exam fees, license application and renewal fees, CE course costs, advanced-degree tuition — students should know the rough order of magnitude before making decisions

## Vocabulary

- **Certification** — voluntary recognition by a professional body that a practitioner has met defined competency standards (e.g., CPR/BLS, ACLS, CMA); often employer-required even when not legally required. *(also in c8B)*
- **Licensure** — legal authority granted by a state to practice a regulated profession; suspendable or revocable; protected scope. *(also in c2O)*
- **Registration** — listing on an official roster (e.g., state CNA registry, ARRT radiologic-technologist registry); usually verifies baseline training/exam completion.
- **Continuing education (CE / CEU)** — required hours of ongoing learning to maintain a license or certification; lapsed hours = lapsed credential.
- **Advanced degree** — formal academic credential beyond entry-level (BSN, MSN, DNP, MD, PharmD, MPH) that unlocks advanced scope and leadership roles.
- **NCLEX** — the NCSBN-administered licensing exam for RNs (NCLEX-RN) and LVNs (NCLEX-PN).
- **Nurse Licensure Compact (NLC)** — interstate licensure portability for RNs and LVNs; Texas is a compact state. *(also in c5B)*
- **Interstate Medical Licensure Compact** — streamlined multi-state license pathway for physicians; Texas participates.
- **Texas Board of Nursing (BON)** — Texas state licensure issuing body for RNs, LVNs, and APRNs.
- **Credential ladder** — the sequenced path from HS graduation through registration, licensure, certification, and advanced degrees in a chosen profession.

## Assessment stub

- Mechanism match: given 15 healthcare credentials (RN license, CPR BLS, CNA registration, CMA certification, MSN, 24 CE hours, ACLS, DNP, LVN license, phlebotomy certification, radiologic technologist ARRT, Texas BON RN license, pharmacy tech registration, MD license, NP certification), classify each as certification, licensure, registration, continuing education, or advanced degree
- Ladder map: for one chosen healthcare profession, draw the full credential ladder from HS graduation to the highest-level role — label each step with mechanism type, issuing body, typical time, typical cost
- Research short-answer: pick one Texas-specific license, certification, or registration; summarize (1) the issuing body, (2) the entry requirements, (3) the CE or renewal requirements, (4) what happens if you let it lapse; cite your sources
