## Learning objective

Students will be able to identify the five levels of Maslow's Hierarchy of Human Needs and use the framework to prioritize competing patient-care concerns.

## Content stub

- **Physiological needs (base of the pyramid):** air, water, food, elimination, sleep, shelter, temperature regulation — if unmet, nothing above matters; maps directly to the clinical ABCs (airway, breathing, circulation)
- **Safety and security:** physical safety (fall prevention, infection control, violence-free environment), emotional safety, financial security
- **Love and belonging:** relationships, family presence, therapeutic rapport, community; isolation measurably worsens health outcomes
- **Self-esteem:** autonomy, respect, dignity, recognition — easily eroded by hospital gowns, shared rooms, loss of control
- **Self-actualization (top of the pyramid):** realizing potential, meaning, purpose; addressed in palliative care, rehabilitation, mental-health recovery
- **Prioritizing in practice:** lower levels take priority when unmet — don't discuss lifestyle goals with a hypoxic patient; don't address pain before securing airway; Maslow operationalizes "urgent first"

## Assessment stub

- Label-the-pyramid: given a blank Maslow diagram with 10 patient needs listed around it (oxygen, trust in the care team, being addressed by name, pain control, a private room, meaningful conversation), place each at the correct level
- Prioritization drill: given 5 simultaneous concerns from one patient, rank-order them using Maslow and defend the sequence with one-sentence rationales
- Reflection: identify a time a hospitalization (yours or a family member's) felt undignified or isolating; name which Maslow level was unmet and describe one concrete staff behavior that would have helped

## References

- **Maslow, *A Theory of Human Motivation* (1943) and *Motivation and Personality* (1954)** — the original sources for the hierarchy; worth knowing the framework's origin and its limitations as a descriptive (not predictive) model.
- **American Nurses Association (ANA) *Nursing: Scope and Standards of Practice*** — Maslow is explicitly referenced in nursing care-prioritization frameworks and NCLEX-style reasoning.
- **ACLS/BLS and PALS algorithms (American Heart Association)** — operationalize physiological-needs priority via the airway/breathing/circulation (ABC) sequence.
- **Institute for Patient- and Family-Centered Care** — dignity- and belonging-level needs are codified in the patient- and family-centered-care framework adopted by most US hospitals.
