## Learning objective

Students will be able to plan and prepare an oral presentation on a health-science topic — selecting content for a named audience, storyboarding before slide-building, and rehearsing aloud — so the message is delivered clearly within a time limit.

## Content stub

- **Plan before you build:** name the audience, the single message you want them to leave with, the time limit, and what decision or action you want from them
- **Storyboard before slides:** on paper or index cards, sketch the message flow end-to-end; only then choose which cards become slides
- **Slide discipline:** slides support the speaker — one idea per slide, minimal text, honest visuals; the audience should not be reading and listening at the same time
- **Rehearse aloud, not silently:** timing, stumble points, and pacing only surface when you speak the words; rehearse once without slides to confirm the message survives on its own
- **Clinical presentation formats students will encounter:** grand rounds, shift brief / huddle, in-service education, patient-education talk, journal-club summary — each has its own time budget and conventions
- **Manage nerves:** scripting the first 30 seconds, controlled breathing, knowing the opening sentence cold

## Vocabulary

- **Storyboard** — a paper or index-card sketch of the presentation's message flow end-to-end, built before any slide is created.
- **Slide discipline** — one idea per slide, minimal text, honest visuals so the audience is not reading and listening simultaneously.
- **Brief (TeamSTEPPS)** — a short start-of-shift or start-of-case presentation naming goals, roles, and anticipated issues (3–5 min). *(also in c4B, c4C)*
- **Huddle (TeamSTEPPS)** — a 2–3 minute mid-shift regroup to realign the team when conditions change. *(also in c4B, c4C)*
- **Debrief (TeamSTEPPS)** — a post-event structured review of what went well, what didn't, and what to change (5–10 min). *(also in c4B, c4C)*
- **Grand rounds** — a formal hospital teaching presentation format; one of the clinical oral-presentation conventions students will encounter.
- **In-service** — a brief staff-education presentation delivered to colleagues on a unit or department.
- **Rehearse aloud** — speaking the presentation out loud (not silently) to surface timing, stumble points, and pacing before delivery.

## Assessment stub

- Plan-first exercise: submit a written presentation plan (audience, message, time limit, outline, slide list) before any slides are allowed to be built; instructor feedback on the plan before rehearsal
- Presentation practical: deliver a 5-minute oral presentation on a health topic; observer rubric scores planning, structure, slide discipline, precise language, pacing, and eye contact
- Shift-brief simulation: deliver a 60-second clinical "brief" that would open a shift; peer paraphrases back to verify the message landed

## Activities

- **Pitch Script Builder** — student has 6 minutes to write a 90-second pitch script against four delivery-readiness targets: word count 195–240 (a comfortable pace for 90 s of spoken delivery), zero hedging phrases ("kind of", "I think", "maybe"), zero sentence-start fillers ("So,", "Um,"), and no sentence longer than 20 words. Live counters tick up as the student types; the submit button enables only when all four targets are green.
  → *metacognition, procedural fluency*

- **Audience Reshape** — same 3-minute case, written three times for three audiences: peer clinician, patient, hospital board. Each version is scored against its audience-specific rules — jargon is expected for the peer but triggers a red flag for the patient; hedging ("I think", "maybe") is tolerated with the patient but flagged hard on the board; oversimplification to the peer loses points. The three scripts sit side-by-side at the end so the student can see their own audience pivots.
  → *transfer, empathy*

- **Visual Density Audit** — student uploads or picks a slide deck. App scores each slide on word count, font size, and information density, and produces a heatmap showing which slides the audience will stop reading. Rewrite the worst two against a density rubric.
  → *metacognition, analysis*

## References

- **TeamSTEPPS 3.0 (AHRQ) — Brief, Huddle, Debrief tools** — defines the short structured-presentation formats used in real clinical work (shift brief, mid-shift huddle, post-event debrief).
- **SBAR** — the most common clinical oral-communication structure; reinforces planning ahead of speaking.
- **CDC Clear Communication Index** — evidence-based criteria for health communication that apply equally to oral and written products.
- **Hospital grand rounds / in-service presentation conventions** — standard time budgets and structures students will encounter in clinical rotations.

## Activity specs

### Pitch Script Builder

```yaml
name: Pitch Script Builder
format: build_and_critique
supports:
  primary: 127.403-c2E
  secondary: [127.403-c1A, 127.403-c2F]
duration_min: 12
mode: single_player
equipment: one_device_per_student
learning_goal: >
  In 6 minutes, write a 90-second pitch script that hits four
  delivery-readiness targets simultaneously: word count in 195-240,
  zero hedging phrases, zero sentence-start fillers, no sentence
  longer than 20 words.
vocab_exercised:
  - Storyboard
  - Slide discipline
  - Rehearse aloud
  - Brief (TeamSTEPPS)
content_model: script_writing_task
content:
  prompt_pool_size: 8
  each_prompt_provides:
    - prompt_text                   # e.g., "Explain SBAR to a peer in 90s"
    - target_word_count_range: [195, 240]    # ~130-160 wpm at a comfortable spoken pace
    - banned_hedges: ["kind of", "sort of", "I think", "maybe", "I guess", "a little", "pretty much"]
    - banned_sentence_starts: ["So,", "Um,", "Uh,", "Like,", "Well,"]
    - max_sentence_words: 20
initial_state:
  prompt_visible: true
  compose_box_empty: true
  soft_timer_seconds: 360           # 6 min; logged, not enforced
  live_counters: [word_count, hedges, sentence_start_fillers, longest_sentence]
controls: [type, backspace, submit]
feedback:
  moment: real_time
  behaviors:
    - word count counter turns green only inside 195-240
    - hedge count turns amber on first hit, red on second
    - sentence-start filler counter mirrors the hedge behavior
    - longest-sentence counter flashes red on any sentence over 20 words
    - submit button enabled only when all four counters are green
scoring:
  per_counter_in_target: +1
  per_counter_max: 1
  total_max: 4
  mastery_threshold: 4
win_condition:
  type: submit_with_all_counters_green
  mastery: all four counters green at submission
reset:
  clears: [compose_box, counters, soft_timer]
  preserves: [prompt, target_definitions]
student_view: >
  Prompt card at the top; large compose box in the center; four
  live counters as a rail on the right (word count vs. range, hedges
  vs. 0, fillers vs. 0, longest sentence vs. 20); pacing cue in a
  small footer ("this reads as ~135 wpm spoken"). Submit button is
  grey until all counters clear.
teacher_view: >
  Which counter the class fails most — surfaces whether the cohort's
  weak spot is pacing (usually underwriting), hedging, or sentence
  length. Per-prompt word-count distribution histogram is the quickest
  read on where the next debrief lands.
accessibility:
  - Keyboard-first writing experience
  - Counters show numeric values, not color alone ("214 / 195-240")
  - High-contrast focus ring on the compose box
  - Soft timer, no hard cutoff
success_criteria: Student reaches all-counters-green on 2 of 3 prompts within one class period.
tech_stack:
  base: html+css+js
  libraries:
    - name: Tailwind CSS (Play CDN)
      cdn_url: https://cdn.tailwindcss.com
      purpose: Rail layout, counter chips, large compose box styling
    - name: Alpine.js
      cdn_url: https://unpkg.com/alpinejs@3.x.x/dist/cdn.min.js
      purpose: Real-time counter state, submit gating, banned-phrase detection
ai_required: []
visual_notes: >
  Stage-cue aesthetic — prompt card feels like an index card, compose
  box is the performance surface. Counters are small pill chips on
  the right rail, each showing current value vs. target ("214 / 195-240").
  Submit button gates clearly — grey with a single word "LOCKED" until
  all counters clear, then turns a confident green "SUBMIT". No strobe,
  no modals.
```

**Instructions to student:** Write a 90-second pitch answering the prompt. Target: 195–240 words (about 130–160 words per minute at a comfortable speaking pace), no hedging phrases, no sentence-start fillers, no sentence longer than 20 words. Watch the four counters on the right — submit unlocks only when all four are green.

**Teacher notes:** The single most common failure is hedging. Project the class counter-failure distribution: if hedges dominate, spend 5 minutes on *stating, not suggesting* — words like "I think" and "maybe" hide behind politeness and should come out of clinical-facing scripts. If word count is the miss, students are underwriting — they haven't yet learned that 90 seconds of speech is more words than they think.

### Audience Reshape

```yaml
name: Audience Reshape
format: build_and_critique
supports:
  primary: 127.403-c2E
  secondary: [127.403-c1A]
duration_min: 15
mode: single_player
equipment: one_device_per_student
learning_goal: >
  Write the same 3-minute case three times — once for a peer clinician,
  once for the patient, once for the hospital board — with each
  version meeting its audience-specific rules at >= 4/5 per audience.
vocab_exercised:
  - Storyboard
  - Slide discipline
  - In-service
  - Grand rounds
content_model: audience_switch_text_task
content:
  case_pool_size: 5
  audiences:
    - name: peer_clinician
      jargon_policy: expected        # plain-only flagged — signals uncertainty
      hedge_max: 4
      reading_grade_floor: 10
    - name: patient
      jargon_policy: banned_unless_glossed   # every medical term must be followed by a plain-language gloss
      hedge_max: 2
      reading_grade_ceiling: 6
    - name: hospital_board
      jargon_policy: light
      hedge_max: 1
      banned_phrases: ["I think", "probably", "we believe"]
      reading_grade_floor: 9
  each_case_provides:
    - shared_facts_pool
    - per_audience_rules
    - gold_script_per_audience      # one model, multiple acceptable answers
initial_state:
  case_visible: true
  three_tabs: [peer_clinician, patient, hospital_board]
  each_tab_empty: true
controls:
  - select_audience_tab
  - type
  - backspace
  - submit_audience
  - view_metrics
feedback:
  moment: on_submit_per_tab
  shows:
    - per-rule pass/fail per audience (jargon count, hedge count, reading grade, banned phrases)
    - audience-specific violations flagged inline (e.g., "jargon to patient: 'tachycardia' not glossed")
    - once all three tabs are submitted, split-three view compares the scripts side-by-side
scoring:
  per_audience: 0-5 (auto rule-check)
  total_max: 15
  mastery_threshold: 12
win_condition:
  type: all_three_audiences_submitted
  mastery: total >= 12/15
reset:
  clears: [all_three_tabs, metrics]
  preserves: [case, audience_definitions]
student_view: >
  Case panel on the left with the shared facts; three audience tabs
  across the top, each with its rules summarized in chips (reading
  grade, jargon policy, hedging tolerance); compose box fills the
  bottom. Once all three tabs are submitted, the split-three view
  compares them.
teacher_view: >
  Per-student matrix of which audience each handled best/worst.
  Most cohorts ace the peer tab, leak jargon to the patient, and
  hedge to the board. The matrix tells you which subskill to drill
  next.
accessibility:
  - Keyboard tab navigation between audiences
  - Rule chips show numeric targets, not color alone
  - Plain textareas, screen-reader friendly
success_criteria: Total >= 12/15 within one class period.
tech_stack:
  base: html+css+js
  libraries:
    - name: Tailwind CSS (Play CDN)
      cdn_url: https://cdn.tailwindcss.com
      purpose: Tab layout, rule chips, split-three comparison view
    - name: Alpine.js
      cdn_url: https://unpkg.com/alpinejs@3.x.x/dist/cdn.min.js
      purpose: Tab state, per-audience submit, metrics reveal, comparison unlock
  notes: Medical-term dictionary ships as a static JSON asset; jargon detection is a client-side lookup. Reading-grade level computed via Flesch-Kincaid on the client.
ai_required: []
visual_notes: >
  Tab-based audience switcher with subtle tints per audience (peer = slate,
  patient = warm neutral, board = deep slate). Rule chips on each tab
  show current value vs. target inline. Final side-by-side comparison of
  the three scripts is the reveal — no modal, no celebration, just the
  three columns letting the student see their own audience-switching.
```

**Instructions to student:** Same case, three times, three audiences. Tab to the audience, read its rules, write the script. The rule chips on each tab show you where you are vs. the target — jargon count, hedge count, reading grade. Submit each tab when its rules are green. Once all three are submitted, you'll see them side by side.

**Teacher notes:** The per-student matrix tells you where the cohort is weakest. Patient-audience jargon leaks and board-audience hedging are the two most common failure modes. If a student aces all three on the first try, check whether they over-condensed the peer version — expertise signals come through in *some* density, not uniform simplicity. Pair this with the Pitch Script Builder drill for students who hedge to the board.

### Visual Density Audit

```yaml
name: Visual Density Audit
format: build_and_critique
supports:
  primary: 127.403-c2E
  secondary: [127.403-c2B]
duration_min: 10
mode: single_player
equipment: one_device_per_student
learning_goal: >
  Audit a 6-slide deck against three rules (word count <= 25 per slide,
  font size >= 24 pt, single-idea density), then rewrite the two worst
  slides to score in the green zone on all three dimensions, total
  >= 24/30.
vocab_exercised:
  - Slide discipline
  - Storyboard
content_model: deck_review_task
content:
  deck_pool_size: 8
  slides_per_deck: 6
  each_slide_provides:
    - rendered_text
    - word_count
    - font_size_px
    - density_score                   # 1-5; auto-computed (word count + idea count)
    - gold_rewrite                    # for the 2 worst slides only
initial_state:
  deck_loaded: true
  slide_thumbnails_visible: true
  density_heatmap_rendered: true
controls:
  - click_slide_to_inspect
  - edit_slide_text
  - edit_font_size
  - submit_two_rewrites
feedback:
  moment: real_time
  shows:
    - density heatmap updates as student edits
    - rubric chips per slide (word count, font size, density) flip red -> amber -> green
    - on submit, gold rewrite shown side-by-side for each rewritten slide
scoring:
  per_rewrite_max: 15            # 5 per dimension x 3 dimensions
  total_max: 30
  mastery_threshold: 24
win_condition:
  type: two_rewrites_submitted
  mastery: total >= 24/30
reset:
  clears: [edits, heatmap_state]
  preserves: [deck_pool]
student_view: >
  Deck thumbnails across the top with red / amber / green tint plus a
  text label; large slide editor in the center with a font-size slider;
  rubric chips on the right side. The two worst slides are flagged
  with a "rewrite this" badge.
teacher_view: >
  Per-deck pre/post density score; common student mistakes (most often:
  cutting words but leaving font tiny, or splitting one bad slide into
  two equally bad ones).
accessibility:
  - Keyboard slide nav (left / right arrows)
  - Density indicator includes numeric value, not color alone
  - Font slider has tick marks at standard sizes (18, 24, 32, 40)
success_criteria: Total >= 24/30 within one class period.
tech_stack:
  base: html+css+js
  libraries:
    - name: Tailwind CSS (Play CDN)
      cdn_url: https://cdn.tailwindcss.com
      purpose: Thumbnail strip, editor pane, rubric chips
    - name: Alpine.js
      cdn_url: https://unpkg.com/alpinejs@3.x.x/dist/cdn.min.js
      purpose: Slide selection, edit state, real-time rubric updates
ai_required: []
visual_notes: >
  Thumbnails render as actual mini-slides with the live text and font
  size — students see their edits update the thumbnail in real time.
  Heatmap tint is subtle (red = soft pink, green = muted sage), with
  text labels for accessibility. Gold rewrite reveal appears below the
  editor, not in a modal.
```

**Instructions to student:** Open the deck. The two worst slides are flagged. Edit them — cut words, raise the font size, split or merge ideas — until all three rubric chips turn green. The thumbnail updates as you type. Submit when both flagged slides are in the green zone. Goal: 24/30.

**Teacher notes:** The two most common failure modes are (1) cutting words but leaving the font at 12 pt (because the text "fits") and (2) splitting one overstuffed slide into two equally overstuffed slides. Both surface in the teacher view. The fix for both is the same — *one idea per slide* — and it pairs well with a quick whiteboard exercise: write a slide title, then state the one idea aloud in a single sentence.
