## Learning objective

Students will be able to organize ideas and source material, compile them accurately with citations, and write a short healthcare report or summary that uses audience-first structure and plain language.

## Content stub

- **The three verbs as a sequence, not three skills:** *organize* (outline: audience, purpose, key points) → *compile* (gather facts, data, and sources, cite them) → *write* (produce the document)
- **Audience-first structure:** headline / main point → background → findings → recommendations; one idea per paragraph; plain language for patient-facing material
- **Report genres students will actually produce:** incident reports, shift summaries, patient-education handouts, lab write-ups, public-health briefs
- **Citing sources in healthcare writing:** cite original data and guidelines; quote sparingly; never fabricate numbers; flag where the evidence is weak
- **Editing pass before submission:** clarity (does every sentence add something?), precision (are values measurable?), completeness (is the audience's next question answered?)
- **Relationship to c1A and c2F:** c1A sets the clarity/conciseness bar, c2F sets the precision bar — c2D is the production-of-a-written-artifact standard that must meet both

## Vocabulary

- **Organize** — the first verb of the three-verb sequence: outline the audience, purpose, and key points before gathering material or writing.
- **Compile** — the second verb: gather facts, data, and source citations; never fabricate numbers; flag weak evidence.
- **Audience-first structure** — healthcare report layout that leads with the headline/main point, then background, findings, and recommendations.
- **Incident report** — a factual, non-blaming organizational record of an adverse event or near-miss, such as a medication near-miss. *(also in c12C)*
- **Patient-education handout** — a short patient-facing document that must meet plain-language health-literacy standards.
- **Citation** — a specific reference to an original data source or guideline; required in healthcare writing, with quoting used sparingly.
- **AMA Manual of Style** — the default style reference for US medical writing, covering citation, abbreviation, and numerical conventions.
- **Editing pass** — the pre-submission check for clarity, precision, and completeness before a report or summary is submitted.

## Assessment stub

- Summary build: given a 5–10 page public-health report, produce a one-page summary for a non-expert audience; peer-scored on audience fit, clarity, completeness, and citation
- Outline-first exercise: given a stack of unordered facts about a patient or a health topic, produce a written outline for a named audience before drafting — instructor feedback on the outline, then the draft
- Incident-report practical: given a simulated adverse event (e.g., medication near-miss), produce a factual, non-blaming incident report that meets organizational reporting requirements

## Activities

- **Incident-Report Generator** — audio of an incident plays (patient fall, medication near-miss). Student fills the incident form with who / what / when / where, objective facts only. Rubric flags editorializing live: *"the patient was confused"* is editorializing, *"patient asked what year it was"* is a fact.
  → *procedural fluency, metacognition*

- **Shift-Summary Card** — 20+ shift events compress into a 5-bullet handoff card. Rubric enforces: must include any deterioration, any med changes, any family concerns — and nothing else. Over-reporting is penalized as hard as under-reporting.
  → *analysis, communication under pressure*

- **Note-Type Sorter** — mixed stack of note excerpts appears (SOAP progress note, admission H&P, discharge summary, nursing note, operative report). Student drags each into its correct note type. Rewards spotting the structural signatures (HPI vs. "hospital course" vs. "ROS").
  → *recall, pattern recognition*

## References

- **AHRQ Health Literacy Universal Precautions Toolkit** — plain-language writing standards for patient-facing reports and summaries.
- **AMA Manual of Style (11th ed.)** — the default style reference for US medical writing, including citation, abbreviation, and numerical conventions.
- **AHRQ / TJC event-reporting formats** — structure and required elements for incident reports, including factual, non-blaming language.
- **ICMJE Recommendations** — source for the audience-first / IMRAD-adjacent structure used in health-sciences writing.

### Incident-Report Generator

```yaml
name: Incident-Report Generator
format: build_and_critique
supports:
  primary: 127.403-c2D
  secondary: [127.403-c1A, 127.403-c2F]
duration_min: 15
mode: single_player
equipment: one_device_per_student
learning_goal: >
  Given an audio incident clip and a structured incident form, produce
  a complete factual non-blaming report scoring at least 4/5 on each
  rubric dimension (who/what/when/where, factual-vs-editorial,
  completeness, plain language, deadline met).
vocab_exercised:
  - Incident report
  - AMA Manual of Style
  - Editing pass
  - Audience-first structure
content_model: audio_to_form_writing_task
content:
  clip_pool_size: 12
  duration_per_clip_seconds: 45-90
  each_clip_provides:
    - source_audio
    - transcript                     # for accessibility, hidden by default
    - gold_form_fields               # who / what / when / where + chronology
    - banned_phrases                 # editorializing words ("confused," "panicked," "negligent")
    - required_facts                 # objective observations to capture
  incidents: []                      # populate with 12 entries, each: {id, gold_form_fields, required_facts, banned_phrases}. PLACEHOLDER.
  scripts: []                        # populate with 12 entries matching incidents[] by id; each: {id, speaker: {persona, role, gender, age_band}, script_text, pacing_notes, language}. PLACEHOLDER.
  dimension_rubric:
    factual_vs_editorial:
      5_example: "<every claim is directly observed or attributed; no inference presented as fact>"
      3_example: "<1-2 subtle editorializing phrases slip past the banned-phrase regex>"
      1_example: "<report is heavy with inference ('the patient was confused', 'obviously agitated')>"
      guidance: "penalize every claim that cannot be traced to an observation in the source audio"
    completeness:
      5_example: "<all required_facts present; chronology intact; no extraneous content>"
      3_example: "<1-2 required facts missing OR chronology scrambled>"
      1_example: "<core facts missing OR cause and effect confused>"
      guidance: "ask whether a reader could reconstruct the incident from this report alone"
initial_state:
  audio_loaded_not_played: true
  form_blank: true
  rubric_sidebar_visible: true
  soft_timer_seconds: 600
controls:
  - play_audio
  - pause_audio
  - scrub_audio
  - type_form_field
  - toggle_transcript                # accessibility
  - submit
feedback:
  moment: end_of_activity
  during_typing:
    - banned_phrases get a live amber underline
  on_submit:
    - per-dimension rubric score
    - gold form revealed side-by-side
    - one-sentence diagnostic per dimension below 4/5
scoring:
  rubric:
    who_what_when_where: 0-5         # auto: required field completion
    factual_vs_editorial: 0-5        # auto: zero if any banned_phrase submitted
    completeness: 0-5                # auto: required_facts keyword match
    plain_language: 0-5              # auto-heuristic: reading-grade level, jargon density
    deadline_met: 0-5                # 5 if under timer, scaled below
  mastery_threshold: 20
  notes: deterministic auto-scoring; banned_phrase auto-zeroes the editorial dimension
win_condition:
  type: student_submit
  mastery: total >= 20/25
reset:
  clears: [form, audio_position, timer]
  preserves: [clip_pool, completed_clips_history]
student_view: >
  Audio player at the top with play / scrub / transcript-toggle;
  structured form below (who / what / when / where headings, then a
  narrative box); rubric sidebar on the right with each dimension
  expanded on hover.
teacher_view: >
  Per-rubric class averages; the most common editorializing phrases
  captured this period (these are the productive debrief material —
  most students editorialize on the same handful of words).
accessibility:
  - Transcript toggle for the audio (always available, never penalized)
  - Standard textarea fields, screen-reader friendly
  - Banned-phrase underline accompanied by an inline icon, not color alone
  - Soft timer only — no anxiety pressure for students processing audio slower
success_criteria: Student reaches mastery on 2 of 3 incident clips within one class period.
tech_stack:
  base: html+css+js
  libraries:
    - name: Tailwind CSS (Play CDN)
      cdn_url: https://cdn.tailwindcss.com
      purpose: Three-column layout (audio + form + rubric sidebar)
    - name: Alpine.js
      cdn_url: https://unpkg.com/alpinejs@3.x.x/dist/cdn.min.js
      purpose: Form state, banned-phrase live detection, submit unlock, rubric reveal
ai_required:
  - category: tts
    reason: "Generating 12+ realistic incident audio clips (fall, medication near-miss, elopement) scales cleaner with TTS than re-recording every variation."
    fallback: "Ship with a fixed pool of pre-recorded clips; feature stays in scope but scale is capped at the recorded set."
    voice_profile:
      persona: narrator
      gender: neutral
      age_band: middle
      accent: en-US general
    pacing: natural
    output_format: mp3
    sample_rate: 24000
    filename_pattern: "c2d-incident-{index:02d}.mp3"
  - category: text_rubric_scoring
    reason: "Factual-vs-editorial and completeness dimensions catch subtle framing ('appeared fine', 'seemed upset') that a banned-phrase list misses."
    fallback: "Banned-phrase list + required-facts keyword match covers the obvious ~80%; remaining edge cases surface for teacher review."
    dimensions: [factual_vs_editorial, completeness]
    deterministic_dimensions: [who_what_when_where, plain_language, deadline_met]
    prompt_template: text-rubric-v1
    output_schema:
      scores: map<dimension, int 0-5>
      diagnostics: map<dimension, string one-line>
    model: claude-haiku-4-5
    max_tokens: 400
visual_notes: >
  Form-first layout — audio player is a thin band at the top, the form
  is the visual focus. Banned-phrase underlines are amber (not red — the
  student needs to see them as warnings, not failures, while typing).
  Gold form on review appears in a two-column diff with edits in cyan.
```

**Instructions to student:** Listen to the incident audio (use the transcript if you need it). Fill the incident form: who, what, when, where, then a brief narrative. Stick to objective facts — *"the patient asked what year it was"* is a fact, *"the patient was confused"* is an opinion. The system will underline editorializing words live as you type. Submit when done; the model report unlocks then.

**Teacher notes:** The "most common editorializing phrases" report is your debrief material. Almost every cohort gravitates to the same three or four words (*confused*, *panicked*, *uncooperative*, *negligent*). Spend 5 minutes on those words specifically — what objective observation would replace each one? That's the skill the standard is actually teaching.

### Shift-Summary Card

```yaml
name: Shift-Summary Card
format: build_and_critique
supports:
  primary: 127.403-c2D
  secondary: [127.403-c1A, 127.403-c4B]
duration_min: 10
mode: single_player
equipment: one_device_per_student
learning_goal: >
  Given a 12-hour shift event log of 20+ items, produce a 5-bullet
  handoff card that includes every clinically relevant signal
  (deterioration, med changes, family concerns) and excludes
  everything else, scoring at least 8/10 on a must-include /
  must-exclude rubric.
vocab_exercised:
  - SBAR
  - Audience-first structure
  - Editing pass
content_model: log_to_summary_task
content:
  log_pool_size: 8
  events_per_log: 20-25
  each_log_provides:
    - timestamped_event_list
    - gold_5_bullet_card
    - must_include_facts          # clinically relevant (deterioration, meds, family)
    - must_exclude_facts          # noise (routine vitals if stable, snack preferences)
  shifts: []                     # populate with 8 entries matching the schema above. PLACEHOLDER.
  dimension_rubric:
    over_reporting:
      5_example: "<card has zero bullets beyond must-include signals; no stable vitals, no social niceties>"
      3_example: "<1 bullet is noise — a stable vital or benign family comment that belongs in the chart, not the handoff>"
      1_example: "<2+ bullets are routine content crowding out a real must-include signal>"
      guidance: "penalize each bullet that neither matches a must-include fact nor fills a must-exclude category explicitly — over-reporting is as costly as under-reporting"
initial_state:
  log_visible_top: true
  blank_card_below: true
  max_bullets_enforced: 5
  soft_timer_seconds: 480
controls:
  - type_in_bullet
  - add_bullet                  # blocked at 5
  - delete_bullet
  - reorder_bullets
  - submit
feedback:
  moment: end_of_activity
  shows:
    - per-bullet must-include match
    - over-reporting flags on bullets that contain must-exclude content
    - gold card revealed below
scoring:
  must_include_hit: +1 each
  must_include_missed: -1 each
  must_exclude_in_bullet: -1 each
  bullet_count_over_5: hard reject — submit blocked, soft warning
  mastery_threshold: 8
win_condition:
  type: student_submit
  mastery: score >= 8/10
reset:
  clears: [card, soft_timer]
  preserves: [log_pool, completed_logs_history]
student_view: >
  Scrollable event log on the left (timestamped, color-coded by
  category — vitals, meds, family, comments); 5 numbered slot cards
  on the right; word counter under each slot.
teacher_view: >
  Aggregate must-include miss rate per log — surfaces which clinical
  signals the class consistently filters out (often family concerns
  or pain mentions). Also: per-student over-reporting tendency.
accessibility:
  - Keyboard navigation for all bullets
  - Bullet-limit enforced with a clear announce ("5 of 5 bullets used")
  - Color-coded log categories also use icons
success_criteria: Student reaches 8/10 on 2 of 3 logs in one class period.
tech_stack:
  base: html+css+js
  libraries:
    - name: Tailwind CSS (Play CDN)
      cdn_url: https://cdn.tailwindcss.com
      purpose: Two-pane layout, log color coding, bullet card styling
    - name: Alpine.js
      cdn_url: https://unpkg.com/alpinejs@3.x.x/dist/cdn.min.js
      purpose: Bullet state, max-bullet enforcement, submit gating
ai_required:
  - category: text_rubric_scoring
    reason: "Borderline — must-include and must-exclude matching is mostly keyword-driven, but over-reporting detection on student-written bullets benefits from semantic judgment on whether a bullet 'says something new'."
    fallback: "Strict keyword match against must-include and must-exclude lists covers deterministic scoring; any bullet that matches neither surfaces for teacher review."
    dimensions: [over_reporting]
    deterministic_dimensions: [must_include_hit, must_exclude_violation]
    prompt_template: text-rubric-v1
    output_schema:
      scores: map<dimension, int 0-5>
      diagnostics: map<dimension, string one-line>
    model: claude-haiku-4-5
    max_tokens: 250
visual_notes: >
  Handoff aesthetic — the 5-bullet card uses a subtle monospace nod
  ("this is a clinical document"). Missed must-include signals are
  highlighted on the log in amber after submit. No celebration modal;
  the gold card appears inline below the student's card.
```

**Instructions to student:** You're handing off to the next nurse. Read the full shift log, then write a 5-bullet card that captures everything clinically relevant — deterioration, med changes, family concerns — and nothing else. You only get 5 bullets. Make them count.

**Teacher notes:** Watch the must-include miss rate by category. If family concerns or pain notes are filtered out by most of the class, that's a signal to teach *who needs to know what* explicitly. Over-reporting (5 bullets full of routine vitals) is the other failure mode — those students are treating the card like a chart note, not a handoff. Both patterns deserve named feedback.

### Note-Type Sorter

```yaml
name: Note-Type Sorter
format: drag_and_drop
supports:
  primary: 127.403-c2D
  secondary: [127.403-c2C]
duration_min: 8
mode: single_player
equipment: one_device_per_student
learning_goal: >
  Sort 20 mixed clinical-note excerpts into their correct note types
  (SOAP progress note, admission H&P, discharge summary, nursing
  note, operative report) at >= 18/20 within 4 minutes by recognizing
  structural signatures.
vocab_exercised:
  - Audience-first structure
  - Incident report
content_model: drag_classification_pool
content:
  excerpt_pool_size: 60
  excerpts_per_session: 20
  bins:
    - SOAP progress note
    - Admission H&P
    - Discharge summary
    - Nursing note
    - Operative report
  each_excerpt_provides:
    - excerpt_text                    # 2-4 sentences, structural signature intact
    - correct_note_type
    - structural_signature_hint       # shown only on miss (e.g., "ROS belongs in H&P")
initial_state:
  bins_visible: true
  excerpt_deck_shuffled: true
  timer_seconds: 240
  score: 0
controls:
  - drag_excerpt
  - drop_in_bin
  - undo_last_placement
  - submit_all
feedback:
  moment: end_of_activity
  shows:
    - per-bin accuracy
    - structural-signature hint for each missed excerpt
    - confusion-matrix preview (which two note types the student conflates)
scoring:
  correct_placement: +1
  incorrect_placement: 0
  unsubmitted_excerpt: 0
  mastery_threshold: 18
win_condition:
  type: all_submitted_or_timer_expiry
  mastery: score >= 18/20
reset:
  clears: [placements, timer]
  preserves: [excerpt_pool]
student_view: >
  Five labeled bins across the bottom of the screen, each with a
  subtle structural-signature icon (S/O/A/P chip for SOAP, etc.);
  shuffled deck of excerpt cards along the top; timer top-right.
teacher_view: >
  Class-level confusion matrix between note types — surfaces which
  pairs are most commonly conflated (typically SOAP-vs-nursing-note
  and H&P-vs-discharge-summary).
accessibility:
  - Keyboard drag (Tab to excerpt, number 1-5 to bin, Enter to drop)
  - Bin labels include the icon AND a text shortcut
  - Screen reader announces each excerpt's text on focus
success_criteria: Student reaches 18/20 within 4 minutes on one session.
tech_stack:
  base: html+css+js
  libraries:
    - name: Tailwind CSS (Play CDN)
      cdn_url: https://cdn.tailwindcss.com
      purpose: Bin layout, card styling, signature icons
    - name: Alpine.js
      cdn_url: https://unpkg.com/alpinejs@3.x.x/dist/cdn.min.js
      purpose: Placement state, timer, submit
    - name: SortableJS
      cdn_url: https://cdn.jsdelivr.net/npm/sortablejs@1.15.2/Sortable.min.js
      purpose: Polished drag-and-drop (HTML5 native is too rough)
ai_required: []
visual_notes: >
  Card-and-bin layout. Excerpt cards lift subtly on hover, snap into
  bins with a soft transition. Misplaced excerpts on review get an
  amber outline; the structural-signature hint appears as italic
  text below the card, not a popover.
```

**Instructions to student:** You'll see 20 excerpts from real clinical notes. Drag each into the right note-type bin: SOAP, H&P, discharge summary, nursing note, operative report. Look for the structural signatures — *ROS* almost always means H&P; *"hospital course"* means discharge summary; *PARQ* means operative report. You have 4 minutes. 18 right = mastery.

**Teacher notes:** The confusion matrix tells you which note types feel similar to your students. SOAP-vs-nursing-note is the most common conflation (both are progress-style); H&P-vs-discharge-summary is the second (both are bookend documents). Project the matrix and walk through one excerpt from each conflated pair — the structural difference becomes obvious in 2 minutes of discussion.
