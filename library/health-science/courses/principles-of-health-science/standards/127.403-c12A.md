## Learning objective

Students will be able to research and identify the technological equipment used across the five health science systems — diagnostic, therapeutic, health informatics, support services, and biotechnology research and development — and explain the function of representative equipment in each system using authoritative sources.

## Content stub

- **Uses the same five-systems framework as c7A:** students already know the career-side of the five systems; c12A populates each system with the equipment that makes the work possible
- **Representative equipment by system (survey level — depth grows in specialty courses):**

  **Diagnostic services**
  - *Imaging* — X-ray (radiography), CT (computed tomography), MRI (magnetic resonance imaging), ultrasound, fluoroscopy, mammography, nuclear medicine gamma cameras, PET scanners, bone-density (DEXA) scanners
  - *Cardiac diagnostic* — EKG/ECG machines, Holter monitors, event monitors, echocardiography units, stress-test equipment
  - *Pulmonary diagnostic* — spirometers, pulse oximeters, arterial blood-gas analyzers
  - *Laboratory* — automated chemistry analyzers (e.g., CMP, lipid panels), hematology analyzers (CBC), coagulation analyzers, blood-gas analyzers, microbiology culture systems, molecular diagnostic platforms (PCR), flow cytometers, urinalysis analyzers, glucose meters (point-of-care)

  **Therapeutic services**
  - *IV therapy and medication delivery* — smart infusion pumps, PCA pumps, insulin pumps, barcode medication-administration systems
  - *Respiratory therapy* — mechanical ventilators (invasive and non-invasive), CPAP/BiPAP, oxygen delivery systems, nebulizers, suction equipment
  - *Cardiac therapy* — defibrillators (AED and manual), external pacemakers, implantable cardioverter-defibrillators (ICDs), cardiac-arrest carts
  - *Surgical equipment* — electrosurgical units, anesthesia machines, surgical robots (e.g., da Vinci), laparoscopic towers, endoscopy systems, operating tables and lights
  - *Rehabilitation* — physical-therapy modalities (ultrasound, TENS, electrical stimulation), gait-training equipment, prosthetics and orthotics, assistive technology
  - *Dialysis* — hemodialysis machines, peritoneal-dialysis systems
  - *Radiation therapy* — linear accelerators, brachytherapy delivery systems
  - *Patient support at bedside* — electric hospital beds, patient lifts, nurse-call systems, pressure-mapping surfaces

  **Health informatics**
  - *EHR workstations and mobile clinical devices* — on-unit workstations on wheels (WOWs), tablets, handheld barcode scanners
  - *Patient-monitoring systems* — central telemetry monitors, bedside monitors, continuous glucose monitors, remote patient-monitoring devices
  - *Health information exchange (HIE) infrastructure* — interoperability networks, FHIR APIs, patient portals
  - *Coding and billing systems* — encoder software, claims-management systems, denial-management tools
  - *Clinical decision support systems* — drug-interaction checkers, sepsis-prediction algorithms, imaging-triage AI

  **Support services**
  - *Sterile processing* — steam sterilizers (autoclaves), low-temperature sterilizers (ethylene oxide, hydrogen peroxide plasma), washer-disinfectors, ultrasonic cleaners
  - *Environmental services* — UV-C room disinfection units, HEPA filtration (HVAC), negative-pressure airborne-infection isolation rooms (AIIRs)
  - *Patient transport and logistics* — gurneys, wheelchairs, pneumatic-tube systems for specimens, automated guided vehicles in large hospitals
  - *Dietary* — therapeutic-diet software, trayline equipment, allergen-management systems
  - *Safety and facility systems* — fire-suppression systems, medical-gas systems with zone valves, emergency power generators, nurse-call and code-blue alert systems

  **Biotechnology research and development**
  - *Molecular biology* — PCR thermocyclers, real-time PCR (qPCR), DNA/RNA extraction systems, next-generation sequencing (NGS) platforms
  - *Cell biology* — biosafety cabinets (Class I/II), incubators, centrifuges, flow cytometers, fluorescence microscopes, confocal microscopes
  - *Protein and immunoassay* — plate readers, Western-blot apparatus, ELISA platforms, mass spectrometers
  - *Pharmaceutical development* — HPLC (high-performance liquid chromatography), dissolution-testing apparatus, bioreactors for biologics
  - *Bioinformatics* — high-performance computing clusters, genomic data-analysis pipelines
- **Research verb, operationalized:**
  - Students use credible sources to go beyond the survey list: manufacturer documentation, FDA 510(k) summaries, professional-organization reference materials, peer-reviewed medical literature
  - Research a specific piece of equipment end-to-end: what it does, how it works at a basic level, what it costs (order of magnitude), what credentials operate it, what failure modes matter
- **How equipment shapes what roles can do:** credentials and equipment co-evolve — a respiratory therapist is defined partly by which equipment they operate; the same is true of radiologic technologists, medical laboratory scientists, and surgical techs
- **Crosswalks with other standards:** c7A (the five-systems framework for careers), c7B (how teams across systems collaborate), c12B (malfunctions), c12C (reporting)

## Vocabulary

- **FDA 510(k)** — the FDA premarket-notification pathway for most moderate-risk medical devices; publicly searchable database of cleared devices.
- **Smart infusion pump** — an IV-therapy pump with a drug library and dose-error reduction alerts; representative therapeutic-services equipment.
- **Linear accelerator (LINAC)** — the radiation-therapy delivery device used in most external-beam treatment.
- **Autoclave** — steam sterilizer used in central sterile processing; a canonical support-services device.
- **Biosafety cabinet** — Class I/II enclosure used in cell biology and molecular work; representative biotechnology R&D equipment.
- **Next-generation sequencing (NGS)** — high-throughput DNA/RNA sequencing platform used in biotech R&D and increasingly in clinical diagnostics.
- **FHIR** — Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources; the modern API standard underlying health-information-exchange infrastructure.
- **Workstation on wheels (WOW)** — mobile EHR workstation used at the bedside; representative health-informatics equipment.
- **AIIR** — Airborne Infection Isolation Room; negative-pressure infrastructure in support services. *(also in c11B)*
- **ECRI Institute** — independent authoritative source on medical-device safety and top-10 health-technology hazards.

## Assessment stub

- System-classification drill: given 30 pieces of technological equipment, correctly assign each to one of the five systems; for ambiguous cases (e.g., a continuous glucose monitor — diagnostic? therapeutic? informatics?), explain the reasoning
- Equipment-research profile: pick one piece of technological equipment from any system; produce a one-page research profile covering (a) what it does, (b) clinical use case, (c) representative manufacturer(s), (d) approximate cost or cost range, (e) the credentialed role that operates it, (f) at least three authoritative sources (manufacturer documentation, FDA 510(k) summary, peer-reviewed article, professional-organization reference)
- Five-system equipment map: for a single patient episode (e.g., a scheduled outpatient MRI with IV contrast; a hospitalized pneumonia admission with IV antibiotics; a routine newborn care sequence; a Type 1 diabetes clinic visit), list every piece of technological equipment touched across all five systems and identify which system each belongs to
- Short response: in 150–200 words, explain how a single new piece of technology (e.g., continuous glucose monitors, at-home sleep apnea diagnostic devices, or smartphone-based ECG monitors) crosses two or more of the five TEKS-named systems and what that means for how the equipment gets regulated and who is trained to use it

## Activities

- **Five-System Equipment Sort** — 30 equipment cards stream across the screen (CT scanner; smart infusion pump; da Vinci surgical robot; autoclave; PCR thermocycler; workstation on wheels; pulse oximeter; AIIR negative-pressure room; linear accelerator; hemodialysis machine; flow cytometer; ECRI-tracked device; FHIR API gateway; mass spectrometer; central-sterile washer-disinfector). Student drags each to one of five system pads (Diagnostic / Therapeutic / Informatics / Support / Biotech R&D) under a 4-minute timer. Ambiguous cases — continuous glucose monitor (diagnostic? therapeutic? informatics?) — open a text-box for justification and earn full credit on well-reasoned answers.
  → *pattern recognition, analysis*

- **Equipment Research Profile** — student picks one device from a locked menu (smart infusion pump, linear accelerator, PCR thermocycler, autoclave, biosafety cabinet Class II) and has 15 minutes to build a one-page profile by pulling from FDA 510(k) database, manufacturer documentation, and a professional-org equipment reference. Required fields: what it does, clinical use case, manufacturer(s), approximate cost range, credentialed operator, three cited sources. Missing citations grey the submit button. A "check against ECRI" final step asks whether the device or category appears on the current Top 10 Health Technology Hazards list.
  → *procedural fluency, analysis*

- **Patient-Episode Equipment Trace** — a single patient episode loads (outpatient MRI with IV contrast; pneumonia admission with IV antibiotics; routine newborn care sequence; Type 1 diabetes clinic visit). Student must list every piece of equipment the patient touches across all five systems, drag each onto a system pad, and name which role operates it. A 5-system coverage meter lights up as systems get populated — the common miss (biotech R&D and/or informatics) lights last.
  → *systems thinking, recall*

## References

- **FDA 510(k) Premarket Notification Database (fda.gov)** — authoritative primary source for US medical-device summaries; research any cleared device by name or manufacturer.
- **FDA Classification of Medical Devices (21 CFR Parts 862–892)** — the regulatory classification system that groups devices by risk and use.
- **US Bureau of Labor Statistics *Occupational Outlook Handbook*** — describes which equipment each healthcare occupation operates.
- **Professional-organization equipment and practice references** — ASRT (imaging), AARC (respiratory), ASCP (lab), APTA (PT), AAMA (medical assisting), AORN (surgical), CBSPD (central sterile processing).
- **NIH National Library of Medicine *MedlinePlus* equipment pages** — patient-oriented introductory material on common equipment; useful for plain-language starting research.
- **ECRI Institute device-safety reports and top-10 health-technology hazards lists** — independent authoritative source on medical-device safety and failure modes.
- **NCHSE Health Science Career Clusters** — the five-system framework the TEKS organizes around.
