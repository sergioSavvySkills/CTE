## Learning objective

Students will be able to interpret common forms of health science technical material — journal abstracts, drug package inserts, clinical practice guidelines, and safety data sheets — identifying purpose, findings, limitations, and the clinical decisions each document supports.

## Content stub

- **Types of technical material students will encounter:** peer-reviewed journal articles and abstracts, FDA-approved drug package inserts (Prescribing Information), clinical practice guidelines (USPSTF, AHA/ACC, CDC), discharge summaries and care plans, lab reports, Safety Data Sheets (SDS) for lab and clinical chemicals
- **Journal abstracts follow IMRAD structure:** Introduction (why the study) → Methods (what they did and to whom) → Results (what they found, with numbers) → Discussion (what it means, limitations); read the abstract to decide whether the full article is worth your time
- **Drug package insert map (21 CFR 201.56–57 format):** Highlights of Prescribing Information → Indications and Usage → Dosage and Administration → Contraindications → Warnings and Precautions → Adverse Reactions → Drug Interactions → Use in Specific Populations — know where to look, don't try to read end-to-end
- **Clinical practice guidelines:** pull the recommendation statement, the strength of the recommendation, the quality of the underlying evidence, and the population the recommendation applies to — a Grade A recommendation for adults 50–75 does not automatically apply to a 40-year-old
- **Reading for purpose:** before reading anything technical, name the decision you're trying to inform — it changes what sections to focus on
- **Spotting limitations:** sample size, study population (age, sex, comorbidities), who funded the study, single-center vs. multi-site, length of follow-up
- **Verifying claims against the primary source:** a secondary summary or headline can distort; when stakes are high, go to the original document

## Vocabulary

- **IMRAD** — Introduction, Methods, Results, and Discussion; the standard structure for peer-reviewed biomedical articles and abstracts.
- **Package insert / Prescribing Information (PI)** — the FDA-regulated labeling document for a drug; 21 CFR 201.56/57 defines the required sections.
- **Highlights of Prescribing Information** — the short front-of-label summary of the PI with indications, dosing, warnings, and contraindications, designed for fast reference.
- **Black-box warning** — the FDA's strongest warning on a drug's label, boxed and prominently placed because of serious or life-threatening risk.
- **Clinical practice guideline** — a formal recommendation document (USPSTF, AHA/ACC, CDC) with graded strength-of-recommendation and quality-of-evidence ratings.
- **USPSTF grade** — letters A, B, C, D, I that rate a prevention recommendation's strength and the evidence behind it; drives whether insurers must cover a service. *(also in c10B)*
- **Safety Data Sheet (SDS)** — the 16-section OSHA/GHS document for any workplace chemical that covers hazards, handling, storage, and emergency response.
- **Primary vs. secondary source** — primary = the original study, statute, or label; secondary = a summary or news story; high-stakes decisions require the primary.
- **Study limitations** — the sample size, population, funding, follow-up length, and site issues that constrain how broadly a study's findings apply.

## Assessment stub

- Package-insert practical: given a one-page excerpt of a real drug's Prescribing Information, answer 8 questions covering indication, contraindications, dosing for a specified patient, black-box warning, and one common adverse reaction
- Abstract analysis: given a published clinical-research abstract, identify the four IMRAD sections, state the main finding in one sentence, and list two limitations the abstract admits to
- Guideline application: given an excerpt from a USPSTF or AHA/ACC guideline, identify the recommendation, the evidence grade, the target population, and determine whether it applies to a specified patient; justify the answer

## Activities

- **Journal-Club Speedrun** — one abstract, six multiple-choice questions at 20 seconds each: population? outcome? trial or observational? sample size adequate? limitations admitted? Builds the reflex to extract PICO structure fast.
  → *recall, analysis*

- **Package-Insert Puzzle** — a drug package insert is open on screen; a patient case sits beside it. Can this patient take the drug? Find the contraindication. Timed. Forces students to navigate ugly pharma documents the way a real clinician does.
  → *application, pattern recognition*

- **Jargon Decoder** — a dense consult note or radiology report fills the screen. Unknown terms are underlined. Student hovers each to guess the meaning before the definition reveals. Score tracks guess accuracy. Builds the habit of inferring from context rather than defaulting to a lookup reflex.
  → *transfer, metacognition*

## References

- **FDA 21 CFR 201.56 and 201.57 — "Physician Labeling Rule"** — the federal regulation that defines the mandatory structure of every US drug package insert, including the Highlights of Prescribing Information section.
- **ICMJE (International Committee of Medical Journal Editors) Recommendations** — the source for the IMRAD abstract/article structure used across peer-reviewed biomedical journals.
- **USPSTF Grade Definitions and AHA/ACC Class of Recommendation / Level of Evidence framework** — the standard schemes students will see for rating clinical-guideline recommendations.
- **OSHA Hazard Communication Standard (29 CFR 1910.1200) / GHS Safety Data Sheet format** — 16-section SDS structure used in clinical labs and on any chemical a healthcare worker handles.
- **CDC MMWR (Morbidity and Mortality Weekly Report)** — accessible example of public-health technical material students can practice interpreting.

## Activity specs

### Journal-Club Speedrun

```yaml
name: Journal-Club Speedrun
format: rapid_fire_quiz
supports:
  primary: 127.403-c2C
  secondary: [127.403-c2B]
duration_min: 10
mode: single_player
equipment: one_device_per_student
learning_goal: >
  Given one IMRAD-structured abstract per round, correctly answer 6
  PICO/limits multiple-choice questions (population, outcome, study
  type, sample-size adequacy, primary finding, admitted limitation)
  at >= 5/6 within 20 seconds per question across 3 abstracts.
vocab_exercised:
  - IMRAD
  - Study limitations
  - Primary vs. secondary source
  - Clinical practice guideline
content_model: abstract_question_bank
content:
  abstract_pool_size: 18
  abstracts_per_session: 3
  questions_per_abstract: 6
  question_categories:
    - population              # who was studied
    - outcome                 # what was measured
    - study_type              # RCT / cohort / case-control / cross-sectional
    - sample_size_adequacy    # adequate / underpowered / unclear
    - primary_finding         # one-sentence summary
    - admitted_limitation     # what the abstract itself flags
  each_abstract_provides:
    - rendered_abstract_text  # 200-300 words, IMRAD layout preserved
    - section_anchors         # IMRAD spans for highlight-on-question
    - question_set            # 6 multiple-choice, 4 options each
    - gold_answers
    - one_line_rationale_per_question
initial_state:
  timer_seconds_per_question: 20
  abstract_visible: true
  question_index: 0
  score: 0
controls:
  - click_answer_chip
  - keyboard_shortcuts: [1, 2, 3, 4]
  - skip_question                # forfeits the point, advances timer
feedback:
  moment: immediate
  on_correct:
    - green border flash (120 ms)
    - corresponding IMRAD section briefly highlights in the abstract
  on_wrong:
    - red border flash (120 ms)
    - one-line rationale shown for 2 s, then auto-advance
  on_timeout:
    - amber dim, gold answer revealed for 2 s
scoring:
  correct: +1
  wrong: 0
  timeout: 0
  skip: 0
  mastery_threshold: 5    # per abstract
  session_mastery: 2 of 3 abstracts at >= 5/6
win_condition:
  type: 3_abstracts_completed
  mastery: 2 of 3 abstracts at >= 5/6
reset:
  clears: [score, current_abstract, current_question, timer]
  preserves: [abstract_pool, completed_abstracts]
student_view: >
  Abstract fills the upper two-thirds of the screen with IMRAD section
  labels in the margin; the active question and four answer chips sit
  in a bar pinned to the bottom; a slim countdown bar slides across the
  top. Question counter and abstract title top-right.
teacher_view: >
  Per-question class accuracy across all abstracts; surfaces which PICO
  facet (typically sample-size adequacy and admitted limitation) the
  class struggles with most; per-student trend across the three
  abstracts to see who improves with reps.
accessibility:
  - Keyboard shortcuts for every answer (1-4) and skip (S)
  - Border-flash feedback, not full-screen strobe
  - Section-anchor highlights pair with bold weight, not color alone
  - Screen reader reads the question, four options, and current section anchor
success_criteria: Student clears 2 of 3 abstracts at mastery within one class period.
tech_stack:
  base: html+css+js
  libraries:
    - name: Tailwind CSS (Play CDN)
      cdn_url: https://cdn.tailwindcss.com
      purpose: Two-pane layout, IMRAD margin labels, answer-chip styling
    - name: Alpine.js
      cdn_url: https://unpkg.com/alpinejs@3.x.x/dist/cdn.min.js
      purpose: Timer, question rotation, score, IMRAD-anchor highlight state
ai_required: []
visual_notes: >
  Editorial-journal aesthetic — serif body for the abstract, sans-serif
  for the question bar. IMRAD section labels in small-caps in a thin
  margin; the section currently relevant to the question lights up in
  cyan as a hint after wrong answers (never as the first reveal). No
  modal dialogs; the rationale appears as a one-line strip beneath
  the answer chips.
```

**Instructions to student:** You will see one journal abstract at a time, then six questions about it — 20 seconds each. Pick the option that the abstract actually supports, not what sounds plausible. If you do not know, skip rather than guess; wrong answers do not penalize but they do not help. Use number keys 1-4 to move faster.

**Teacher notes:** The two questions students miss most are sample-size adequacy and admitted-limitation — both require reading the Methods and Discussion sections, which novices skip. After the activity, pull up one abstract whose limitation question the class missed and read the Discussion paragraph aloud. The skill is not memorizing study-type definitions; it is the habit of looking past the headline finding to ask *what is the author themself worried about?*

### Package-Insert Puzzle

```yaml
name: Package-Insert Puzzle
format: timed_drill
supports:
  primary: 127.403-c2C
  secondary: [127.403-c2B, 127.403-c2F]
duration_min: 15
mode: single_player
equipment: one_device_per_student
learning_goal: >
  Across 6 patient-case + package-insert pairs, correctly determine
  whether the patient can take the prescribed drug AND identify the
  specific labeled section that justifies the answer (Contraindications,
  Warnings, Use in Specific Populations, Dosage, or Drug Interactions)
  at >= 5/6 within 90 seconds per case.
vocab_exercised:
  - Package insert / Prescribing Information (PI)
  - Highlights of Prescribing Information
  - Black-box warning
  - Study limitations
content_model: case_plus_label_match
content:
  case_pool_size: 20
  cases_per_session: 6
  labeled_sections_in_play:
    - highlights_of_prescribing_information
    - indications_and_usage
    - dosage_and_administration
    - contraindications
    - warnings_and_precautions
    - adverse_reactions
    - drug_interactions
    - use_in_specific_populations
  each_case_provides:
    - patient_vignette          # 3-4 sentences: age, sex, comorbidities, current meds, allergies
    - drug_under_consideration
    - rendered_PI_excerpt       # one-page styled excerpt with all 8 sections
    - go_or_no_go               # gold answer
    - justifying_section        # which PI section provides the basis
    - one_line_rationale
initial_state:
  timer_seconds_per_case: 90
  PI_section_navigation_visible: true
  case_index: 0
controls:
  - click_PI_section_tab
  - scroll_within_section
  - click_go
  - click_no_go
  - click_section_chip          # appears after Go/No-go to identify justifying section
  - keyboard_shortcuts: [G, N, 1-8]
feedback:
  moment: per_case
  on_correct_decision_and_section:
    - green ring on the chosen section chip
    - one-line rationale slides in
  on_correct_decision_wrong_section:
    - amber ring; gold section highlighted in the PI panel
  on_wrong_decision:
    - red ring; gold answer revealed; section that justifies it pulses for 2 s
scoring:
  correct_decision: +2
  correct_justifying_section: +1
  wrong_decision: 0
  wrong_section_correct_decision: +2 only
  timeout: 0
  mastery_threshold: 15   # of 18 max (6 cases x 3)
win_condition:
  type: 6_cases_completed_or_timer_expiry
  mastery: score >= 15 / 18 AND >= 5/6 correct go-or-no-go decisions
reset:
  clears: [case_progress, score, timer, current_case]
  preserves: [case_pool, completed_cases]
student_view: >
  Two-pane layout: patient vignette card on the left, package-insert
  excerpt on the right with eight section tabs running down its side.
  Decision buttons (Go / No-Go) bottom-left under the vignette; section
  chips appear after a decision. Timer ribbon top.
teacher_view: >
  Per-case accuracy plus a confusion grid showing which PI section the
  class wrongly cited as the justifier; flags the cases where the
  decision was correct but the justification was wrong (rote pattern
  matching without label literacy).
accessibility:
  - Keyboard navigation across PI section tabs (1-8) and decision (G/N)
  - PI section tabs use icons + text labels (no icon-only)
  - 16 px minimum body text in the PI panel; user-controlled zoom
  - Screen reader announces section name on focus and current section content
success_criteria: Student reaches 5/6 correct decisions and >= 4/6 correct justifying sections within one class period.
tech_stack:
  base: html+css+js
  libraries:
    - name: Tailwind CSS (Play CDN)
      cdn_url: https://cdn.tailwindcss.com
      purpose: Two-pane layout, PI tab navigation, section chip styling
    - name: Alpine.js
      cdn_url: https://unpkg.com/alpinejs@3.x.x/dist/cdn.min.js
      purpose: Section navigation, decision/section state machine, timer, scoring
ai_required: []
visual_notes: >
  Pharma-document aesthetic — small serif body for the PI excerpt, dense
  but legible; section tabs in a deliberately bureaucratic style to
  match real PI navigation. Highlights of Prescribing Information at the
  top is visually distinct (boxed) so students learn its role as the
  fast-reference summary. Black-box warnings render with a thin black
  border and slight inset shadow, no color screaming. No modals.
```

**Instructions to student:** Each case gives you a patient and a drug. The full Prescribing Information sits on the right with eight section tabs. Decide Go or No-Go in 90 seconds, then click the section chip that justifies your decision. The Highlights section is meant for fast reference — start there and drill into the full sections only when you need to. Wrong section + right decision still earns the decision points; the justification points reward label literacy.

**Teacher notes:** The high-value teaching moments are the cases where students get the decision right but cite the wrong section — that means they are pattern-matching on patient features ("elderly + renal" -> "no") rather than actually reading the label. Pull up one such case in debrief and walk the class through Use-in-Specific-Populations vs. Contraindications vs. Warnings — three sections that often look interchangeable to novices but reflect different regulatory weights.

### Jargon Decoder

```yaml
name: Jargon Decoder
format: build_and_critique
supports:
  primary: 127.403-c2C
  secondary: [127.403-c2F]
duration_min: 12
mode: single_player
equipment: one_device_per_student
learning_goal: >
  Across 4 dense consult notes or radiology reports, correctly infer
  the meaning of >= 8 of 10 underlined unknown terms per document
  using context before any definition reveal, with at least 2 documents
  reaching >= 8/10.
vocab_exercised:
  - Medical terminology
  - Primary vs. secondary source
  - Package insert / Prescribing Information (PI)
content_model: passage_with_tagged_terms
content:
  document_pool_size: 12
  documents_per_session: 4
  terms_per_document: 10
  document_genres:
    - radiology_report
    - cardiology_consult
    - operative_note
    - pathology_report
  each_document_provides:
    - rendered_document_text
    - underlined_term_spans      # 10 per document
    - gold_definition_per_term   # one-line plain-language definition
    - acceptable_synonyms        # auto-grading allowance per term
  documents: []                  # populate with 12 entries matching the schema above. PLACEHOLDER.
  dimension_rubric:
    semantic_match:
      5_example: "<student answer captures the meaning AND the clinical significance of the term>"
      3_example: "<student answer captures meaning but misses the clinical implication>"
      1_example: "<student answer is wrong, vague, or copies the jargon itself>"
      guidance: "score 5 only when both the plain-language meaning and the 'why it matters' are present; 3 if only the meaning; 1 otherwise"
initial_state:
  current_document_index: 0
  terms_visible_underlined: true
  guess_input_disabled_until_term_focused: true
controls:
  - click_term                   # opens a small inline guess box
  - type_guess
  - submit_guess
  - reveal_definition            # only available after submit_guess
  - skip_term
  - next_document
feedback:
  moment: per_term
  on_keyword_match:              # gold definition keywords or acceptable synonyms
    - green ring on the term inline
    - definition revealed beneath in muted type for confirmation
  on_partial_match:              # one of the keyword set matches
    - amber ring; gold definition shown beside the guess
  on_no_match:
    - red ring; gold definition revealed; guess kept beside for self-comparison
scoring:
  guess_full_match: +1
  guess_partial_match: +0.5
  reveal_without_guess: 0
  skip: 0
  mastery_threshold: 8       # per document
  session_mastery: 2 of 4 documents at >= 8/10
win_condition:
  type: 4_documents_completed
  mastery: 2 of 4 documents at >= 8/10
reset:
  clears: [guesses, current_document, scores]
  preserves: [document_pool, completed_documents]
student_view: >
  One clinical document fills the screen in a single column; underlined
  terms are slightly thicker weight; clicking opens a small inline guess
  box just under the term (no modal). Document genre and progress
  counter top-right; a small "guesses scored / total" tally bottom.
teacher_view: >
  Per-term class match rate across all documents; surfaces the terms
  the class consistently cannot infer (these are the high-value
  vocabulary candidates to add to the glossary). Per-genre score
  distribution to see whether students are stronger on radiology vs.
  pathology vs. operative notes.
accessibility:
  - Tab to walk between underlined terms; Enter to open guess box; Esc to close
  - Underline styling paired with a small icon, not weight alone
  - Guess input is a standard textarea — screen-reader friendly
  - Screen reader announces the term, the surrounding sentence, and the focus state
success_criteria: Student clears 2 of 4 documents at >= 8/10 within one class period.
tech_stack:
  base: html+css+js
  libraries:
    - name: Tailwind CSS (Play CDN)
      cdn_url: https://cdn.tailwindcss.com
      purpose: Single-column document layout, inline guess-box styling
    - name: Alpine.js
      cdn_url: https://unpkg.com/alpinejs@3.x.x/dist/cdn.min.js
      purpose: Term focus state, guess-box lifecycle, scoring, document progression
ai_required:
  - category: text_rubric_scoring
    reason: "Scoring a student's in-their-own-words guess of a clinical term against a gold definition requires semantic-similarity judgment, not string match."
    fallback: "Accept any submission, reveal the gold definition, and log the student's guess for instructor review rather than auto-grade."
    dimensions: [semantic_match]
    deterministic_dimensions: []
    prompt_template: text-rubric-v1
    output_schema:
      scores: map<dimension, int 0-5>
      diagnostics: map<dimension, string one-line>
    model: claude-haiku-4-5
    max_tokens: 200
visual_notes: >
  Real-document aesthetic — fixed-width body, slight monospace nod for
  the document header (patient ID, date, ordering provider) so it feels
  like a chart excerpt. Underlines are subtle; the inline guess box
  appears flush under the term with a thin connector line, not a
  floating tooltip. Definition reveals in a muted gray to keep the
  student's guess visually dominant for self-comparison. No modals,
  no celebratory animations.
```

**Instructions to student:** Every underlined term is one you may not know. Click it, type your best guess from context — the surrounding sentence almost always tells you something. Submit before you reveal the definition; revealing without guessing earns no credit. A partial-keyword match still earns half a point, so do not be afraid to commit to an approximate meaning. The point is to build the inference reflex.

**Teacher notes:** The reflex this drill builds is *infer first, look up second* — the opposite of the lookup-first habit students fall into when documents feel intimidating. Watch the per-term class match rate in the teacher view. Terms with very low match rates are signal: either the surrounding context truly does not give it away (in which case they belong in the glossary you teach explicitly) or the class is collectively skipping a contextual cue you can teach in 60 seconds. Either way, those are the highest-value debrief targets.
