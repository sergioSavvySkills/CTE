# National Career Clusters Framework — Taxonomy

## Source

Advance CTE, *Guidebook: The Modernized National Career Clusters® Framework*
(updated April 2025, published June 2025). Authoritative PDF is kept in
`source/Guidebook-National-Career-Clusters-Framework.pdf` alongside this
file. All structure below is copied verbatim from that document — cluster
names, sub-cluster names, and groupings are Advance CTE's.

Contact: <careerclusters@careertech.org> · <https://careertech.org>

## Counts

- **6 Cluster Groupings** — large purpose-driven meta-sectors
- **14 Career Clusters** — industry sectors (11 sector-specific + 3 cross-cutting)
- **72 Sub-Clusters** — major groupings of career areas within a given field
- **3 Cross-Cutting Clusters** — Digital Technology, Management &
  Entrepreneurship, Marketing & Sales — apply across all other clusters
- **12 Career Ready Practices** — employability skills (not enumerated in
  this file; see the guidebook)

## Cluster Groupings (6)

The 14 Clusters organize into 6 meta-sectors. Cross-cutting clusters are
grouped under *Connecting & Supporting Success* and apply across every
other cluster.

## Notes on Usage

- **Spine** — this is the taxonomic spine for the atom library. Every atom
  ultimately tags back to one or more of these 72 Sub-Clusters.
- **Cross-cutting clusters** — Digital Technology, Management &
  Entrepreneurship, and Marketing & Sales have implications for content
  taken in all other clusters. Expect many atoms to tag a sector-specific
  cluster *and* a cross-cutting cluster (e.g., a healthcare-informatics
  atom tags Healthcare & Human Services + Digital Technology).
- **State data is still on the legacy 16** — when we ingest state CTE
  frameworks and IBC lists in Phase 3, we'll crosswalk them to this
  modernized spine using the table above.

## Not in This File (by design)

- Cluster *definitions* (one paragraph per cluster) — see pp. 5–18 of the
  source guidebook.
- *Example Programs of Study* per Sub-Cluster — also in the guidebook,
  per cluster. Those are illustrative, not normative; they change as
  states update their course catalogs.
- The **12 Career Ready Practices** — treated separately; they are
  employability skills that apply across every cluster.
- *Original cluster pathways* from the legacy 16-cluster framework
  (79 pathways) — not brought forward; we work against the modernized 72
  Sub-Cluster spine.
