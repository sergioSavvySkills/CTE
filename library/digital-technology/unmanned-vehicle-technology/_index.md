# Digital Technology → Unmanned Vehicle Technology

## Scope (Advance CTE definition)

> *"Careers related to the development, operation, and use of unmanned
> vehicles, such as unmanned aerial vehicles, drones, and autonomous
> ground vehicles, across various sectors. Applications include aerial
> surveying, precision farming, search and rescue, delivery services,
> resource management, hazardous environment remediation, and
> infrastructure inspection. Professionals in this field engage in
> design, programming, data analysis, and operational management."*

Source: *Guidebook: The Modernized National Career Clusters Framework*,
Advance CTE, April 2025 (p. 5).

**Example programs of study:** Drone Technology, Unmanned Aircraft
Systems.

## Provenance & verification status

- Canonical data lives in `unmanned-vehicle-technology.csv` alongside
  this file.
- Researched against **7 states**: CA, TX, FL, NY, PA, OH, IL.
- This is the **most cross-cutting** Digital Technology sub-cluster —
  drones appear in Agriculture (precision ag), Arts (aerial photography),
  Construction (site surveys), Energy (infrastructure inspection), Public
  Safety (SAR), and Transportation (air cargo).
- **Smallest credential set** in Digital Technology (~14 credentials).
  The sub-cluster is dominated by one flagship federal license (FAA
  Part 107) and one ASTM-aligned industry cert (USI sUAS Safety).
- Texas and Ohio have the strongest confirmed state-level UAS credential
  recognition. CA, NY, PA, IL have not yet added drone-specific
  credentials to centralized lists.

## Anchor recommendations

**Two-credential anchor stack** (this sub-cluster is narrower than
others — two anchors cover the full HS pathway):

1. **FAA Part 107 Remote Pilot Certificate** — the undisputed flagship.
   Every CTE drone program in the country targets this. Federal license
   required for all commercial drone operations. Age 16+ means HS
   juniors/seniors can sit it. TX IBC confirmed. Cross-cutting across
   Agriculture, Construction, Energy, Public Safety, Arts, and
   Transportation. **Primary anchor.**

2. **USI Small UAS Safety Certification — Level 1** — the strongest
   HS-appropriate proctored industry cert that is NOT a federal license.
   Ohio IWIP confirmed at 6 pts. ASTM-aligned (F3266, F3330). 70-item
   proctored exam. 150-hour prep course aligns to a semester-length
   CTE course. **Secondary anchor, especially for 10th/11th grade
   before students are Part 107 age-eligible.**

**Student organization alignment:**
- TSA Drone Challenge (UAV) — MS and HS divisions.
- SkillsUSA Commercial sUAS Drone Competition — team event.
- RECF Aerial Drone Competition — ages 10-18, introductory.

## State research notes

- **Texas:** FAA Part 107 confirmed on 2025-2030 IBC list (Tier 2),
  aligned to "Drone (Unmanned Vehicle)" program of study. Also
  references an "Aerial Mapping and 3D Modeling Certification" as
  stackable IBC — needs verification against Pix4D.

- **Florida:** USI sUAS Safety (USINS001) found in articulation
  agreements linked to Professional Pilot Technology A.S. Likely on
  Master Credentials List. FAA Part 107 CAPE status unconfirmed.

- **Ohio:** **Strongest confirmed recognition.** USI sUAS Safety L1
  (CR67, 6 pt), USI VSO Ground (CR69, 6 pt), USI VSO Flight (CR68,
  6 pt) — all on IWIP and TechCred eligible. USI/PAST Foundation
  partnership drives adoption.

- **California:** No state-level UAS credential list. Community colleges
  offer UAS programs targeting Part 107 but these are institutional,
  not state-standardized.

- **New York:** No UAS credentials on any NYSED-approved CTE list.
  Individual schools seek NYSED program approval independently.

- **Pennsylvania:** No UAS credentials found on PDE-approved list.

- **Illinois:** No drone-specific credentials confirmed on ISBE list.
