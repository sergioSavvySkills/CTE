## Learning objective

Students will be able to explain how computers represent data in binary and convert small values between decimal and binary.

## Content stub

- **Bit:** binary digit: 0 or 1; the smallest unit; electrically an off/on state
- **Byte:** 8 bits = 256 possible values; enough for a single ASCII character or a small number
- **Binary numbers:** place values: 128, 64, 32, 16, 8, 4, 2, 1 (right to left)
- **Converting decimal to binary:** find highest power of 2 that fits, subtract, repeat
- **Bigger units:** KB, MB, GB, TB — each ~1,000 of the prior (or 1,024 in strict 'binary' units)
- **Why this matters for AI:** everything — images, audio, text — is ultimately bits; model sizes in GB mean billions of bytes

## Assessment stub

- Convert: 13 in decimal = ? in binary; 1010 in binary = ? in decimal
- Lab: use Python bin() and int() to check your conversions
- Short answer: why is a 'byte' 8 bits and not, say, 10?
