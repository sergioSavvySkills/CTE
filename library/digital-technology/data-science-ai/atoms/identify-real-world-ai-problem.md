## Learning objective

Students will be able to identify real community problems that AI is well-suited to address.

## Content stub

- **Start with pain points:** what frustrates people around you? long wait times, repeated questions, sorting work, forecasting
- **Check AI fit criteria:** pattern recognition on data, repetitive judgment, or scale beyond human capacity
- **Constraint check:** is data available? is the harm of errors acceptable? do stakeholders consent?
- **Community lens:** problems in your school, neighborhood, family life — you already understand the context
- **Scoping the problem:** 'predict exact traffic' is too big; 'estimate cafeteria line length at lunch' is tractable
- **Check prior art:** search for existing solutions first; don't reinvent something that already works

## Assessment stub

- Brainstorm: list 5 problems in your school that might benefit from AI
- Scope: pick one and narrow it to a 4-week project
- Short answer: why is narrowing scope a survival skill for student AI projects?
