## Learning objective

Students will be able to inventory the kinds of data internet users collect and share, and link each to a real-world AI use.

## Content stub

- **Text data:** messages, posts, search queries, reviews, documents — powers NLP, search, sentiment
- **Image and video data:** photos, social uploads, livestreams, surveillance — powers CV, facial recognition, content moderation
- **Audio data:** calls, voice assistants, songs — powers speech-to-text, voice cloning, music recommendation
- **Behavioral data:** clicks, scrolls, dwell time, purchases — powers recommendation and advertising engines
- **Location data:** GPS traces, check-ins, map queries — powers navigation, rideshare, geomarketing
- **Sensor and IoT data:** wearables, smart-home devices, industrial sensors — powers fitness AI and predictive maintenance

## Assessment stub

- Inventory exercise: list 10 pieces of data your phone has collected about you in the last hour
- Map: for each of the 6 data types, name one AI product that uses it
- Reflection: which kind of data about yourself are you most protective of, and why?
