## Learning objective

Students will be able to evaluate data sources for reliability, recency, and suitability and identify good repositories for ML datasets.

## Content stub

- **Source credibility:** who collected the data, with what methodology, how recent, is it updated regularly?
- **Licensing:** what can you legally do with the data — commercial, research, attribution required?
- **Coverage and representativeness:** does the sample cover your use case? any demographic or geographic gaps?
- **Popular dataset repositories:** Kaggle, UCI ML Repository, Hugging Face Datasets, Google Dataset Search, government open-data portals
- **Red flags:** no source citation, no license, broken download links, outdated, proprietary behind login

## Assessment stub

- Audit: pick a Kaggle dataset and evaluate it across the 5 criteria
- Short answer: why might an otherwise-great dataset be inappropriate for commercial use?
- Research: find one government open-data portal and list 3 datasets it publishes
