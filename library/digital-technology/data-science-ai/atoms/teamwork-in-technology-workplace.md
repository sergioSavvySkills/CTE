## Learning objective

Students will be able to work both independently and collaboratively as a team member in technology settings, applying skills that contribute to shared deliverables.

## Content stub

- **Independent work skills:** self-direction on assigned tasks, knowing when to proceed and when to ask; managing your own blockers; producing work that does not require rework
- **Team roles:** typical tech-team roles including leads, individual contributors, product, design, QA; how responsibilities overlap on small teams
- **Collaboration tools:** shared documents, version control, issue trackers, stand-ups and retrospectives; async vs. synchronous communication norms
- **Constructive conflict:** separating ideas from people, disagreeing without personal attacks, committing to a decision once made even if you argued against it
- **Giving and receiving feedback:** specific and actionable feedback, code and design reviews, receiving critique without defensiveness
- **Shared accountability:** celebrating wins together, sharing credit, addressing failures as a team rather than seeking individual blame

## Assessment stub

- Group project rubric: students complete a two-session team task and rate their own and peers' contributions against a shared rubric
- Role-play: mediate a disagreement between two teammates about which technical approach to use; document the resolution process
- Reflection: describe one situation where independent work was appropriate and one where teamwork was essential, with reasoning
