## Learning objective

Students will be able to identify ethical issues in data science work and describe mitigation strategies.

## Content stub

- **Consent and privacy:** was the data collected with informed consent? can individuals opt out? PII handling
- **Fairness:** does the dataset represent all groups? are analyses sensitive to protected attributes?
- **Reproducibility:** can others verify your work? document code, data versions, random seeds
- **Impact of findings:** will publishing this analysis cause harm to any group? weigh harms vs. benefits
- **Conflicts of interest:** who funded the analysis? disclose stakes; data can be cherry-picked under pressure
- **Dual-use:** analyses that help also can be weaponized — anticipate misuse

## Assessment stub

- Analyze: describe the ethics issue in a real case (Cambridge Analytica, Target-pregnancy-prediction, etc.)
- Design: write 5 ethical guidelines for a student-data analytics project
- Short answer: why is 'anonymized' data often not truly anonymous?
