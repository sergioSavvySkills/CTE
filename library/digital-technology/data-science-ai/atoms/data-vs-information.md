## Learning objective

Students will be able to distinguish raw data from actionable information and explain what context adds.

## Content stub

- **Data:** raw, unorganized facts and measurements — a list of numbers with no labels is data
- **Information:** data placed in a context that makes it useful — same numbers labeled 'weekly sales, Store 5' is information
- **The transformation:** adding labels, units, relationships, or comparisons converts data into information
- **Hierarchy:** data → information → knowledge → wisdom (DIKW pyramid); each layer adds interpretation
- **Why AI needs both:** models train on raw data but produce outputs that are information (predictions in context)

## Assessment stub

- Given 3 pieces of raw data, turn each into information by adding context
- Short answer: how do labels in a dataset convert data to information?
- Analyze: review a news chart and identify what context makes the numbers meaningful
