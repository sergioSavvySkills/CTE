## Learning objective

Students will be able to present a professional image through appropriate appearance, behavior, and language in workplace and interview contexts.

## Content stub

- **Appearance and dress:** industry norms across tech roles (startup casual, business casual, client-facing formal); grooming basics; dressing one level above the daily norm for interviews and presentations
- **Professional behavior:** handshakes and greetings, meeting etiquette, being on time and prepared, phone and device etiquette during meetings
- **Professional language:** avoiding slang and profanity in workplace settings, using precise technical vocabulary, code-switching between peer banter and client conversations
- **Digital presence:** LinkedIn profile basics, GitHub and portfolio hygiene, keeping personal social media content compatible with professional goals
- **Email and messaging etiquette:** subject lines that summarize, signatures, response-time expectations, and how tone reads differently in text than in person
- **Representing the organization:** understanding that once on the job you speak for the employer in emails, posts, and public events; brand and confidentiality awareness

## Assessment stub

- Interview-ready self-check: students submit a photo or description of interview attire with a short explanation of why it fits the role
- Digital-footprint audit: review own public social profiles against a professional-image checklist and list three changes
- Email rewrite: given an overly casual email to a manager, students rewrite it for tone, clarity, and professionalism
