## Learning objective

Students will be able to research a potential AI project, assess feasibility, and decide whether AI is the right tool.

## Content stub

- **User research:** interview stakeholders: what do they do today, what's frustrating, what would 10x better look like
- **Assess AI fit:** does this problem involve pattern recognition on data, or logic that's hard to code directly? if yes, AI may help
- **Anti-AI signal:** if a simple rule works, if data is sparse, if errors are catastrophic — rethink using AI
- **Market research:** what already exists? is there a commercial tool or open-source project you could adopt/adapt?
- **Feasibility check:** do you have the data, compute, and skills? what's the MVP you could build in 2 weeks?
- **Writing a problem statement:** Target user + current pain + AI intervention + success metric — all in 2-3 sentences

## Assessment stub

- Research: pick a problem you care about and research whether AI has been tried for it
- Draft: write a 3-sentence problem statement for an AI project at your school
- Short answer: name 2 problems where AI is a bad fit and explain why
