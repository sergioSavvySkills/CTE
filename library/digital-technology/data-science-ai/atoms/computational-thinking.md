## Learning objective

Students will be able to apply the four pillars of computational thinking to break down complex problems.

## Content stub

- **Decomposition:** split a big problem into smaller sub-problems that can be solved independently
- **Pattern recognition:** notice similarities across problems; reuse solutions from similar problems already solved
- **Abstraction:** identify the essential features; ignore irrelevant details; focus on what matters for the solution
- **Algorithms:** design a step-by-step process that solves the problem reliably
- **CT in AI:** AI design uses all four: decompose a big AI system, recognize patterns in data, abstract the key features, design ML algorithm
- **Beyond coding:** CT applies to cooking, music, sports strategy — anywhere you need to solve problems systematically

## Assessment stub

- Apply: use all 4 pillars to plan a homework-reminder app
- Non-code example: apply CT to planning a school event
- Short answer: why is abstraction critical when working with large datasets?
