## Learning objective

Students will be able to apply critical-thinking and problem-solving strategies to locate, analyze, and evaluate career and postsecondary information.

## Content stub

- **Structured problem solving:** defining the decision, identifying options, gathering evidence, weighing trade-offs, and committing to a next step rather than a forever choice
- **Locating reliable career information:** O*NET, Bureau of Labor Statistics, state labor market data, GeorgiaBEST and GAfutures, professional association sites; distinguishing primary data from marketing copy
- **Analyzing job postings:** reading required vs. preferred qualifications, decoding salary ranges, identifying repeated skills across postings in a target role
- **Evaluating postsecondary options:** cost vs. outcome comparisons for technical college, four-year degree, apprenticeship, and certification paths; total cost including time and opportunity cost
- **Avoiding reasoning traps:** anchoring on the first job seen, confirmation bias, survivorship stories ('my cousin skipped college'), and making long-term decisions from small samples
- **Decision frameworks:** pros-cons lists, weighted criteria matrices, and setting review checkpoints so a plan can be revisited with new information

## Assessment stub

- Career comparison matrix: students analyze three roles in the AI/data pathway across pay, education, growth, and day-to-day tasks using cited sources
- Job-posting teardown: given two real postings for the same title, students list what is identical, what differs, and what to infer about each employer
- Decision write-up: students draft a one-page plan for their next 24 months with at least two alternative paths and the conditions under which they would switch
