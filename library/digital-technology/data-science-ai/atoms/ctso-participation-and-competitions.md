## Learning objective

Students will be able to describe CTSO competitive events, leadership development opportunities, and community-service activities, and plan their own participation.

## Content stub

- **Levels of competition:** region or district, state conferences, and national conferences; how students qualify to advance and typical timelines across the school year
- **IT and CS competitive events:** coding, cybersecurity, networking, website design, mobile app development, IT fundamentals, and presentation or business-plan events that use technology
- **Preparing for competition:** studying event guidelines, practicing under time, reviewing rubrics, and iterating on a submission with advisor and peer feedback
- **Leadership development:** chapter officer roles, running meetings, parliamentary procedure basics, and leadership academies or institutes offered by state and national organizations
- **Community service and civic engagement:** chapter service projects, partnering with local nonprofits, tech-for-good initiatives such as device refurbishing or digital-literacy tutoring
- **Planning your year:** mapping competitive events, leadership roles, and service hours onto the school calendar; setting goals and tracking progress with your advisor

## Assessment stub

- Event plan: students pick one IT-focused CTSO competitive event, read its guidelines, and outline a preparation timeline with milestones
- Leadership reflection: describe an officer role or leadership activity of interest and the three skills it would build
- Service proposal: draft a one-page chapter community-service project including goal, audience, tasks, and success measure
