## Learning objective

Students will be able to demonstrate creativity and innovation by asking meaningful questions and exploring new methods for completing workplace tasks.

## Content stub

- **Curiosity as a skill:** asking 'why does this process exist?' and 'what would we do if we started from scratch?'; treating questions as contributions rather than challenges
- **Divergent vs. convergent thinking:** brainstorming broadly before narrowing; deferring judgment during idea generation; using techniques like mind maps and 'five whys'
- **Innovative work methods:** identifying repetitive tasks that could be automated, streamlined, or redesigned; suggesting small process improvements without waiting for permission
- **Idea pitching:** framing an idea with the problem it solves, the proposed change, and the expected benefit; anticipating objections and costs
- **Learning from failure:** treating experiments that don't work as data, documenting what was learned, and iterating rather than abandoning
- **Innovation and ethics:** considering who is affected by a change, whether new methods introduce bias or risk, and balancing novelty with reliability

## Assessment stub

- Idea journal: over one week, students log three workplace or classroom processes they would change and why
- Brainstorm exercise: in small groups, generate ten ways to improve a familiar task in three minutes, then pick the most promising and explain the choice
- One-minute pitch: students present an innovative idea following problem/proposal/benefit structure to the class
