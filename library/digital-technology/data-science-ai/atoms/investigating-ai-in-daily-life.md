## Learning objective

Students will be able to investigate a daily-use AI product and determine which type(s) of AI it relies on.

## Content stub

- **Evidence-gathering:** look at the product's documentation, academic papers from the team, reverse-engineer from behavior
- **Pattern: social media:** recommender systems = supervised + RL; content moderation = supervised CV + NLP
- **Pattern: voice assistants:** wake word = CV-on-audio; command understanding = NLP; response = GenAI + retrieval
- **Pattern: smartphones:** camera scene detection = CV; autocorrect = NLP; battery prediction = regression
- **Pattern: shopping:** product recommendations = collaborative filtering (unsupervised-ish); price prediction = regression
- **Journalistic skills:** read the Terms of Service, privacy policy, and help pages — often tells you what AI they use

## Assessment stub

- Investigate: pick 3 apps you use daily and identify the AI types they likely use, with evidence
- Research: read one major platform's AI transparency page and summarize
- Short answer: why might a company be coy about exactly what AI they're using?
