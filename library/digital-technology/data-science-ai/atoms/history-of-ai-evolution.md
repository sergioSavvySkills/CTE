## Learning objective

Students will be able to describe key eras and milestones in AI history and identify important early contributors.

## Content stub

- **1950s origins:** Turing Test (1950), Dartmouth Workshop (1956) where the term 'AI' was coined, early optimism
- **Symbolic AI era (1960s-70s):** rule-based expert systems, ELIZA chatbot, logic theorem-provers; first AI Winter late 70s
- **Knowledge-based era (1980s):** expert systems in industry; second AI Winter late 80s when promises exceeded delivery
- **Machine-learning era (1990s-2010s):** statistical methods, IBM Deep Blue beats Kasparov (1997), IBM Watson wins Jeopardy (2011)
- **Deep-learning + LLM era (2012-now):** AlexNet (2012), AlphaGo (2016), GPT-3 (2020), ChatGPT (2022), multimodal GenAI (2024+)
- **Key contributors:** Alan Turing, John McCarthy, Marvin Minsky, Geoffrey Hinton, Yann LeCun, Yoshua Bengio, Fei-Fei Li

## Assessment stub

- Timeline exercise: place 8 events on a timeline labeled 1950-2026
- Match 5 contributors to their signature contributions
- Short answer: what caused the 'AI Winters' and what ended them?
