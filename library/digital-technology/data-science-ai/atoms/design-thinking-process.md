## Learning objective

Students will be able to explain the five phases of Design Thinking and apply them to a real-world problem.

## Content stub

- **Empathize:** interview users, observe behavior, walk in their shoes; goal is understanding not solving
- **Define:** synthesize findings into a clear problem statement: '(user) needs (need) because (insight)'
- **Ideate:** brainstorm broadly without judgment; quantity before quality; then converge on the top 3
- **Prototype:** build a rough, cheap version to test the idea — paper, clickable mockup, or MVP
- **Test:** put the prototype in front of real users; observe; gather feedback; iterate
- **Iterative, not linear:** you loop back — testing often sends you back to redefine the problem

## Assessment stub

- Lab: apply the 5 phases to 'improve how students check homework assignments' — produce 5 artifacts
- Critique: given a failed product, identify which DT phase was skipped
- Short answer: why do designers say 'fall in love with the problem, not the solution'?
