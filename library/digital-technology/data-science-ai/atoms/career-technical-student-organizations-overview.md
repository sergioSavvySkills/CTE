## Learning objective

Students will be able to explain the purpose, mission, and goals of Career and Technical Student Organizations (CTSOs) and identify those aligned with the Information Technology pathway.

## Content stub

- **What a CTSO is:** a co-curricular student organization that extends CTE classroom learning through competitions, leadership, and service; recognized at local, state, and national levels
- **Mission and goals:** developing technical skills, leadership, citizenship, and career readiness; integrating CTSO activities into course content rather than treating them as extracurriculars
- **CTSOs for IT and computer science:** FBLA (Future Business Leaders of America) and TSA (Technology Student Association) are the primary CTSOs for the Georgia IT pathway; both run technology- and computer-science-focused events
- **Membership structure:** chapter-level officers and advisors, state associations, and national organization; dues, meetings, and typical yearly calendar
- **Benefits to students:** scholarships, recognition, networking with industry and postsecondary partners, resume and college-application credentials
- **Integration with coursework:** CTSO projects that align with class standards, teachers as advisors, and how participation reinforces employability skills covered throughout the course

## Assessment stub

- Compare-and-contrast: students build a table of FBLA and TSA showing mission, relevant IT/CS events, membership cost, and typical activities
- Short response: explain in 150 words why a CTSO is considered co-curricular rather than extracurricular
- Interest inventory: students rank five CTSO activity types (competition, leadership, service, travel, networking) and justify their top choice
