## Learning objective

Students will be able to exhibit work-readiness skills including integrity, punctuality, time management, and respect for diversity in professional settings.

## Content stub

- **Integrity and ethics:** honesty in reporting progress, admitting mistakes, following licensing and data-privacy rules, and the long-term cost of shortcuts
- **Punctuality and attendance:** arriving prepared for meetings and shifts, communicating early when delays are unavoidable, and understanding how reliability compounds into trust
- **Time management:** prioritizing with urgent/important framing, breaking work into estimable chunks, using calendars and task lists, and protecting focus time
- **Respect for diversity:** working effectively with colleagues of different backgrounds, perspectives, abilities, and communication styles; inclusive language; recognizing bias
- **Accountability:** owning outcomes of your work, following through on commitments, and escalating early when you cannot deliver
- **Workplace policies:** dress code, acceptable-use, harassment and equal-opportunity policies; knowing how to report concerns and who the trusted channels are

## Assessment stub

- Scenario analysis: for five short workplace scenarios (missed deadline, dishonest peer, exclusionary comment), students describe a professional response
- Time-log exercise: students track one school day in 30-minute blocks and identify two time-management improvements
- Self-assessment rubric: students rate themselves on eight professional traits and set one measurable goal for the semester
