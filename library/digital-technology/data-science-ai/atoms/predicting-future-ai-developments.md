## Learning objective

Students will be able to apply structured forecasting methods to make defensible predictions about AI developments.

## Content stub

- **Forecasting methods:** extrapolate current trends; reference class forecasting; scenario planning; Delphi panels
- **Base rates:** how long have similar technologies taken to reach 10M / 100M / 1B users? use these as baselines
- **Driver analysis:** what forces accelerate (compute, capital, talent) vs. slow (regulation, energy, trust) a given prediction
- **Calibration:** track your predictions with confidence intervals; review and update as evidence comes in
- **Avoiding pitfalls:** beware of confident-sounding timelines; watch for motivated reasoning; diversify your sources

## Assessment stub

- Make 3 predictions for 2027 with explicit probabilities (e.g., '70% chance X happens')
- Identify one prediction from 2020-2022 about AI that aged well and one that aged poorly; explain why
- Design: build a one-page scenario for 'the best-case 2030 AI world' and 'the worst-case'
