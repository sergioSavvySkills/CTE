## Learning objective

Students will be able to define artificial intelligence, distinguish it from ordinary software, and reflect on the current state of the field.

## Content stub

- **Definition:** AI = computer systems that perform tasks normally requiring human intelligence: perception, reasoning, learning, decision-making, language
- **Narrow vs general AI:** narrow (task-specific, today's reality) vs general (human-level across domains, research goal) vs super (speculative)
- **How AI differs from normal software:** rules are learned from data rather than hand-coded; systems adapt and improve with more data
- **Current state (2026):** large language models, image/video generation, autonomous systems, AI copilots in every industry
- **Limits of current AI:** brittle outside training data, hallucinations, high compute cost, bias inherited from data, no true understanding

## Assessment stub

- Short answer: write a 3-sentence definition of AI in your own words
- Classify 6 example systems (calculator, Netflix recommender, thermostat, ChatGPT, self-driving car, spell-check) as 'AI' / 'not AI' / 'borderline' with justification
- Reflection: name one AI tool you used this week and what it did for you
