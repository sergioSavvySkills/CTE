## Learning objective

Students will be able to define and identify bias, perception, privacy, and accuracy issues in AI systems.

## Content stub

- **Bias in AI:** systematic unfairness — dataset bias, historical bias, measurement bias, selection bias
- **Perception:** how AI 'sees' the world can differ from reality (e.g., facial-recognition error rates higher for some groups)
- **Privacy:** what info does the AI collect, store, share? can it re-identify anonymized data?
- **Accuracy:** how often is the model right? breaks down by group — global accuracy can hide disparities
- **Real examples:** COMPAS recidivism scores, Amazon résumé screener, facial recognition audits

## Assessment stub

- Match: given 4 real AI incidents, identify which issue (bias / perception / privacy / accuracy) each illustrates and explain in one sentence
- Short answer: why can a model be 'accurate overall' and still be unfair to a specific group?
