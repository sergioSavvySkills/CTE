## Learning objective

Students will be able to define Big Data using the '5 Vs' and explain why modern AI is data-hungry.

## Content stub

- **Volume:** terabytes to petabytes; web-scale datasets like Common Crawl, YouTube corpora
- **Velocity:** streaming data from sensors, clicks, financial feeds — must process in near real-time
- **Variety:** mixed formats (text + image + sensor) that require different tools to unify
- **Veracity:** quality and trustworthiness; noisy data is everywhere; cleaning is 80% of DS work
- **Value:** the payoff — what business/societal decisions the data unlocks
- **Why AI needs Big Data:** modern ML models have billions of parameters; they need billions of examples to generalize

## Assessment stub

- Apply: pick a product (e.g., Netflix) and describe its data through each of the 5 Vs
- Short answer: why can't a deep-learning model trained on 100 images compete with one trained on 10 million?
- Research: find one real dataset with stated size (rows, GB) and classify each V
