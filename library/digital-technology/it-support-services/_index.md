# Digital Technology → IT Support & Services

## Scope (Advance CTE definition)

> *"Careers focused on providing setup, assistance, and problem-solving
> solutions for software, hardware, and other technology-related issues
> to ensure smooth and efficient operation of communication and data
> systems for individuals and organizations. This field is essential in
> maintaining, supporting, and optimizing technological infrastructure,
> ensuring minimal disruption and maximum efficiency."*

Source: *Guidebook: The Modernized National Career Clusters Framework*,
Advance CTE, April 2025 (p. 5).

**Example program of study** (Advance CTE): Information Support & Services.

**What belongs here, what doesn't:** helpdesk, desktop/end-user support,
hardware/software install and troubleshoot, basic security from the
end-user angle. **Does NOT include** network administration (→ Network
Systems & Cybersecurity), software development (→ Software Solutions),
cloud architecture (→ Web & Cloud), or data/AI (→ Data Science & AI).

## Full detail

### CompTIA IT Fundamentals+ *(ITF+)*
- **Issuer:** CompTIA
- **Grade band:** HS (often 9th/10th grade)
- **Exam format:** CBT, 75 items, 60 min, multiple choice
- **Current version:** FC0-U61
- **Version effective:** 2018-09
- **Objectives source:** <https://www.comptia.org/certifications/it-fundamentals>
- **Objective count:** ~80 across 6 domains (IT Concepts & Terminology,
  Infrastructure, Applications & Software, Software Development,
  Database Fundamentals, Security)
- **State status:** Texas IBC · Florida CAPE · Ohio IRC (3/3, 2024–25)
- **Anchor candidate:** **yes** — the "Should I go into IT?" decision
  credential. Fits the 9th/10th-grade opening-year pattern of most
  state IT pathways.
- **Notes:** Pre-A+ entry credential; light on hands-on, heavy on
  concepts. Complements the Google IT Support Certificate as a
  second-year option.

### CompTIA A+
- **Issuer:** CompTIA
- **Grade band:** Both (HS senior + PS)
- **Exam format:** CBT, two exams (Core 1 + Core 2), performance-based
  items + multiple choice
- **Current version:** 220-1201 / 220-1202
- **Version effective:** 2025-09 (replaced 220-1101 / 220-1102)
- **Objectives source:** <https://www.comptia.org/certifications/a>
- **Objective count:** ~270 across 4 domains per exam
- **State status:** Texas IBC · Florida CAPE · Ohio IRC (3/3, 2024–25)
- **Anchor candidate:** **yes** — the flagship IT Support & Services
  anchor. Most-prepped IT credential in U.S. CTE. If we pick a single
  anchor for this sub-cluster, it's this.
- **Notes:** Revises every ~3 years; expect next revision ~2028.
  Covers networking and security fundamentals at the end-user-support
  level, which is why some teachers conflate it with Network+ — keep
  scope clean.

### Google IT Support Professional Certificate
- **Issuer:** Google (delivered via Coursera)
- **Grade band:** Both (HS often via dual-enrollment)
- **Exam format:** Course completion + graded assessments across 5
  courses (*not* a single proctored exam)
- **Current version:** rolling — content updated continuously
- **Version effective:** rolling
- **Objectives source:** <https://www.coursera.org/professional-certificates/google-it-support>
- **Objective count:** 5 courses, each multi-week with module
  assessments; objectives not enumerated like CompTIA
- **State status:** Texas IBC · Florida CAPE · Ohio IRC (3/3, 2024–25)
- **Anchor candidate:** **yes** (strong complementary anchor) — stackable
  to CompTIA A+. Strong employer recognition through Google Employer
  Consortium.
- **Notes:** Course-completion shape differs structurally from
  CompTIA's objective-list shape; objective alignment is fuzzier. If
  anchored, treat as syllabus-based rather than objective-based.

### Cisco Certified Support Technician (CCST) IT Support
- **Issuer:** Cisco
- **Grade band:** Both (designed for HS / early career)
- **Exam format:** CBT, 50 minutes
- **Current version:** CCST IT Support (single current version)
- **Version effective:** 2023 (launched)
- **Objectives source:** <https://www.cisco.com/site/us/en/learn/training-certifications/certifications/technician/it-support/index.html>
- **Objective count:** ~30–40
- **State status:** **verify** — newer credential, state-list adoption
  is growing but not consistently confirmed for 2024–25.
- **Anchor candidate:** maybe — watch adoption trajectory before
  commitment.
- **Notes:** Newer credential (2023 launch). Cisco's play for entry /
  early-career learners outside the Networking/Cybersecurity exams.
  Siblings are CCST Networking and CCST Cybersecurity, which live in
  the Network Systems & Cybersecurity sub-cluster.

### Microsoft 365 Fundamentals (MS-900)
- **Issuer:** Microsoft
- **Grade band:** Both
- **Exam format:** CBT, ~40–60 items, 45 min
- **Current version:** MS-900
- **Version effective:** 2019 (periodic content updates)
- **Objectives source:** <https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/credentials/certifications/exams/ms-900/>
- **Objective count:** ~30–40
- **State status:** Texas IBC · Florida CAPE · Ohio IRC (3/3, 2024–25)
- **Cross-cutting:** primary home is **Web & Cloud** — listed here
  because IT Support teachers frequently use MS-900 in their pathway.
- **Anchor candidate:** no (for IT Support specifically; primary home
  is Web & Cloud).
- **Notes:** Entry Microsoft 365 / cloud services fundamentals. If
  student is IT-Support-bound, A+ is the primary anchor; MS-900 is a
  useful complement.

### ITIL 4 Foundation
- **Issuer:** PeopleCert (on behalf of AXELOS)
- **Grade band:** PS (occasional HS dual-enrollment)
- **Exam format:** CBT, 40 multiple-choice, 60 min
- **Current version:** ITIL 4 Foundation
- **Version effective:** 2019-02
- **Objectives source:** <https://www.peoplecert.org/browse-certifications/it-governance-and-service-management/ITIL-1/itil-4-foundation-8>
- **Objective count:** ~30 learning outcomes across 7 domains
- **State status:** Texas IBC · Florida CAPE · **verify Ohio**
- **Cross-cutting:** Management & Entrepreneurship → Leadership &
  Operations. Service management is a management discipline as much
  as an IT one.
- **Anchor candidate:** no (PS-dominant; weak HS fit).
- **Notes:** Strong fit for two-year IT programs. In HS contexts, only
  relevant for dual-enrollment seniors.

### TestOut PC Pro
- **Issuer:** TestOut (a CompTIA company since 2022 acquisition)
- **Grade band:** HS (primarily)
- **Exam format:** Performance-based simulation + multiple choice
- **Current version:** PC Pro (continuous updates via LabSim platform)
- **Version effective:** rolling
- **Objectives source:** <https://w3.testout.com/k12/certifications/pc-pro>
- **Objective count:** ~10 domains
- **State status:** Texas IBC · Florida CAPE · Ohio IRC (3/3, 2024–25)
- **Anchor candidate:** no — treat as complement to CompTIA A+, not
  a separate anchor (post-acquisition, content alignment converges).
- **Notes:** Heavy simulation emphasis is pedagogically strong; fits
  CTE lab contexts well. If we anchor A+, TestOut's role is as the
  practice / lab software, not as a separate credential target.

## Anchor recommendations (Maya's picks)

For the IT Support & Services sub-cluster, if this becomes the first
wedge, I'd recommend a **three-credential anchor stack** rather than
single-credential anchoring:

1. **CompTIA ITF+** — 9th/10th-grade entry, covers fundamentals.
2. **CompTIA A+** — 11th/12th-grade flagship, the job-ready credential.
3. **Google IT Support Professional Certificate** — parallel/senior-year
   option, especially for students who don't sit CompTIA exams; also
   opens employer-consortium hiring.

Rationale for the stack: the typical CTE pathway runs 2–3 years. A
single anchor only covers one year of content. The stack gives us full
pathway coverage from 9th through 12th, with A+ as the primary job-ready
outcome.

**Not anchoring (for this sub-cluster):** Cisco CCST IT Support (still
too new to bet on), MS-900 (cross-cutting home is Web & Cloud), ITIL 4
(PS-only), TestOut PC Pro (treat as A+ practice lab, not standalone
anchor).

## Items to verify before any strategic commitment

1. **Cisco CCST IT Support state status** — research flagged as
   "growing"; confirm against the 2024–25 Texas IBC, Florida CAPE, and
   Ohio IRC lists.
2. **ITIL 4 Ohio IRC status** — not confirmed in research.
3. **CompTIA A+ version** — I've used 220-1201 / 220-1202 as the
   current 2025 release. If the actual Core 1/Core 2 dropped on a
   different schedule, anything we author against A+ will carry the
   wrong version tag.
4. **CompTIA ITF+ version** — FC0-U61 has been in market a long time;
   CompTIA periodically drops successor versions quietly. Verify
   FC0-U61 is still being issued and not superseded.
5. **Google IT Support course inventory** — Google periodically
   restructures their Coursera offerings; verify the 5-course structure
   is current.

For each of these, the objective-ingestion step (Phase 1) will force
verification anyway — we can't pull an objective tree for a wrong
version. So they're not blockers for this catalog, just known soft
spots.
