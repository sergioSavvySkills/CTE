## Learning objective

Students will be able to configure browser security settings to protect against common web-based threats and assist end users with browser security issues.

## Content stub

- **HTTPS and certificate warnings:** verifying padlock, certificate details, what to do when a cert is invalid, HSTS
- **Malicious site blocking:** Safe Browsing (Chrome/Firefox), SmartScreen (Edge); how they work; reporting false positives
- **Pop-up and redirect blocking:** legitimate vs. malicious pop-ups; override for trusted sites; drive-by download prevention
- **Saved passwords and autofill:** risks of browser-saved passwords; recommending a password manager instead; clearing saved data
- **Extensions security:** identifying malicious extensions; auditing permissions; enterprise-managed extension allowlist
- **Privacy settings:** third-party cookie blocking, fingerprinting protection, private mode limitations; Firefox Enhanced Tracking Protection

## Assessment stub

- Browser audit: given a browser with insecure settings, apply a hardening checklist and document each change
- Extension review: examine 3 extensions, note their permissions, and classify each as safe/concerning
- Short answer: what information can a website collect even in private/incognito mode?
