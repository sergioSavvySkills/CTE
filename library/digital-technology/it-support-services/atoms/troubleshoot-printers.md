## Learning objective

Students will be able to diagnose and resolve common printer problems including paper jams, print quality defects, and connectivity failures.

## Content stub

- **Paper jams:** locating jam (all access panels), removing paper fully without tearing, roller wear as root cause
- **Print quality issues:** faded print (low toner/ink), streaks (dirty drum/cartridge), smearing (fuser failure), wrong colors
- **Connectivity issues:** offline status, wrong IP, spooler stuck, driver mismatch between 32/64-bit
- **Laser-specific problems:** background ghosting (drum worn), lines (drum scratch), toner not fusing (fuser temperature)
- **Inkjet-specific problems:** clogged nozzles (print-head cleaning), ink smearing (wrong paper type), banding
- **Network printer issues:** discovering printer by IP, checking print queue on server, clearing stuck jobs via spooler

## Assessment stub

- Print defect diagnosis: given 6 sample printout images, identify the cause of each defect
- Spooler fix lab: simulate a stuck print job and clear it via the Services console and print queue
- Short answer: a laser printer's output smears when touched immediately after printing — which component is failing?
