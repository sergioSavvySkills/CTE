## Learning objective

Students will be able to use advanced spreadsheet features including charts, pivot tables, conditional formatting, and data validation to analyze and present data.

## Content stub

- **Charts and graphs:** column, bar, line, pie charts; choosing the right chart type; chart titles, labels, and legends
- **Conditional formatting:** highlight cells based on rules (greater than, duplicates, top 10%); data bars; color scales
- **Pivot tables:** creating from a dataset; rows/columns/values/filters; summarizing data without formulas; refreshing
- **Data validation:** dropdown lists, number range restriction, date limits; input messages; error alerts
## Assessment stub

- Chart exercise: given a monthly sales dataset, create a line chart with proper labels and a trend line
- Pivot table lab: build a pivot table that summarizes sales by region and product category
- Short answer: what is the purpose of conditional formatting in a data-heavy spreadsheet?
