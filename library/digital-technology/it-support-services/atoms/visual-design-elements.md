## Learning objective

Students will be able to identify and apply the core visual design elements — line, shape, color, texture, space, and typography — in the context of web and digital design.

## Content stub

- **Line:** types (straight, curved, diagonal); implied lines; how lines guide eye movement
- **Shape:** geometric vs. organic; how shapes communicate mood; using shapes in UI components
- **Color:** hue, saturation, value; warm vs. cool; emotional associations; color in branding
- **Texture:** real vs. implied texture; using texture to add depth without clutter; background textures
- **Space:** positive vs. negative space; white space as a design tool; breathing room in layouts
- **Typography:** serif vs. sans-serif; display vs. body fonts; type hierarchy (H1 → body); line length (50-75 chars)

## Assessment stub

- Element identification: annotate a provided web design screenshot, labeling each visual element
- Color mood board: create a color palette for two contrasting brands (e.g., children's app vs. law firm) and explain your choices
- Short answer: why is white space considered a design element rather than wasted space?
