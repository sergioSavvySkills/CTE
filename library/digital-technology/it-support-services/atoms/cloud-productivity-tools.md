## Learning objective

Students will be able to identify major cloud productivity suites, describe how they differ from desktop software, and configure basic collaboration features.

## Content stub

- **Cloud vs. desktop apps:** always updated, accessible anywhere, subscription pricing, internet dependency
- **Microsoft 365:** Word/Excel/PowerPoint online, OneDrive, Teams, SharePoint; licensing tiers
- **Google Workspace:** Docs/Sheets/Slides, Drive, Gmail, Meet, Classroom; free vs. paid tiers
- **Collaboration features:** real-time co-editing, comments, version history, sharing permissions
- **Sync and offline access:** desktop sync clients, offline mode in browser, conflict resolution
- **IT support considerations:** licensing management, SSO integration, data retention policies, BYOD access

## Assessment stub

- Side-by-side task: complete the same formatting task in Word desktop vs. Word Online; note differences
- Sharing lab: share a Google Doc with specific permissions and verify a classmate's access level
- Short answer: what is one business risk of storing company documents in a personal cloud account?
