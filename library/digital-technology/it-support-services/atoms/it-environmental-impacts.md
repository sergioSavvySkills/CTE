## Learning objective

Students will be able to explain the environmental impact of IT equipment, describe responsible e-waste disposal, and identify energy-efficiency practices.

## Content stub

- **E-waste hazards:** lead, mercury, cadmium, beryllium in electronics; leaching into soil/water; why landfill disposal is harmful
- **Responsible disposal:** R2 and e-Stewards certified recyclers; manufacturer take-back programs; donation of working equipment
- **Data sanitization before disposal:** must sanitize before recycling or donating; reference to data-destruction-and-disposal atom
- **Energy efficiency:** 80 PLUS power supplies; Energy Star certification; server power management; virtualization reducing physical hardware
- **Data center environmental controls:** PUE metric; cooling efficiency; hot-aisle/cold-aisle containment; renewable energy use
- **Green IT initiatives:** thin clients, device refresh cycles, paper reduction, remote work reducing commute/office energy

## Assessment stub

- E-waste research: find your local certified e-waste recycler and document their accepted items and process
- Energy calculation: given a list of 5 computers, calculate annual electricity cost at current kWh rates
- Short answer: what hazardous material in older CRT monitors makes them especially dangerous to landfill?
