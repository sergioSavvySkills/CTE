## Learning objective

Students will be able to identify mobile security risks, configure basic protections, and explain how MDM enforces policy on mobile devices.

## Content stub

- **Mobile threat landscape:** lost/stolen devices, malicious apps, unsecured Wi-Fi, SMS phishing, outdated OS
- **Screen lock and encryption:** PIN/biometric requirements; iOS and Android full-disk encryption by default; when to enable
- **MDM and MAM:** Mobile Device Management enforces policies (lock screen, encryption, remote wipe); MAM manages only apps
- **BYOD considerations:** personal vs. corporate data separation, containerization (Android Work Profile, iOS Managed Apps)
- **App security:** only install from official stores; review app permissions; sideloading risks
- **Remote wipe and locator:** Find My (Apple), Find My Device (Google); selective wipe vs. full wipe; when to use each

## Assessment stub

- MDM policy design: given a company BYOD scenario, list 6 policy settings you would enforce via MDM
- App permission audit: on a device or simulator, review the permissions of 3 installed apps and flag any excessive requests
- Short answer: what is the difference between a selective wipe and a full wipe in MDM?
