## Learning objective

Students will be able to describe foundational AI and machine learning concepts, identify how AI is used in IT support tools, and recognize the limitations of AI-assisted support.

## Content stub

- **AI and ML basics:** supervised vs. unsupervised learning; training data; models; AI is pattern recognition, not understanding
- **AI in IT support tools:** AI-assisted ticketing (auto-categorization, suggested resolutions), chatbots, predictive alerting
- **AI in security:** anomaly detection, user behavior analytics (UBA/UEBA), AI-powered antivirus (EDR)
- **Generative AI in IT:** using LLMs for script writing, documentation, knowledge base drafting; prompt engineering basics
- **Limitations and risks:** hallucinations, bias in training data, over-reliance, privacy concerns with sending data to AI
- **Responsible AI use:** verifying AI-generated scripts before running, not sending PII to public AI tools, human oversight

## Assessment stub

- AI tool survey: use an AI assistant to generate a PowerShell script, then audit it for correctness and safety
- Limitation analysis: given 3 AI-generated IT recommendations, identify any errors or risks
- Short answer: why should you never paste customer PII into a public AI chat tool?
