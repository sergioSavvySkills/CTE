## Learning objective

Students will be able to identify IT support career opportunities, describe the duties, education, and certifications required for each, and articulate the role of resumes and portfolios in securing an IT position.

## Content stub

- **IT support roles:** help desk technician, desktop support, field technician, systems administrator, network technician, IT operations
- **Duties and tasks:** day-in-the-life for each role — ticket volume, customer contact, hardware vs. software focus
- **Education pathways:** high-school CTE, community college, four-year degrees, bootcamps, self-study
- **Certifications and their role:** CompTIA (ITF+, A+, Network+, Security+), Google IT Support, vendor certifications, how IBC funding works in Texas
- **Resume essentials for IT:** technical skills section, projects, certifications, soft skills
- **Portfolios:** lab write-ups, GitHub for scripts, documentation samples, home-lab screenshots
- **Self-assessment:** aligning personal interests (hardware tinkering vs. customer service vs. scripting) to roles

## Assessment stub

- Career research project: student selects two IT roles, documents required education, skills, average salary, and 2–3 local/regional job postings
- Draft a one-page IT resume appropriate for an entry-level help-desk position
- Portfolio plan: list five artifacts the student will collect over the course and explain how each demonstrates employability
