## Learning objective

Students will be able to describe how human behavior affects organizational security, explain acceptable-use policies, and identify privacy best practices.

## Content stub

- **Human factor in security:** insider threats, negligence, social engineering susceptibility; why people are the weakest link
- **Acceptable-use policies:** what employees may and may not do with company devices/networks; consequences of violations
- **Password hygiene:** reuse dangers, password managers, not sharing credentials, changing defaults
- **Privacy policies:** what data organizations collect, how it is used, regulatory requirements (FERPA, HIPAA, GDPR overview)
- **Clean-desk and clear-screen:** physical documents, unlocked workstations, shoulder surfing in public
- **Security awareness training:** why recurring training reduces incidents; phishing simulations; reporting suspicious activity

## Assessment stub

- AUP analysis: read a sample acceptable-use policy and identify three things an employee must not do
- Scenario judgment: given 5 employee behaviors, classify each as a security violation or acceptable practice
- Short answer: why is a password manager safer than memorizing passwords or writing them down?
