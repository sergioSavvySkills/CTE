## Learning objective

Students will be able to explain why databases are used, describe the role of a DBMS, and identify common database use cases in business and IT contexts.

## Content stub

- **Why databases?:** structured, persistent, multi-user data storage; advantages over flat files and spreadsheets
- **DBMS role:** software layer between data and users; enforces rules, handles concurrency, manages storage
- **Common DBMS products:** MySQL, PostgreSQL, Microsoft SQL Server, Oracle, SQLite; open-source vs. commercial
- **Database in IT support:** ticketing systems, Active Directory, asset management, inventory — all use databases behind the scenes
- **Data vs. information:** raw data transformed into meaningful information via queries and reports
- **ACID properties:** Atomicity, Consistency, Isolation, Durability — why transactions must be reliable

## Assessment stub

- Use-case identification: for 6 business scenarios, explain why a database is better than a spreadsheet
- Short answer: what does it mean for a database to be ACID-compliant and why does it matter?
- Discussion: name two databases an IT support technician indirectly uses every day
