## Learning objective

Students will be able to identify symptoms of mobile security compromises and apply remediation steps.

## Content stub

- **Signs of compromise:** unexpected data usage, unfamiliar apps, overheating, pop-up ads, account activity alerts
- **Unauthorized account access:** checking login history, revoking third-party app access, changing passwords, enabling MFA
- **Malicious apps:** identifying apps with excessive permissions or poor reputation; removal steps
- **MDM compliance issues:** device out of compliance alerts, re-enrolling in MDM, selective wipe trigger
- **Lost or stolen device response:** remote lock, remote locate, remote wipe decision; filing a police report; notifying IT
- **Avoiding reinfection:** reviewing installed apps post-clean, changing all credentials, enabling MFA

## Assessment stub

- Scenario response: given a 'device stolen' scenario, list the immediate steps in order
- App audit: review a list of 5 apps with their permission sets and flag any that appear malicious
- Short answer: what should a user do immediately if they notice unfamiliar purchases on an account linked to their phone?
