## Learning objective

Students will be able to design, structure, and deliver an effective slide presentation that communicates a clear message to a target audience.

## Content stub

- **Presentation structure:** opening hook, agenda, body (3-5 key points), conclusion, call to action
- **Slide design principles:** 6×6 rule (max 6 bullets, 6 words each); one idea per slide; high-contrast text; readable fonts
- **Visual storytelling:** using images over text; charts to replace tables; white space; visual hierarchy
- **Speaker notes:** writing notes for presenter use; difference between what the slide shows and what you say
- **Accessibility:** alt text on images, sufficient color contrast, readable font size (min 24pt body), captions
- **Delivery preparation:** rehearsing with slides, handling questions, exporting handouts, sharing via link vs. file

## Assessment stub

- Full presentation project: create and deliver a 7-10 slide presentation on an IT career topic to the class
- Peer critique: evaluate a classmate's presentation using a design rubric (clarity, visuals, structure, delivery)
- Short answer: what does the '6×6 rule' mean and why does it improve presentations?
