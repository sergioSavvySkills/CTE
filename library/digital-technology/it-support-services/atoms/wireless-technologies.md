## Learning objective

Students will be able to compare 802.11 Wi-Fi standards by speed, frequency, and range, and identify Bluetooth and cellular wireless technologies.

## Content stub

- **Wi-Fi standards:** 802.11a/b/g/n/ac/ax (Wi-Fi 6)/be (Wi-Fi 7); frequency bands, max theoretical speeds, range
- **Wi-Fi 6 and 6E:** OFDMA, MU-MIMO, BSS coloring; 6 GHz band for Wi-Fi 6E; reduced congestion
- **Bluetooth:** versions 4.0 through 5.3; Classic vs. BLE; range and throughput; pairing process
- **NFC:** near-field communication; 10 cm range; used for payments, access badges, device pairing
- **Cellular in IT:** LTE/5G as a backup WAN or primary for IoT; hotspot use cases; carrier aggregation
- **Frequency trade-offs:** 2.4 GHz: longer range, more interference; 5/6 GHz: faster, shorter range; choosing the right band for the scenario

## Assessment stub

- Standards chart: complete a table comparing 802.11n, ac, and ax on frequency, speed, and range
- Short answer: why would a technician enable 5 GHz only on an access point in a dense apartment building?
- Hands-on: connect a device to both 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz and compare speed-test results
