## Learning objective

Students will be able to identify major categories of business software, describe common applications in each category, and match software to a business use case.

## Content stub

- **Productivity suites:** word processing, spreadsheets, presentations, email clients — Microsoft 365 and Google Workspace
- **Communication tools:** email (Outlook, Gmail), chat (Teams, Slack), video conferencing (Zoom, Meet), VoIP
- **Collaboration and project management:** SharePoint, Confluence, Asana, Jira, Trello — purpose and typical users
- **Industry-specific software:** ERP (SAP), CRM (Salesforce), accounting (QuickBooks), HRIS, healthcare EMR
- **Creative and multimedia:** Adobe Creative Suite, video editors, CAD tools — heavy hardware requirements
- **Security and management software:** antivirus, backup software, patch management, MDM, RMM tools used by IT staff

## Assessment stub

- Software sort: given a list of 15 applications, categorize each by type
- Scenario: a small business needs email, file storage, and video meetings — recommend a suite and justify
- Short answer: what is the difference between CRM and ERP software?
