## Learning objective

Students will be able to identify common cable types and connectors used in IT, select the correct cable for a given task, and explain the data-transfer limitations of each.

## Content stub

- **USB standards:** USB 2.0/3.0/3.1/3.2/4; connector types (A, B, mini, micro, C); transfer speeds; power delivery
- **Video cables:** HDMI, DisplayPort, DVI, VGA; max resolution and refresh rate per standard; active vs. passive adapters
- **Audio cables:** 3.5 mm TRS/TRRS, RCA, XLR, optical TOSLINK; balanced vs. unbalanced
- **Network cables:** Cat 5e/6/6a/8; RJ-45 connector; straight-through vs. crossover; bandwidth and distance limits
- **Storage cables:** SATA data/power, SAS, PCIe/M.2 key types (M, B, B+M)
- **Fiber-optic:** single-mode vs. multimode; LC/SC/ST connectors; used for long runs and high-speed backbone

## Assessment stub

- Cable identification lab: identify 10 physical cables/connectors by type and standard
- Scenario: user wants to connect a 4K 144 Hz monitor — which cable should they use and why?
- Short answer: what is the maximum length of a standard Cat 6 ethernet run before signal degrades?
