## Learning objective

Students will be able to identify display technologies, describe key specifications, and select an appropriate display for a given use case.

## Content stub

- **Display technologies:** TN, IPS, VA, OLED — viewing angles, response time, contrast, price trade-offs
- **Resolution and pixel density:** 1080p/1440p/4K; PPI; why resolution matters more on smaller screens
- **Refresh rate:** 60 Hz vs 144 Hz vs 240 Hz; importance for gaming and smooth motion; VRR (G-Sync/FreeSync)
- **Connectors:** HDMI (1.4 vs 2.0 vs 2.1), DisplayPort, USB-C/Thunderbolt, VGA (legacy)
- **Backlighting:** CCFL (legacy) vs LED backlight; local dimming zones; brightness (nits)
- **Touch and special displays:** capacitive touch, stylus support, privacy screens, curved vs. flat

## Assessment stub

- Spec sheet exercise: given three monitor spec sheets, recommend one for a graphic designer and one for a call-center agent
- Short answer: why would a video editor prefer IPS over TN?
- Connection troubleshoot: a user plugs an HDMI cable into a DisplayPort port — what adapter is needed?
