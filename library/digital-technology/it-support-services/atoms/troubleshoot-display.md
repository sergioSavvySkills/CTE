## Learning objective

Students will be able to diagnose and resolve common display problems including no signal, artifacts, dead pixels, and backlight failures.

## Content stub

- **No signal:** check cable seating, correct input source selected, test with different cable and monitor
- **Flickering and refresh rate:** driver issue vs. cable bandwidth limitation vs. wrong refresh rate in settings
- **Artifacts and corruption:** GPU overheating (check fan/thermal paste), VRAM failure, driver corruption
- **Dead and stuck pixels:** dead (always black) vs. stuck (wrong color); pixel-fixing tools; manufacturer warranty thresholds
- **Backlight failure:** dim image visible with flashlight = backlight dead not GPU; inverter vs. LED strip failure
- **Laptop display issues:** loose hinge damaging cable, digitizer vs. display failure, external monitor as diagnostic tool

## Assessment stub

- Symptom diagnosis: given 6 display complaints, identify the most likely cause for each
- Lab: use display test patterns to identify artifacts and document findings
- Short answer: a user sees a faint image on their laptop screen — what component has likely failed?
