## Learning objective

Students will be able to identify common social engineering attack types, explain why they succeed, and apply countermeasures to protect against them.

## Content stub

- **Phishing variants:** phishing (bulk email), spear phishing (targeted), vishing (voice), smishing (SMS), whaling (executives)
- **Pretexting:** fabricating a scenario to gain trust; impersonating IT, vendors, or management
- **Baiting and quid pro quo:** dropping infected USB drives; offering fake tech support in exchange for access
- **Tailgating and piggybacking:** following an authorized person through a secured door; why physical security training matters
- **Why social engineering works:** authority, urgency, scarcity, social proof, familiarity — psychological triggers
- **Defenses:** verification procedures, security awareness training, callback confirmation, reporting culture

## Assessment stub

- Attack identification: read 6 scenario descriptions and name the social engineering technique used
- Phishing analysis: examine a sample phishing email and list 5 red flags
- Short answer: why is security awareness training the primary defense against social engineering?
