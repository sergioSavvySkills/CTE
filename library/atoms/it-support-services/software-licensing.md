## Learning objective

Students will be able to identify software license types, explain the rights and restrictions of each, and select the appropriate license for a given organizational need.

## Content stub

- **Proprietary licenses:** vendor retains ownership; EULA restrictions; per-seat, per-device, subscription models
- **Open-source licenses:** GPL (copyleft), MIT/BSD (permissive), Apache; what each allows users to do and redistribute
- **Freeware and shareware:** free to use but closed-source; shareware trial limitations; donationware
- **Commercial license models:** perpetual vs. subscription; OEM vs. retail; volume licensing (Microsoft EA, VLA)
- **License compliance:** audit risk; true-up; software asset management (SAM); consequences of non-compliance
- **Creative Commons:** CC licenses for content (not software); BY, SA, ND, NC variants

## Assessment stub

- License sort: classify 10 software products by license type using their official license text
- Scenario: a school wants to deploy an app on 500 machines — which license model is most cost-effective?
- Short answer: what is the key difference between GPL and MIT open-source licenses?
