## Learning objective

Students will be able to identify mobile device hardware features, connection types, and accessories, and configure basic mobile device settings.

## Content stub

- **Mobile hardware:** SoC (system-on-chip), LPDDR RAM, UFS storage, battery types, display tech (LCD vs OLED)
- **Cellular connectivity:** GSM vs CDMA legacy; LTE bands; 5G sub-6 vs mmWave; SIM vs eSIM
- **Short-range wireless:** Wi-Fi 6/6E, Bluetooth 5, NFC (tap-to-pay, pairing), IR blaster
- **Mobile accessories:** cases, screen protectors, docking stations, stylus, MagSafe/wireless charging
- **Synchronization:** iCloud/Google backup; device-to-PC sync; MDM enrollment
- **Ports and expansion:** USB-C, Lightning (legacy), MicroSD slots; what accessories require adapters

## Assessment stub

- Spec comparison: compare iPhone and Android flagship side-by-side on RAM, storage, connectivity
- Configuration lab: on a practice device or simulator, enable hotspot, pair Bluetooth speaker, and back up to cloud
- Short answer: what is a SIM card and what happens if you swap it between carriers?
