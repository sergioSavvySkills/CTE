## Learning objective

Students will be able to configure browser privacy and security settings, manage extensions, and identify common browser-based threats.

## Content stub

- **Major browsers:** Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari — market share, rendering engines, sync features
- **Privacy settings:** cookies (first-party vs. third-party), do not track, private/incognito mode limitations
- **Security settings:** HTTPS-only mode, certificate warnings, safe browsing, pop-up blocker, download scanning
- **Extensions and plugins:** legitimate productivity extensions vs. malicious browser hijackers; reviewing permissions
- **Browser data management:** clearing cache, history, cookies, saved passwords; impact on performance and privacy
- **Enterprise browser management:** group policy to lock settings, managed extensions, homepage enforcement

## Assessment stub

- Configuration lab: lock down a browser per a provided security checklist (block pop-ups, HTTPS-only, disable autofill)
- Extension audit: review the permissions requested by three extensions and classify each as safe/risky
- Short answer: why does clearing the cache sometimes fix a broken website?
