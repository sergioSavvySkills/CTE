## Learning objective

Students will be able to identify PSU ratings, connectors, and efficiency standards, and determine whether a PSU is adequate for a given system build.

## Content stub

- **AC to DC conversion:** how a PSU converts 120/240V AC to 3.3V/5V/12V DC rails; multi-rail vs. single-rail
- **Wattage and rails:** calculating system power draw; 80+ efficiency ratings (Bronze/Gold/Platinum); headroom rule of thumb
- **PSU connectors:** 24-pin ATX, 4/8-pin CPU, 6/8-pin PCIe GPU, SATA power, Molex legacy
- **Form factors:** ATX, SFX, TFX; matching PSU form factor to case
- **Modular vs. non-modular:** cable management trade-offs; semi-modular as a middle ground
- **PSU failure symptoms:** random shutdowns, burning smell, no POST, voltage instability; using a PSU tester

## Assessment stub

- Wattage calculation: given a parts list, use an online calculator to find minimum PSU wattage
- Connector ID: identify 5 PSU connectors from photos
- Short answer: a PC randomly shuts off under heavy load — why is the PSU the first suspect?
