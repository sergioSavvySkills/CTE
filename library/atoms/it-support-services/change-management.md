## Learning objective

Students will be able to describe the change management process, classify change types, and explain why formal change control reduces IT risk.

## Content stub

- **Why change management:** uncontrolled changes cause most outages; change process reduces risk and improves predictability
- **Change types:** standard (pre-approved, low risk), normal (requires CAB review), emergency (expedited for critical fixes)
- **Change request components:** description, risk assessment, rollback plan, testing evidence, implementation window
- **Change Advisory Board:** who reviews changes; CAB meeting frequency; approval criteria
- **Change window:** why changes happen in maintenance windows; communication to stakeholders
- **Post-change review:** verifying success, documenting lessons learned, closing the change record

## Assessment stub

- Change classification: given 8 IT changes, classify each as standard/normal/emergency and explain
- Change request draft: write a change request for upgrading a server's OS, including risk and rollback
- Short answer: what is a rollback plan and why is it required in every change request?
