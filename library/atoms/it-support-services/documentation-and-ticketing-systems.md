## Learning objective

Students will be able to create accurate ticket documentation, navigate a ticketing system, and explain why proper documentation is essential in IT support.

## Content stub

- **Why documentation matters:** knowledge transfer, accountability, SLA measurement, compliance, training new staff
- **Ticketing system components:** ticket fields (priority, category, requester, assignee, status), SLA timers, escalation paths
- **Writing good ticket notes:** what the user reported, what was observed, steps taken, outcome; avoiding jargon
- **Knowledge base articles:** converting resolved tickets into reusable KB articles; structure and search tags
- **Asset and inventory documentation:** CMDB concepts; tracking hardware, software, and relationships
- **Change log and runbooks:** documenting recurring procedures as runbooks; change records in ITSM tools

## Assessment stub

- Ticket writing lab: given a scenario description, write a complete ticket from open through close
- KB article draft: convert a completed troubleshooting ticket into a reusable KB article
- Short answer: why is it important to document steps that did NOT fix the problem?
