## Learning objective

Students will be able to locate and configure common Windows settings including display, power, time/region, accessibility, and privacy settings.

## Content stub

- **Display settings:** resolution, scale, orientation, multi-monitor arrangement, night light, refresh rate
- **Power plans:** Balanced, High Performance, Power Saver; sleep vs. hibernate; lid-close behavior on laptops
- **Time and region:** time zone, date format, language packs, keyboard layout; NTP sync (w32tm)
- **Accessibility options:** Magnifier, Narrator, high contrast, sticky keys, on-screen keyboard
- **Privacy and telemetry:** diagnostic data levels, app permissions (camera/mic/location), activity history
- **User account settings:** local vs. Microsoft account; PIN setup; account type (Standard vs. Administrator)

## Assessment stub

- Settings walkthrough: configure a provided checklist of settings on a lab PC (display scale, power plan, region)
- Scenario: a user's laptop is sleeping too aggressively during presentations — identify and fix the setting
- Short answer: what is the difference between Sleep and Hibernate power states?
