## Learning objective

Students will be able to distinguish between different categories of computing devices — desktop, laptop, server, thin client, IoT — and identify the appropriate use case for each.

## Content stub

- **Desktop vs. laptop:** trade-offs: upgradeability, portability, battery, price per performance
- **Workstations:** high-end desktops for CAD/video/science; ECC RAM, multi-core CPUs, pro GPUs
- **Servers:** rack-mounted and tower; redundant power, RAID, remote management (IPMI/iDRAC); always-on
- **Thin clients:** minimal local processing; depends on server or cloud; used in VDI environments
- **Mobile devices:** smartphones, tablets — ARM processors, battery-first design, mobile OS
- **IoT devices:** embedded systems, sensors, smart devices; limited compute; network-connected; security risk surface

## Assessment stub

- Scenario matching: given 5 workplace scenarios, select the correct device category and explain why
- Short answer: why would a company deploy thin clients instead of full desktops?
- Discussion: name two IoT devices in your home and describe the security risk each poses
