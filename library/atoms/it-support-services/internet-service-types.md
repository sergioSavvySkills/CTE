## Learning objective

Students will be able to compare internet connection types by technology, speed, and use case, and recommend an appropriate connection for a given scenario.

## Content stub

- **DSL:** Digital Subscriber Line; uses phone lines; asymmetric upload/download; typical speeds
- **Cable:** coaxial cable shared with TV service; DOCSIS standard; faster than DSL; shared bandwidth
- **Fiber:** fastest consumer option; symmetric speeds; GPON/EPON; limited availability
- **Cellular/mobile:** 4G LTE and 5G; hotspot devices; latency and data caps; good for mobility
- **Satellite:** Starlink and legacy geostationary; high latency; last-resort rural option
- **Fixed wireless and WiMAX:** line-of-sight radio towers; used in rural/suburban gaps

## Assessment stub

- Scenario recommendation: given three household scenarios, recommend the best ISP technology and justify
- Speed-test lab: run a speed test and record upload, download, and latency; compare to advertised speeds
- Short answer: why does a fiber connection generally have lower latency than satellite?
