## Learning objective

Students will be able to apply structured critical-thinking techniques to solve IT-support problems that have incomplete information or multiple possible causes.

## Content stub

- **Defining the problem:** distinguishing symptoms from root causes, narrowing vague user reports into testable hypotheses
- **Gathering evidence:** what information is available, what is missing, what can be verified vs. assumed
- **Evaluating hypotheses:** forming a theory of probable cause, prioritizing by likelihood and impact
- **Testing and iterating:** trying the least-destructive solution first, reverting changes that don't work
- **Documenting reasoning:** writing down what was tried and why so others can follow
- **Bias awareness:** avoiding premature conclusions, recognizing when you're anchored on a familiar answer

## Assessment stub

- Case study: given an ambiguous ticket ("my computer is slow"), students list the questions they would ask, two hypotheses, and how they would test each
- Comparison exercise: two solutions to the same problem — identify which is better and defend the choice
- Reflection: describe a past problem the student solved and map it to the critical-thinking steps
