## Learning objective

Students will be able to connect, configure, and troubleshoot common peripheral devices including input and output peripherals.

## Content stub

- **Input peripherals:** keyboard, mouse, touchpad, webcam, microphone, scanner — connection types
- **Output peripherals:** monitors, speakers, projectors — signal types (HDMI, DisplayPort, VGA)
- **Connection interfaces:** USB-A/B/C, Bluetooth, Wi-Fi — plug-and-play vs. manual driver install
- **Driver management:** what a driver is, how to update or roll back, Device Manager basics
- **Accessibility peripherals:** adaptive keyboards, switch access, screen readers — why they matter
- **Multi-monitor setup:** extending vs. mirroring the desktop; resolution and refresh rate

## Assessment stub

- Lab: connect keyboard, mouse, and external monitor to a computer; verify all function
- Troubleshoot scenario: printer appears in Device Manager with a yellow exclamation — walk through the fix
- Short answer: what is a driver and why is it required?
